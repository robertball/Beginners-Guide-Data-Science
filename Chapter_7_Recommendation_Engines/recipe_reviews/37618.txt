I really don't know whether to give it 4 or 5 stars since I'm only using this recipe as a guide. I'm gluten intolerant & was looking for a good basic cracker recipe to adapt for my needs. It's hard to find basic cracker recipes & this seems to fit well. I've made crackers years ago & they're basically all similar. It's up to the individual cook to personalize the recipe the way they wish to.

But I wanted to add a couple of suggestions for any die-hard home-made cracker fans. It's important to prick the dough all over to help it rise & crisp nicely. If you're going to be making a lot of crackers, it's time consuming. My trick is to buy an inexpensive hairbrush - a round barrel with thick nylon bristles. You can roll it over your dough & prick it evenly quickly. A handheld nylon shampoo massage brush works well too but you can't roll it. Save this just for baking - you'll probably find other uses for it too. :-D

And I saw a trick that a Japanese master chef used when he made noodles that comes in handy. Get two long pieces of wood, square-diameter, that's the thickness that you wish to roll out your dough. Good to have a couple since diff doughs get rolled out in diff thickness. Lay them on either side of the dough you're going to roll out & when your rolling pin makes contact with the wood, it's the thickness you want. I noticed the crackers I sprayed with water before salting crisped better, and that letting the crackers crisp above the vent made them a good texture. Not impossible, but difficult to get consistent results.
