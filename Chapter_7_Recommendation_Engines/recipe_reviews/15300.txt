This has quickly become a family favorite! I just made them for a second time yesterday, and these are the changes I made that helped them turn out perfect! 1) Upped the oven eat to 350F. 2) Reduced butter to 3/4 cup. 3) 1/4 cup brown sugar, 1/4 cup white sugar. 4) Reduced to 1/2 cup chocolate chips. 5) Added 1 tsp vanilla 5) Baked for 20 min, cooled for 15 min. Remember to flatten the cookies before baking them. When they first come out of the oven, they taste cakey and bland. So wait for them to harden! Believe me, they taste a whole world better. So buttery and crisp and salty/sweet -- delicious with milk or coffee! And not too sweet like regular soft chocolate chip cookies. Remember to flatten them before they go in the oven -- they only expand a little. They are big, neat, scone-looking cookies. This yielded exactly 12 medium-sized cookies for me.

I made these cookies without the walnuts and think they are good, however, I think they could be promoted to a great rating with a couple simple changes.  
First, although I am not a salt fiend, I do like salt and find it essential on certain foods like: steamed veggies, popcorn, celery, and enjoy the addition of sea-salt on many items now sold today like chocolate.  If you are like me, and struggle to thoroughly enjoy something that is under-salted, then you will want to use salted butter in this recipe.
Second, I chose this recipe because I am a big fan of both shortbread AND chocolate.  I followed the recipe and only used the 3/4 cup choc-chips and found it a bit on the skimpy side. I would recommend 1 cup of choc-chips and then fine-tuning quantity according to personal preference.

Mine all came out a tad large, which is exactly how I prefer my chocolate-chip cookies to be, but these babies are so rich do to high butter content, that I think a smaller size would suit them better.
