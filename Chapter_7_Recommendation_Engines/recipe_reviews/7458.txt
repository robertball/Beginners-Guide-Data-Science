This was a great recipe basis. However, I like chocolate frosting to be SUPER chocolatey and not very sugary at all. So I used much less sugar (to taste) and melted two oz. unsweetened chocolate with a handful of semisweet chips and a tab of butter. I then added this to the frosting, making it dreamily rich and intense. To bring out the chocolate flavor even more, I stirred some instant coffee with a bit of water (one could also mix it with the extract) and added to taste. The result: sinfully rich and uber chocolatey frosting that holds its shape and is perfect on Chocolate-Zucchini Cupcakes (also on this site). It couldn't get any better.

Ok recipe as written, but extremely sweet. To cut sweetness, I did a few things differently. Gently brown the butter over low heat, add a heaping Tbsp flour. and 1 Tbsp good quality cocoa powder (NOT hot cocoa powder), and then add the chocolate chips. Heat thru and stir until all chips are melted. Next, instead of waiting until mixture cools, just let it sit for a few minutes, stirring occasionally, then add the powdered sugar before everything else. Stir to combine well (adding to still warm butter/chocolate mixture helps to soften the sugar crystals so that frosting is less "grainy" in texture), then add the vanilla and salt. Add the sour cream last, a little at a time, until you have desired tartness to sweetness.

I've made this frosting twice. The first time, I made it exactly as directed, and it turned out perfectly. The second time, I decided to melt a square of unsweetened chocolate with the chocolate chips to kick up the chocolate a notch, and it was even better.  I also made 1 1/2 times the recipe so I could dirty-ice the cakes first.

Some reviewers complained the icing was too thick. I didn't have this issue, nor did I have trouble beating in all the confectioners sugar. This frosting does get thicker the longer it stands. I dirty-iced the cake when the frosting was thinner and then finished it with the thickened frosting. Also, don't chill the butter-chocolate mixture; it only needs to be a room temp before adding the sour cream. Finally, don't use an electric mixer: it incorporates air and cools the frosting too fast. 

I admit, I don't understand how anyone thinks a frosting is "too sweet." It's largely sugar and butter, and frosting by definition is SWEET. If you prefer desserts that aren't very sweet, serve fruit and cheese, or maybe a plain pound cake.  

This is an easy recipe for a thick, rich, chocolatey frosting with a subtle tang. It's my new go-to recipe for frosting. Superb!
