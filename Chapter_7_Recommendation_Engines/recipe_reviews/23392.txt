text:
when making this icing you don't just dump everything into the pot. You melt the butter first over medium low heat then add the cocoa. Then the milk, vanilla, and sugar. Turn the heat up to medium and stir constantly. When it begins to bubble around the edges turn the heat off and keep stirring for 1 minute. Then beat the icing until it just starts to stiffen. Use the icing immediately as it will harden. If it hardens before you are done icing put a splash of milk in and beat it again, just until its soft.

I used this to dip pretzels in. It turned out kind of grainy but it tasted good with pretzels so I don't really mind. My notes are that :
1. When the recipe says to stir "constantly", it means literally be stirring it at all times. Or it will burn, and catch fire.
2. When you add the milk to the mixture, it won't mix. Just let go and add the MELTED butter, then it will mix and it will be about the consistency of brownie batter. Don't be worried if the milk won't mix in with the cocoa and sugar. You just have to add the butter.
3. Take it off of the stove when it starts bubbling. You might not be able to see if you are stirring, so if you think it is ready stop stirring and see if bubbles pop up.
4. Instead of using an electric mixer, I used a whisker and beat it for three minutes. Then I waited a little bit for it to cool before dipping the pretzels. 

If you want it on a cake, you have to pour it QUICK, without letting it cool after beating it. Otherwise it will become UNSPREADABLE. If the cake is not completely cooled, it might actually help. 

I put it in the fridge for a few hours, and now I'm eating it like fudge with pretzels. 

So, TLDR, it was good but kind of grainy so don't use this on like a wedding cake or something really important. Bye!

This is the first time I have made this recipe, and I love it. The generous tips shared by other users were essential to the recipe's success, including:
-- The cautions to pour (not spread) the frosting over the cake before it hardens from a liquid to a solid as it cools. After removing it from the stove, I beat it with a wooden spoon till the resistance told me it was about to turn fudgey, and
-- The advice to make 150-200% of the recipe for a layer cake. I would make a double batch for two layers. The recipe, as is, covered the top and sides of one 9-inch round layer.
