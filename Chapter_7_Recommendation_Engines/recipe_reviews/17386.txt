I first made this recipe about 10 years ago for my kids for Christmas, it was my first year being divorced. I hadn't made it again until 2 years ago, I had a decent sized fancy sit down Christmas party for friends. I made this again the same way as the first time. I actually stuff the pork then baked it (takes quite a bit longer but so worth it). I had hot, herbed chicken stock on the stove and I basted the pork and the stuffing throughout to keep it moist. All I can say, is this is one of the most delicious special dinners I've ever made and probably the best I've ever had, anywhere. This beats beef tenderloin any day. It's so flavorful. I took the additional stuffing (and there was a lot) and mounded it into muffin cups (I basked for about 1/2 hour). My kids and my guests LOVE it like that. The best stuffing comes from inside the pork, all the cooking juice flavors it, but the muffins are a a great runner up. I made this with honey ginger carrots and a lemon zest/roasted garlic green beans.  Both are beautiful sides.

I'm making this again this year for a Christmas dinner for 8, I ordered a 14 rib roast.  My husband can't wait!
