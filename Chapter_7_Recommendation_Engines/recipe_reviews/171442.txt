Only changed a couple of minor things ingredient wise.  First we didn't chop the spinach, just put it on top and let it wilt. That's as much a matter of preference and convenience as anything, but there's really no need to chop spinach that you're going to cook.  Second we had a block of pepper jack on hand that was exactly the right amount so we substituted that for the mozzarella. And finally, for any recipe calling for diced or cubed chicken breast, I always use the chicken "tenders" rather than whole breasts. It comes from the same part of the bird, and costs much less.

As to the technique, I found that in the process of getting the pasta cooked, the skillet kept boiling dry and I had to add liquid incrementally to keep the process going.  I also covered the skillet after adding the shells to prevent losing too much liquid to evaporation until the pasta began to soften.

But it was completely worth the effort!  This is a definite keeper.  We used plain diced tomatoes and seasoned them liberally with basil, oregano, less liberally with thyme and black pepper, and added just a pinch of fennel seed and marjoram.  I think next time I might try it with fresh tomatoes and add a bay leaf to the mix.
