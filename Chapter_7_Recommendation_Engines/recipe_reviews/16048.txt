These brownies are amazing! Best I've ever eaten. EVER. and everyone RAVED at Christmas over them.

I read a lot of reviews and they came out PERFECT the first time. Some things to note: #1 - I made sure to use a METAL pan. #2 - I used the baking caramels from Kraft so that I didn't have to unwrap them. There were less than 15 oz of caramels, so I just used slightly less milk and it turned out great and I had plenty to cover the brownies. #3 - I used semi-sweet chocolate chips and I felt that milk chocolate would have been way too sweet. #4 - When layering the caramel and chips on the 1st layer of brownie mix, the brownies will drop since they're not completely done. Totally worth making. A++++++

This recipe does require a lot of patience, and you're not always going to get it right the first time. These turned out excellent.  Used aluminum pan,  as one reviewer said not to use glass.  Here are some helpful hints to make these turn out stellar:  (1) use the ingredients only listed in the recipe.  Do not make the german choc cake per the back side of the cake box,  (2) one reviewer talked about them being super grainy.  Use the evaporated milk (liquid) which comes in a can, not non-fat evaporated dry milk powder in a box, (3) to melt the caramels, put in a 1100 watt microwave for 1 min 35 seconds, stir, then another 1 min 35 seconds, stir well.  This should do it, (4) when you're putting the top layer of batter on top, keep a bowl of water nearby.  Either moisten your fingers to then flatten the batter super thin before placing over caramel/choc chip layer, or you can dip each spoonful of batter very quickly/briefly into the water before flattening out with your fingers.  Either way, the moisture makes it much easier to work w/ the batter, and the extra moisture doesn't at all affect the outcome of the baking.  

I also elected to put a fudge icing on top.   1 c sugar,  1/4 c milk, 1/4 c butter, 2 t vanilla.  In a pan, bring to a boil for 1 minute.  Remove from heat, beat icing w/ mixer 3 min or until thickens to desired consistency to spread over completely cooled brownies.

Makes a delectable 13 x 9 batch of brownies!  Two taste testers so far, two rave reviews.  This one takes some time to make (unwrap caramels, flatten the top layer), but well worth it.
