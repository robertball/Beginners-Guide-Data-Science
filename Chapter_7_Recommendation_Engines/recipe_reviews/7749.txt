If you love Oreo cookies (and who doesn't?), you'll love this cake. I've made this many times for more than 15 years, because it's always been a crowd pleaser. I make it as a layer cake, using 2 eggs rather than 3 egg whites--this makes for a more moist cake, and there's no waste of yolks.  I cover the sides of the cake completely with additional crushed Oreos and stand more cookies, cut in half, upright along the top edge. I make the frosting a little differently, mostly to ensure an ample amount for a layer cake: 4 c. powdered sugar, 1/2 c. shortening, 1/4 c. milk and 1 tsp. vanilla. This is a great go-to recipe when you need a cake that's beautiful, easy, and delicious!

I loved the frosting!  I made it with 1/4 cup pasteurized egg whites.  Because they're more yellow than regular egg whites, (more like a beaten egg), and because I also used butter-flavored shortening, my frosting was fairly off-white in color, but still tasted silky and very delicious!  Both the texture and taste were supreme.  Having said that, I didn't feel the cake part was very special.  I used a dark chocolate mix and couldn't discern any of the Oreos in it (I used 1-1/2 cups of coarsely chopped).  It basically tasted like a box cake, but I did think the dark chocolate flavor went well with that of the frosting.  The end product looked attractive with finely crushed cookie crumbs on it.  Overall, my husband and kids liked it and the frosting made it a little more special.  Not five stars to me, but worth trying.
