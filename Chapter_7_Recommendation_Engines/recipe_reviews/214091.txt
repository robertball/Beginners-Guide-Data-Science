A few tips for those new to flan and custurd.

Carmalized sugar is suppose to harden quickly as it cools, so you must pour it into the flan dish as soon as the desired color is reached. It needs to be light brown to amber in color, then immediately removed from the heat because it can quickly go from amber to burnt (30sec-1min). Don't even try to use the carmel if it looks black because it will be bitter and ruin your dessert. The caramelized sugar liquifies again once left for a long period of time in the fridge. If you find that there is "leftover" or wasted sugar in the flan dish, it is because you choose to eat the flan sooner rather then later. If you leave the flan in the fridge for 24-48 hours, all the caramelized sugar will liquify and there will be none wasted. To clean up caramelized sugar or black burnt sugar in your pans fill pan with water to cover all the burnt sugar and boil until sugar dissolves! 

If your flan has bubbles, hardened egg pieces, or fluffy in texture like scrambled eggs, I hate to tell you this but you OVERCOOKED your flan. It should be silky smooth like a creme brulee, but usually slightly firmer. The flan got too hot. Either it cooked too long in the oven or the bain Marie (water bath) was too shallow and the custard got too hot while baking. Custard must be cooked very slowly with even heat.
