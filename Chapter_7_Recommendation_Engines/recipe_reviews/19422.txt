This is a good base recipe but needs just a bit of tweeking.  1. Because I have a picky toddler I avoided the onions.  2. When making the sauce, add 1 cup of milk to your sauce pan and reserve 1/2 a cup cold milk to mix your dry ingredients in.  Mix the dry ingredients into the 1/2 cup cold milk and mix thoroughly--you will prevent lumps this way. Add the remaining 1/2 cup and stir.  I found it didn't thicken very well until I added the cheese.  I kept the cheese on the heat until the sauce thickened.  3. I recommend adding a second cheese.  I added romano but parmasean, monteray Jack or any other sharp cheese would  really enhance the flavour. 4.  I crushed up 2 crackers and put them on top, I sprayed a bit of olive oil onto them to allow for browning.  I also grated a bit of cheedar cheese onto the bread crumbs maybe 2-3 tbsp.

First, a couple of things for people having trouble: 1. If the sauce was grainy or your cheese didn't melt, you didn't make a proper roux. Don't blame the recipe for your lack of cooking skills! First, you melt the butter. Then, you sautee your onions (a very fine mince works best; you can pulse them in a processor of blender if you don't want chunks, or you can really take a shortcut and use dried, minced onions--that's what I did; it intensifies the flavor a lot. I used 2 Tbsp and heated it with the butter, until golden). Whisk in the flour. You MUST stir continually, or you'll have lumps. This is cooking 101. Don't be cheap about the time you spend here. You need a smooth base. If it's grainy, you're not finished cooking it. Then, when your flour has taken on a rich golden color, and is smooth(!), slowly add milk and keep stirring!!  It's done when the sauce thickens enough to cover the back of a metal spoon.  2. "Salt and Pepper, to taste" is somewhat intuitive. It takes experience and common sense. If your sauce is "bland," you didn't add enough seasoning. You can fix this by tasting it before removing it from the pot. You want it just a little on the side of "overpowering," because the noodles will absorb the flavor, and dilute the saltiness (also add salt to your noodles as they cook; it makes a huge difference).
