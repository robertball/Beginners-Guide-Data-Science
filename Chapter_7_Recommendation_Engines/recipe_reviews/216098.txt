My husband asked for a pot of beans and this recipe seemed to be closest to what he was asking for.  I have never cooked beans because I don't like the gas.  But I did some research on how to eliminate it and this recipe covered it.  The soaking for at least 8 hrs and rinsing well is important.  The other gas eliminating  property is the Bay Leaves.  My Bay leaves were very small, so I put 2.5 of them in (one was a broken leaf, lol). I have no idea where to get a Smoked Turkey Leg, but I had some Country Ham pieces I had bought for cooking with and used about a third of that package. I also added about 2T of Wyler's Chicken Granules at another reviewers suggestion. I was worried about adding it at the beginning because I had read salt will make the beans tough, but since I had to leave, I went on and added all my ingredients at the beginning except for the Olive Oil.  I only had 6 hrs to cook, so I started them on high for 1.5 hrs, then changed to low when I had to leave home.  The beans came out great, and not tough. If I had returned home earlier, I may have added the Olive Oil, but decided not too add at the last minute. They were done in time for me to take a meal to my 90 and 93 yr parents who were excited when I told them I would bring them beans for dinner. My 92 yr mother-in-law is getting some leftovers and she will love them too.  I served with cornbread. Best part is, NO GAS!!! So I will definitely make sure to not only soak well, but add the Bay Leaves.
