This recipe is easy but not quite the MOIST burek I love so much! I think moisture is also the key to working with phyllo dough so here are a few ideas. 
1) Don't drain the beef too much - or at all! The grease helps to make the dough sheets pliable. 2) When you are not using the dough sheets cover them directly with a damp kitchen rag so they don't crack when you roll them up. 3) If the dough still isn't moist enough to work with you'll have to keep lathering on the butter as you roll it. Also I threw an egg in the beef mixture, which seemed to help.  

A little more grease may not make it healthier but it definitely makes it more delicious and more authentic!

For those people who are having trouble 'snaking' the filled pastry - change the wash you're putting onto the philo dough to 2Tbsp melted butter, 2Tbsp water, 3Tbsp Greek yoghurt and 1 large egg. Whisk together.

Also you'll need more layers of dough. I used 2 as the base > brush with egg wash > layer of dough > brush with egg wash > layer of dough > brush with egg wash > fill with meat filling, roll. Total of 4 sheets of dough per roll gives it a good bit more structure.
