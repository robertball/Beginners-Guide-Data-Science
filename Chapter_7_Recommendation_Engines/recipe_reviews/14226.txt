A "not bad" adaptation of the classic.  I preferred to peel and slice the potatoes into 1/4" slices before cooking, preferred olive oil over vegetable oil, white wine vinegar over tarragon vinegar and chicken broth over the beef consomme.  I used all ingredients to taste - in amounts that looked right for the amount of potatoes I used.  I used russet potatoes which are NOT the best choice for a recipe like this, but it's what I had on hand.  A red potato, new potato or Yukon Gold is what's needed here and it would be good to specify that in the recipe.  Best served at room temperature.

I've been making a variation on this for over 30 years from a Julia Child recipe. It's a refreshing change from a heavy mayonnaise-based salad; the flavor of potato really comes through, and it stands up well on a picnic or potluck table. Red or Yukon Gold potatoes are a must. I've always used chicken broth (1/2 cup for 3 pounds potatoes), a good olive oil, cider vinegar (I cannot abide tarragon), and I prefer a mild white or sweet onion. I've sometimes added  things such as hard-cooked eggs, olives, dill pickle, pimiento, celery, or crumbled bacon: whatever suits your fancy! Best at room temperature.
