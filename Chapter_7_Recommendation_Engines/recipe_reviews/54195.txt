One of the best tuna salads I've ever had.  I opted to forgo the tomatoes and it was still terrific.  One note, when you are using tuna the pouch variety that is solid (not chunk, light) is the best quality and you will certainly taste the difference.  The solid tuna is from the loin section and is far better than what you would get with the rest.  The pouch is superior because it allows preservation at lower "canning" temperatures than traditional cans.  This results in better flavor and texture.  Alton Brown has a lot to say about the subject if you're ever curious.

I have eaten in dozens of New Jersey diners, and the tuna melts are not like this. The vinegar in the salad is unnecessary and too sour, and the cheese under the tomato remains stone cold. To make a real NJ diner tuna melt, mix 2 cans chunk light tuna in water, very well drained, with 1/4 cup mayo, 2 Tablespoons each of minced onion and celery, and salt and pepper to taste, spread it on rye toast, top with thinly sliced tomato, if desired, and top with Swiss or American cheese.
