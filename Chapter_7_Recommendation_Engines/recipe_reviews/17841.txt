I have made a similar recipe like this for years, but instead of smoked sausage, you MUST try this with Italian sausage (hot or mild).  I start this off by heating a little olive oil in a skillet and start cooking my potatoes.  After the potatoes have a good start, I add the hot sausage, cut into cubes as well and cook that.  This gives the potatoes a WONDERFUL flavor.  I then add sliced red and green peppers and sliced onions.  I put a lid on it and let it cook so that the potatoes get soft.  YUMMY!  This is great on a buffet for a party.  I haven't made this for a while, but writing this review has made me hungry for it, and now I know what tomorrow's dinner will be!

I've been making this recipe for years now, both as written and with various modifications. Its always our first dinner meal to make on our Northwoods WI or MI cabin trips. Its easy to make, quite tasty, and perfect for a quick meal on a cold night. I always use 1 red and 1 yellow bell pepper (we don't care for green peppers). I find that adding salt to the potatoes and stirring them around a bit seems to result in less mushy potatoes. Add the remaining ingredients when potatoes are just starting to get tender. The sausage will take longer than 5 minutes to cook through. Just stir the potatoes, onions, peppers, and sausage every now and then. The potatoes will finish cooking by the time the other ingredients are done. I will note that I have never gotten the potatoes to brown as shown in the picture, but perhaps that can be accomplished if you cook the potatoes separate from the sausage, onions, and peppers and mix together at the end, resulting in potatoes not becoming mush. VARIATIONS: Try seasoning potatoes with salt, black pepper, garlic powder (or even 1 clove garlic, minced), onion powder, paprika, and herbs such as basil, parsley, oregano, thyme etc. Ive also lightly salted/seasoned each layer of ingredients (i.e. potatoes first, then a little more seasoning to onions, peppers, and sausage). Be careful not to over salt as the sausage already has a higher salt content.
