I did this for my first time cooking a turkey and it came out delicious!  I followed the suggestion to cut the honey 50/50 with karo and it was perfect.  Since then, I've learned to do the following with it (coping from an email to a family member) -- 

I use ground sage, ground parsley, raw honey, and I cut the honey 50/50 with karo. 

Brush the bird liberally with the oil. Liberally dust the bird with the herbs in step 2. I usually pat/rub the herbs into it and make sure the bird is pretty well covered with them. Make sure you get under the wings and on all sides of the bird.

Don't put the bird in a bag, but cover it with aluminum foil. Try to avoid opening the oven except to pull it out and brush it with glaze.  (We have a gas oven).

We brush the bird about every 30 minutes while it's cooking until it's done.

Note: If you scale the recipe to the poundage of bird you have, you'll be drowning in glaze. What I do is scale it up for the first part, then I cut all the other stuff (needed in step 4 for the glaze) in half from there.  I still have glaze left over, but I'm not wasting a ton of ingredients and it's much easier to manage mixing everything together.
