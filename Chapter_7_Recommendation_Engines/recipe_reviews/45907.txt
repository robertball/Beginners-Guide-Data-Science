I added 1 tablespoon worchestershire to the beaten eggs, upped the garlic powder to a teaspoon and a half, eliminated the salt and I used black forest ham. I've made a variation of this meatloaf for years after seeing it on Barefoot Contessa. Minced onion and garlic powder works well if you don't feel like chopping. 1 pound ground beef and 1 pound ground pork are excellent for this recipe. 1 TBS of worcestershire sauce makes this really pop. I omit the salt and pepper. I do add ground basil and oregano. I use prosciutto in place of ham. This meatloaf is good topped with red marinara sauce or nothing on top. It freezes and reheats well after its been cooked. I've used mozzarella when I was out of provolone, it was good but the provolone is better. I line a cookie sheet or baking pan with aluminum foil. I flatten the meat mixture on the the foil, press it out flat in all directions. Should almost cover the whole pan, maybe 1/4 inch thick. Then use the foil to roll the meat, like you would a jelly roll. Once i get the first roll of the meat, i gently pull the foil up and away from me and let the meat roll itself. Unroll the foil, position the meat in the center of the pan and pinch the sides and seam closed. I like to give it a little squish from each end with my hands. Just grab the ends with both hands and push hands towards each.  Cook the meatloaf on the foil lined pan for easily clean up. You will love! I believe Barefoot Contessa called it Italian stuffed meatloaf. Goes great with no yolk or egg noodles and brown gravy. This meatloaf is so good and comes out moist that it won't need gravy. I bake at 350 for 60 minutes
