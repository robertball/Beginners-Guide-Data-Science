I love popovers... so simple and inexpensive, yet they are a showstopper at dinner!  I always put a small pad of butter (about 1/4 teaspoon) in the bottom of my popover pan, and put the pan in the oven for a minute or two BEFORE pouring in the batter.  When the pan is hot and the butter melted, then I quickly pour in the batter and put it in the oven for the recommended time.  I find the butter gives the crust a better texture, it prevents sticking (which allows the batter to rise VERY high for super big popovers) and it adds a little more taste to an otherwise bland bread-product.  I sometimes add a bit of herbs to the batter to keep things interesting!

These were incredible!!!!

A couple things to note (reason for not giving a full 5 stars): 
1. The pan should be preheated before pouring the batter into the cups.
2. To really make them rise quickly, pour 1/2 cm (or a bit more) of vegetable oil into the bottom of the muffin cups before pre-heating the pan. The hot oil combining with the batter will start the cooking & rising process before they even have a chance to get back into the oven!
3. Make SURE the batter is room temperature before pouring it into the pre-heated pan. 

I added 1/2 tsp vanilla and a pinch of cinnamon to the batter, then refrigerated it for a couple hours until I was ready to make them. I took them out of the fridge with enough time for everything to return to room temperature, then pulsed it in the blender for a few whirls to smooth out some large chunks and incorporate more air. 

Keep a close eye on these! Mine were done in only 30 minutes!

I served them with honey butter and there wasn't a crumb left :)

Change the following and you'll get a much better popover:
1. Critical: warm the milk first (30 second in a microwave)
2. Put the pan in the oven while it's preheating.
3. Put a dab of butter in each cup before adding the batter.
4. If you have it, use bread flour instead.
5. Set the oven to 425 instead of 450 for the first 20 minutes.
6. Reduce the salt slightly.

I make a larger batch (3 eggs, 1.5 cups of flour and milk, 1t salt).

Don't get me wrong, if you follow the recipe as written, you'll get something tasty, but it's not a popover.  (I made 2 batches back to back and the difference was night and day.)
