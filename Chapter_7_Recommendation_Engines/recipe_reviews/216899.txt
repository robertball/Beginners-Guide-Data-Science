I made these and changed this recipe up becuase I wanted more of a Mexican flavor. I used 1 pound ground beef, 1/2 chopped white onion, 1 tablespoon Spanish Gardens taco seasoning,  1 can original Rotel, 1/2 cup cooked rice (I cooked the rice in chicken stock for more flavor), a couple pinches of salt (the taco seasoning I used does NOT have added salt, so this needed some salt), 1 heaping teaspoon of minced garlic, serveral shakes of ground black pepper, 4 poblano peppers, and shredded Cobly jack cheese. I cut the Poblanos in half length-wise, deseeded, and roasted them in the oven on 400 for 15 minutes. I browned the beef with the onion, salt and pepper and drained the grease. I  added the garlic, taco seasoning, rotel, and let it simmer about 10 minutes while the rice cooked. Then I added the cooked rice. I spooned the ground beef and rice mixture into the Poblano peppers and sprinkled the shredded cheese on top. I popped them back in the 400 degree oven for about 15 minutes (until the cheese was nicely melted). I served them with sour cream. These were fantastic! Great starter recipe you can make tweaks to for your own taste!
