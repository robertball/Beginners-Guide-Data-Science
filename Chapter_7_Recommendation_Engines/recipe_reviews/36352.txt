This really is a good recipe! I only had a little bit of Catalina dressing left in the fridge, so I made a real small batch of the Russian Style Creamy Salad Dressing from this site and mixed it with the 3 Tbs of Catalina dressing I had, as well as honey to taste and half a package of the onion soup mix. I had 2 - 1 lb pork tenderloins. I seasoned with salt and pepper, and browned on all sides. I put the tenderloins on a lined baking sheet and covered them in the sauce. Because I used the Creamy Salad Dressing, it really adhered to the pork. I did not need to baste it all. I left it uncovered and baked at 350 degrees for 40 minutes (all depends on your oven temperature and size of the meat). They came out at 150 degrees. I covered with foil and let rest for 10 minutes to come up to temperature (160 degrees) before I sliced them. Amazing results! Never would have thought combining the dressing with honey and onion soup mix would be that delicious! My family loved it. Thanks, Christine!

This recipe is wonderful, and does not necessarily need to be used with a pork tenderloin. I've also made this recipe with pork chops and chicken breasts. For the sauce, I use 8 oz. Russian dressing, 1/2 C  honey (3/4 is too sweet), 1 envelope soup mix and 2 tbsp balsamic vinger (per a suggestion from another review). I set aside about 1/2 c. of the sauce and used the rest as a marinade, marinating the chicken breasts for 5 hours.  (Next time I will marinade them for longer.) I then grilled the chicken, using the sauce I set aside, as a baste. The important thing with this sauce is to not baste too soon, as the honey can cause it the chicken to burn. It's best to begin basting when the chicken is 3/4 cooked. Enjoy!

So I made it tonight for the first time.  I carefully read the recipe and instructions AND all of the comments people have posted.  I decided to go with the recipe, using 2/3 cup honey, about 2/3 of a package of onion soup, plus a Tbs Balsamic vinegar, a sprinkle of cayenne pepper powder and 1/4 cup chicken stock.

I tasted the mixture at each stage and found that it improved (IMHO) with the addition of the extra ingredients.  The chicken stock was only used to thin it out a bit as I was definitely worried about ending up with a paste instead of a sauce.  

I tied my tenderloin using butcher string and prepared it according to the directions.  It turned out fantastic!  Going forward, I will probably drain off the "sauce" at the 30 minute mark, when the recipe calls for the removal of the tinfoil.  This way I can prevent the sauce from hardening at the edges of the pan and minimize the grease from the tenderloin, floating on top of the sauce.  I will put it in small bowls and use it for dipping sauce.

I paired this with K.I.S.S. Brussel Sprouts (from allrecipes's) and Suzy's red mashed potatoes (also from allrecipes's).
