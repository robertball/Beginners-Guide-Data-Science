I regularly make bobotie and thought to give this recipe a try. It was "okay", but I have to suggest a few changes:
As said by someone else - you CAN brown the mince and onions in a pan !! (I usually do). Then add the seasoning + 1 pinch of ground cloves + 1 pinch of ground coriander seed.
Use 2 slices of soaked bread that haven't been drained too well and mix into the mince. Then place in a baking dish. Bake at 200C for about 15min.
For the topping: if using 2 eggs it gives a lighter and fluffier dish if you use about 150/200ml of milk (maybe even more if you like the creamy topping). Bake 20 min.
To make it even easier: If you're frying the mince before baking - place the seasoned mince in a baking dish and pour over the topping immediately. Bake at 200C for 35-45min. untill golden brown. The topping seeps into the mince mixture giving you a deliciously moist dish.
Serve with yellow rice with raisins. (Just steam your rice with some tumeric, raisins and a pinch of cinnamon). 
A light green salad, slices of banana and caramalised sweet potatoes make it complete.

The recipe is fine, as long as it's kept in mind that, as a trad. dish, there are hundreds of small variations. I use apricot jam instead of chutney, but a fruity, quite sweet chutney is fine.

One reviewer was wrong when she said the meat and onions are always fried in a pan first. No, this should be a quick dish (once you have the ingredients) and I certainly have never fried the meat first.

One person altered the recipe so much that it could not bear much resemlance to the original! I understand about the milk allergy, but why leave out the breadcrumbs? It improves the texture. Try soy milk next time, or yoghurt, if that's okay.

The egg-milk-turmeric custard should be poured over the top about 20 mins. before it comes out of the oven, and is a "characteristic" of this comfort food.

Low-carb:
Substitute 1/4 cup oats for the white bread. Crumb the oats into oat flour in a food processor, and soak in milk as directions suggest.

As a low-carb version of the traditional side dish, you can make "yellow rice" cauliflower. Break up cauliflower into rice-sized pieces in a food processor. Find a yellow rice recipe you like, and use the spices suggested (usually you add things like raisins and cinnamon). After spicing, cook the cauliflower in a little bit of oil in a frying pan for about five minutes.
