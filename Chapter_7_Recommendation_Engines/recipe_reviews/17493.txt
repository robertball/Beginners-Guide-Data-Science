This is our go-to recipe for a quick, easy macaroni and cheese.  It seems that the problem most people have with it is that it's not creamy, but if you are a fan of baked macaroni and cheese, this is the recipe for you.  (If you want a creamier mac and cheese, leave out the eggs and add a couple of American cheese slices.)  It sets up nice and firm.  I mix everything right in the crock pot so it's a one dish wonder.  The only changes I make to the recipe are doubling it (we are a family of 5 that loves it), and in addition to salt and pepper, adding some dried garlic, mustard powder, cumin, and chili powder for some light chili flavoring.  When I double it, I also use only about 4 cups of cheese mixed in and a little extra on top, and that seems to be plenty.  We cook on low for about 3 hours and it turns out great.  I cook it until the edges are slightly browned because some of us love those crispy bits.

Please note that I made 3 minor alterations: added a bit more cheese (parmesan romano, large grate), added more spices (which I do to basically every recipe ever), and I only cooked it for 4 hours, because it smelled done. But that's it!

What a winner!
I don't understand the complaints about this recipe! Really: this is the totally easiest recipe I maybe have ever made, ever - and it's delicious.
Just like most over-baked pasta dishes, there's a 'lack of creaminess' and a 'custardy-ness' to it, I suppose... but that's primarily a difference in baking over cooking, and has a lot more to do with cooking dry pasta without the typical boil-and-drain than the eggs.
I did not find the additions of the eggs to be either an issue or a detriment to texture or taste, and it adds some needed balance in the nutrition category because - let's face it - mac n cheese isn't exactly health food.
And both my nearly-40-year-old, bacon-obsessed husband and my 11 1/2-month-old gobbled this up, too!
I didn't LOVE the addition of the evaporated milk, because it's the only thing in the recipe I don't usually stock, so I'm going to try to do it with a slightly upped amount of all whole milk next time. And I'll be changing the cooking time, due to my slow cooker running a little hot, to 3.5 hours. Or shift it from 'low' to 'warm' at the three hour mark, and cook a total of four.

Seriously, I looked long and hard for the right one-pot, no boil, slow cooker mac n cheese recipe, and this is it!

Had a day dedicated to yard work and some time in the pool afterward, so I wanted a meal that could be prepared early in the day and good-to-go at dinner time (salmon patties, coleslaw, and mac & cheese).  I think the key to success on this recipe is to not overcook it.  I used 2% Kraft sharp cheese, skim milk (plus evaporated) and followed the directions with the exception of the cooking time.  I read the reviews, and many said it was done at 3 hr instead of 5 to 6, so I checked at 2 hr 45 minutes.  At that point, it already was a little overdone, so in the future I'll turn off the heat at 2-1/2 hr.  The center of the mac cheese may be a little soupy, but if you check around the edges, they're very brown, a bit dry, but the elbow macs are cooked.  Once you give it a stir, it will be creamy (as long as you don't overcook it).  I wouldn't say that I like this better than my homemade mac & cheese made with a cheese sauce and baked in the oven.  But I will say that this was pretty darn good for basically mixing everything together (not even cooking the macaroni), pouring it into a crock pot, and letting it do its thing.  For taste, I'd give this a 5, but the timing is so off, that I'm giving it a 4-star rating.  Just start checking for doneness at about 2-1/2 hours.
