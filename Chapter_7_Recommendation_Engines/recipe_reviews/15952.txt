This is my all-time favorite cookie!!  I am always getting requests for them... they are "my signature cookies".  My recipe is different only that I use 2 eggs instead of 4, so if you are short of eggs...I'm telling you they turn out awesome with 1/2 the eggs.  I let them cool and top with a dollop of chocolate frosting - boil 6TB butter, 3/4 c sugar, 1/3 c milk for a minute, remove from heat and add 3/4 c chocolate chips, stir until thickened.  You must try these cookies!!!  Update for those having trouble getting the cookies off the waffle iron...you might want to give your waffle iron a spray of Pam before you drop the cookies on, after you make them a couple of times you won't need that.  Also, I use a Pampered Chef cookie scoop and drop into each compartment.  Have never put chocolate chips or nuts in them, I wouldn't suggest it though.

These are a very easy cookie to make.  I only make them at Christmas time and my family and friends look forward to them.

I use a small round waffle iron that quarters the waffles and I sift the flour and cocoa.  I don't believe it works well on a Belgian waffle maker.

I warm my waffle iron on medium heat for about 5 minutes so that it is good and hot.  Always spray the top and bottom with some type of cooking spray.  When I feel it is hot enough I put my test ones on and cook for 2 minutes, that way they are good and done.  Maybe even over done.  From then on I put the dough on the waffle iron on medium heat and leave on for 1 minute and 15 seconds, yes I do time it.  Using a fork I separate along the lines of the waffle iron and then lift each one out using a fork.

I cool them and store over night the next day they will be moist.  I mix a chocolate frosting and spread over the waffle cookie and wait for my family to finish them off so I can make more!!!
