UPDATE: MADE THIS AGAIN TONIGHT. THIS CERTAINLY WARMS YOU UP ON A CHILLY NIGHT! HAVING MADE THIS TWO TIMES NOW, I HAVE LEARNED A FEW TRICKS TO MAKE THIS EVEN BETTER. TO REITERATE, BE SURE TO USE FROZEN EGG NOODLES (I.E. REAMS BRAND). THEY ARE AN ABSOLUTE MUST! SECOND, IN ORDER FOR YOUR SAUCE TO THICKEN NICELY, SIMMER FOR AT LEAST 30 MINUTES (I HAD TO SIMMER MINE FOR 40 MINUTES TO REACH A DESIRABLE CONSISTENCY). Yummy! Very much like Grandma's (though her noodles were always homemade).

Here's an alternative for thicker consistency and no precooking the chicken: 

Boil 16 ounces of frozen noodles in water OR chicken broth (your choice)  stop just before noodles are real tender; drain most of the broth or water into a bowl for use if needed later.

Add 2 or 3  10 ounce cans of condensed Cream of Chicken soup and/or Cream of Mushroom soup (kids tend to prefer just the Cream of Chicken  your choice). Stir in 2 cans and judge consistency; add more as desired. You may also want to add a can after judging consistency with chicken added next.

Add 2 to 4 13 oz. CANS of drained and thoroughly rinsed, fully cooked chicken breast (Daily Chef brand from SAM's Club for example). Amount of chicken depends on personal choice. 

Rinse in a strainer to break up chicken chunks into even smaller pieces as desired, this makes sure there's chicken in every bite.

Season to taste and slowly add broth/water saved earlier to thin consistency as desired.

OPTION: for more nutrition, add 1 or 2 small cans of mixed vegetables (like peas and carrots)  again, your choice (gets the kids to eat their veggies!). Even small pieces of cooked cauliflower will work!

Simmer, stirring frequently, for a minimum of 20 minutes.
