I found that if you follow the amount of baking powder in this recipe as directed, it is just way too much.  It leaves a chemical taste to the muffins.  Not good!!  

I actually did a little research (Wikipedia is awesome!) on why this is...  when a recipe has excess acidity (as this one does due to the OJ) the baking power doesn't fully react and you get that chemical taste left behind.  A number of reviewers said that they halved the amount of baking power just by intuition.  Well, I guess there is science behind it too!

YES! This is it! This is the best muffin recipe I've tried. 

But... I confess. I did make it my own. 

I was out of OJ so I made my own lemonade by zesting 1 lemon then microwaving it for 20 seconds and juicing well. (pulp and all) I poured that juice and zest into a 1/2 cup measuring cup and topped it off with water. I added 2 Tb of sugar and 1 teaspoon vanilla extract to that. Then stirred in the oats and set aside. 

The only other changes I made were pretty basic. I added melted butter instead of vegetable oil and used a heaping cup of fresh blueberries. Instead of Sprinkling sugar and cinnamon on top I put on Vanilla Sugar (just a couple cups of sugar in a container with some post-use vanilla bean pods). 

These were a little dry when mixing it all together and I was nervous about that so I added in about 3-4 tablespoons of milk before folding in the blueberries. Mine came out moist, but set. I'll probably just make a little more lemonade mixture the next time I make these. Yes ... there will be a next time!!!

When warm---tender, moist and fluffy. Not the least bit rubbery, heavy, or tough, which happens with over-stirring.
When cool and set---they never did toughen or remind me of a scone. They were still tender, fluffy and tasty.
Marrrrvelous with morning coffee!

*Use 1/4 cup butter for 1/2 of the oil--makes a richer taste. 
*Absolutely add vanilla. I added 1 teaspoon. 
*I used only 1 teaspoon baking powder as suggested. No metallic taste.
*Use the zest from the orange. That sharpens the orange taste of the muffins and you'll never separate out the texture of it from the oatmeal, even if you don't typically like or use orange peel. Give it a try! 
*Egg sizes can vary, so I used two smaller eggs, where I'd have originally used one nice large egg. None in the carton met with my approval. : )
*Also, to activate the baking soda, add either a couple Tablespoons of buttermilk or plain yogurt. This will lighten the muffins considerably, and provide additional moisture to the batter. Mine was never dry or worrisome. 
*Put all the wet ingredients together with the oatmeal and set aside while you measure out the dry ingredients into another bowl. Then add the wet ingredients all at once to the dry, only stirring until just combined; fold in the blueberries. Overstir=rubber muffins.
*The suggested 1/2 tsp. nutmeg added to the topping is a must if you like nutmeg. It pairs with orange extremely well. 
*Use brown sugar when making the topping instead of white. It goes perfectly with oatmeal
