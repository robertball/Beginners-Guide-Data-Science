We like short ribs and I prepare them several times each winter so I am always looking for new. This is an excellent recipe. I have found the best way to reduce the fat content is to oven-brown the short ribs prior to adding to the sauce. Place bone side down in a pan, sesaon with pepper & salt roast for 45 minutes at 450. Drain off fat. If ribs are large return to oven for 15 minutes more.  I was reluctant to try this the first time but it's the only way I pre-pare them now. I got over 1/2 cup of fat from the 2 1/2 lbs ribs I used for this recipe. I browned the onions in a dutch oven, added a a couple minced garlic cloves, and about 1/2 broth to the sauce ingredients and then the browned ribs. Baked covered at 325 for 2 1/2 hours. Served with parmesan mashed potatoes. They were moist and succulent. Received rave reviews with request for promise to repeat.

I tried this recipe as a first attempt at cooking short ribs. I chose it, because it did not include flour, or pre-browning the meat. I was a bit concerned about adding the red pepper flakes, as I can't usually eat spicy foods, but decided to leave them in when preparing the braise. I purchased 3 1/2 lbs. of short ribs, and my butcher graciously cut them in thirds, making 9 portions. The smaller portions made it easier to fit them in the pot. I like mushrooms, and  added an 8 ounce pack of mixed wild mushrooms at the same time to the pot as the meat and onion. 
I turned the meat each 45 minutes, and noticed a few of the bones fell away at the second turning. I tasted the sauce at each turning. At the first turn the sauce was quite acidic and a bit spicy; the second turn was very much more mild, but vinegary. At the third turn, about 30 min before I turned off the heat to the pot, the sauce had become a deeper flavour, and the spice had mellowed to a nice, zesty tone. 
As promised, the meat was fork tender, and I found the sauce to be quite nice. While beef is a bit expensive, I would certainly feel confident serving this to guests, and think it would benefit well from mashed potatoes, rice, or some broad noodles.
