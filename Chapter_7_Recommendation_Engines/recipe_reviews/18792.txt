I don't cook with corn starch so I used about 3-4 tablespoons flour (depending on the thickness you desire) and mixed it with 1/2 cup chicken broth. Used white pepper as a matter of preference to bring out a more asian flavor and omitted the shrimp/beef. Well, let me first say that I'm allergic to shellfish, so the shrimp and oyster sauce were left out.  I made the recipe with a combo of ground beef and pork because I've seen this dish traditionally made with pork.  I also was really tempted to drain off the fat, but didn't because the recipe didn't say to- next time I will.  I served it over rice noodles and it was BLAND.  Not nearly spicy enough.  I ended up adding 2 tbs of chili paste and then it was oh so yummy.  I still think I need to add something in place of oyster sauce to give it depth..maybe a hoisin, fish or teryaki sauce instead?  Next time I will make it with 2 tbs of chili paste and add another eggplant because it really cooks down and becomes unnoticeable in the dish.  My husband didn't even know eggplant was in it until I told him.  Oh, and over a package of rice noodles, this easily serves 6-8.  I would give it 4 stars, but my husband hated it, so I'll have to settle for 3.
