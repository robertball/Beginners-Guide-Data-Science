Normally I wouldn't leave a 5-star review for something I had to tweak, but this cookie is SO worth it! I got 3 bakers dozen from this recipe, measuring my dough about 2 tsp per cookie. 

USE BUTTER. I cannot stress this enough. This is a shortbread cookie. It is not sweet, it will not spread, it will crumble slightly if cooked properly. Also, cream vanilla into butter/sugar/yolk. Most bakers already know to mix their wet, then mix the dry in a separate bowl and add to wet, but a novice baker might not.

CHILL FINISHED DOUGH before shaping into balls. 

ALLOW COOKIE BALLS TO WARM to room temperature before pressing your fingerprint. Cold dough will crack and make your cookie brittle.

FILL BEFORE BAKING. After cooking for 5 min, my cookies had a crust that broke and left a brittle cookie with ugly bare streaks. When I pre-filled, the cookies were smoother, but had no coating because my daughter needed a nice looking batch for her bf. I will test this again with black sesame for good photo contrast and because it's yummy. :)

BAKE FOR 15 MIN AT 300. I also lined my pan with ungreased foil, it makes it easier to move to cooling rack and keeps the bottom from burning.

COOL THOROUGHLY BEFORE TOUCHING. The filling is HOT. YOU WILL GET BURNED. And it's just not fun to run to the sink with jam hardening onto your skin.

These would be a great project for kids over 3 to help with. Just make sure they don't poke through the bottom!

ok....here's the honest to goodness bottom line review on these cookies.  I did a trial run because of all the reviews and differences of opinion.  I wanted to get them right.  They have been a long time favorite of mine since childhood and since mom has passed, it's my turn to bring the memories back.  This recipe has a delicious shortbread flavor.  You can use either walnuts or almonds.  I followed the ingredients perfectly....although I prepared more nuts than suggested. It doesn't yield much so if you want at least 3 dozen, ya gotta double or triple the ingredients. Since the cookies DO NOT spread, make them as large or as petite as you prefer and this will obviously affect your yield.  I cooked them at 300 on non-stick aluminum foil.  I refrigerated the dough just a bit. If you chill it too long, it gets really hard just like re-refrigerating a stick of butter.  Once I rolled the balls in the egg white and nuts, I place them on the cookie sheet.  I then used my thumb and made the center hole.  I found that by making the hole after they have cooked for 5 minutes, they cracked on the sides.  Do not go all the way down to the bottom...just enough of a dent to hold the jelly.  I then filled each of the holes with my choice of jelly.  I put the jelly in a zip lock bag; cut the bottom tip off and piped in the jelly.  I baked them at 300 for 13-15 minutes.  All you are looking for is a lightly browned bottom.  This cookie has a buttery, flaky, shortbread texture.
