These are so delicious!  I did as many reviewers suggested and halved the sauce recipe; I found this to be the perfect amount.  I also cut my bacon into thirds as another reviewer suggested and found that this was ok 95% of the time; the bacon shrinks as it cooks so if you cut your bacon into thirds make sure it's not wrapped too tightly or the bacon breaks.  I made half of my recipe using one can of (sliced) water chestnuts (I doubled up some of the smaller ones) and the other half using (fresh) pineapple (~ 1/6th or so?) as dadelson suggested- both were excellent and I don't know if I could pick a favorite if I had to!  The waterchestnuts are still crisp and so for those it's more of a texture thing but the sweetness of the pineapple perfectly offsets the saltiness of the bacon so...  YUM!  

I baked at the recommended temp until the bacon was "done"- firm but not crispy- about 25 minutes?  Perhaps 30...  Then I dipped each bacon-wrapped morsel in the sauce (tapping off the excess) and transported them to a friend's house for her baby shower.  Once there we popped them in the oven until the sauce had just begun to carmelize- about 10 or 15 minutes, I think.  They were a huge success and the plates were cleaned within about 10 minutes, max!  I will DEFINTELY make these again and again.

I make these 2-3 times a year by request for parties and Holidays. This recipe is the closest I have found to how I actually make them. I use 1/2 cup brown sugar instead of the full cup, 1 cup of chili sauce and 1 cup of ketchup. I also add a tablespoon or two of soy sauce (in addition to Worcestershire) to taste.  Sometimes I add another 1/4 cup of brown sugar if the sauce needs it. I cut the bacon in thirds and cut most of the chestnuts in half to make them more bite sized. 

When I am taking these to a party I pre bake them in the oven without sauce for 10-15 mins. Then I dip each one into the sauce  and transfer to a crock pot on low. I pour the remaining sauce over the contents in the pot. This way you can just take the pot to the party and people can dig in. When I make these at home I pre bake them as I said above and then dip and transfer to a 9x13 pan and finish baking in the oven. I have also used maple bacon to wrap the nuts but keep the brown sugar to a minimum of you do that. 

I also heat the sauce in a small saucepan to make sure all of the elements combine. I think it glazes the bits better this way. Seriously the Heinz chili sauce makes the sauce really special- give it a try!

I looked all through the reviews on this recipe and made these few changes with amazing mouth watering results. Half all of the ingredients except the water chestnuts. Buy whole water chestnuts and cut them in halves. Cut the Bacon in half first vertically and then horizontally (the wrap will leave for great presentation). Use toothpicks! Preheat to 400 degrees while making the sauce. Wrap the bacon slices around the halved eater chestnuts and place toothpick into it to hold Bacon into place, do not add the sauce. Cook in 9x13 pan for 15 minutes at 400 degrees. Remove Bacon wrapped chestnuts and submerge them completely in the sauce. The more you put on the chestnuts the more of a "burnt" texture you receive, which is not a bad thing. Do not put the sauce in the pan, it softens the Bacon. Put the bacon wrapped sauce glazed chestnuts back into the oven for 15 additional minutes. Take out, cool for 10 minutes, and serve. You will not be disappointed.
