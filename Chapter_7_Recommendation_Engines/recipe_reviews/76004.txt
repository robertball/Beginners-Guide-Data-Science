This ice cream was such a hit and SO easy to make!!  I love the idea of using ice sandwiches as a base and all the options you can create.  Instead of using Cool Whip, which I am not a real fan of, I used name brand ice cream, let it melt for a few minutes and using my hand mixer, I blended in a Butterfinger chocolate bar into the semi-melted ice cream.  I used this as my layer between the ice cream sandwiches and on top.  I also used a loaf pan and lined it with plastic wrap for easy removal of the cake.  Once the ice cream cake was semi-hardened, I drizzled melted chocolate on the top.  My kids loved it and my husband said it was better than Baskin Robbins!
This recipe is a keeper!!  Thank you for sharing it with us all!!

I would give this 5 stars for ease and 4 stars for taste.  I actually did something a little different for the cool whip...I mixed 16 oz. cool whip with two-thirds to three-fourths of a carton of  1/2 gallon of vanilla ice cream by mixing it in an electric mixer (obviously, let the ice cream and cool whip soften before you do this).  I used this cool whip/ice cream mixture in place of the 100% cool whip and it worked quite well and tasted quite good.  To add some flavor and color, I crushed up some Butterfingers and sprinkled them in between the ice cream sandwich layers. I did put some caramel topping on that layer as well, but I think I could have left it off just as easily.  This definitely required 24 ice cream sandwiches and I used an aluminum roaster pan as you really need the depth (a normal 9x12 will be too shallow), and this was the perfect size for it all. The layering looked really nice when it is cut into.  Like other posters, you do need to work relatively fast due to melting!  I topped mine off with shaved chocolate bar, but could have also sprinkled it with some leftover crushed Butterfingers...make sure you sprinkle it with these topping just after you make it so it can "set" in the "icing" in the freezer.
