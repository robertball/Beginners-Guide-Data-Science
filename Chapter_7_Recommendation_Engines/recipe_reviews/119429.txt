This recipe was tested by the most picky of food critics...middle school students.

I teach a culinary class through Family and Consumer Sciences at a middle school and gave this recipe as a cooking lab.  

Yes, I heard the familiar, eww...black beans, onions, tomatoes.  However, I told them to try it.  Well, let's just say the room was quiet and mouths were full when they tried it.  They loved it!  I had many kids tell me they were going to make it at home for their parents!

This recipe was very easy, affordable, nutritious and really yummy!

I like to heat up the tortillas on a skillet for a few minutes to toast them and make them more "homemade".  It really makes them taste even better!

This is a truly lovely recipe.  Just as stated in the title it is spiced just right.  The chili's are a great touch! I like the fact that the ingredients are not mushed together, so all the textures can be experienced.  The things I changed were simple, and not taste altering.  I substituted a can of red kidney beans for one can of black beans. To add color and sweetness I added chunky pieces of yam or what New Zealanders call "kumera".  I heated the yam in the microwave until their peels were soft, and began to pull away from the skin when touched, that way they were easy to peel.  I then let them cool, peeled them, and cut them into chunks, adding them at the same time as the beans and spices.  The result was a very colorful dish.
