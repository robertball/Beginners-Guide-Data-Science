text:
I make a very similar recipe. It has been my go to shrimp pasta recipe for years. I saute a small onion in olive oil, then add the garlic cloves for one minute. Then I add the fire roasted tomatoes, 1/2 c. white wine, fresh chopped oregano and some red pepper flakes. I simmer this together for 15 minutes or so. After the pasta is done, I add the shrimp to the sauce and cover the pot. When they have turned pink, I crumble in 4 oz. feta and stir well. Pour it over the pasta, garnish with fresh parsley and serve. Key is not to overcook the shrimp. Cheese does not need to melt, just be distributed well in sauce.

1) In the future I will use jumbo shrimp.   The recipe calls for medium shrimp.  The grocery store only had jumbo shrimp...so the pound was only about 18 shrimp, but by the time they were shelled and cooked they shrunk up.  I would prefer the larger shrimp over the small shrimp.

2) My first step after cleaning and shelling was to put the shrimp in a zip-lock bag, pour in a couple of tablespoons of canola oil and mixed in about 6 cloves of fresh garlic and salt.  I marinated the shrimp in this for about 2 hours and it made all the difference giving the shrimp an incredible flavor.

3) Recipe calls for 3 cloves garlic.  It definitely needs more than that.  I minced about 6 or 7 LARGE cloves of garlic.

4) I use fire-roasted diced tomatoes *with garlic* to add more flavor.

5) The editors suggested 3 tbl of fresh basil instead of the one tablespoon in the recipe.   I think even more could have been used.

6) For an additional 'kick' I added a few shakes of crushed red pepper and about 1/4 tsp of Ancho chili pepper powder. 

7) I served one portion with feta sprinkled at the end. It was MUCH better mixing it into the pasta before serving to let it melt a bit.
