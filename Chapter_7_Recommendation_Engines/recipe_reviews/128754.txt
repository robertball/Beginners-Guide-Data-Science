I started with the posted recipe and yes, I did make some changes to it. I used one can of french cut green beans and about a half package of frozen chopped spinach (it was a 32 oz. bag, so it came out to about 16 oz of chopped spinach) I just added straight from the bag still frozen. I used 1 can of mushroom soup, 1 package of dry onion soup mix ( I always add it to my green bean casserole so went with this time) rather  than a whole cup of sour cream, I used about  1/2 cup of sour cream and 1/2 cup of softened cream cheese. I also added some bacon crumbles because I had some left and figured they would go well in the mix!

For the topping, I put a layer of the french fried onions then added a layer of frozen shredded hashbrowns. I baked it for 1/2 hour then put it under broiler for about 10 minutes to brown up the hashbrowns, once the hashbrowns crisped up a little, I topped with cheddar cheese and returned to broil until the cheese melted. 

My kids have been eating the requisite green bean casseroles in many forms for many years and are usually pretty unimpressed with the dish... I got thumbs up and applause for this version!
