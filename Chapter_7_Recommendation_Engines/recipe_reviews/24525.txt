Excellent recipe, but when I looked at the amount of flour, I knew this wasn't going to make a cake big enough for the six people in my house. For my maternal grandmother, who taught me to cook, all recipes started with 2 cups of flour... it was the only thing she ever gave a definite measure for. So, like her, I started out measuring out those two cups of flour, then doubling that, and the rest of the recipe. I used three eggs instead of four. I used 3/4 cup of granulated sugar and 3/4 cup of Splenda, since I am diabetic, and I regularly convert recipes with sugar so that I can have my cake and eat it, too. LOL! I added about a half a cup of raisins and two teaspoons of vanilla to the cake. My only glitch was in making the streusel. Two tablespoons of butter was a bit too much, possibly because I used Splenda's Brown Sugar Blend to help shave off a little more of the sugar, and instead of being crumbly as it should have been, it was more of a paste; so I dropped it on to the batter in spoonfuls and swirled it into it like a marble cake. The texture of the final product was wonderful... soft inside , with just a bit of cookie like crunch to the outside. Even my son, who doesn't like the taste of Splenda declared it worthy of even his picky palate. All in all, a very taste-pleasing dish.
