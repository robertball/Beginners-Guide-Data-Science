The skin of the hazelnut is quite bitter. To remove it easily, in a large saucepan, bring 3 cups of water to a boil. Add 4 tablespoons of baking soda and 1 cup of hazelnuts and boil nuts for about 3 minutes. Test a nut under cool running water. If the skin doesn't slip off easily, boil nuts another minute or two. After peeling, nuts should be crisped and lightly browned in a 350 degree oven for about 20 minutes.

Once I found this recipe, I haven't even bothered looking for another biscotti recipe. It's the perfect base for any adaptations. I absolutely love this biscotti! I have made it for years now around the holidays and it has become a requested favorite from my family. With that said, here are some tips on how I perfected my recipe. :)

Usually, I substitute almonds since hazelnuts can be hard to come by and are expensive. Even with the almonds, they are delicious. They are not as hard as traditional biscotti which I don't mind. 

I know others have mentioned that the biscotti is delicate and crumbles easily. I found a fix that seems to help with this. When preparing the nuts I grind them in my Ninja blender until it is becomes more of a powder. This allows the nuts to mix into the dough while reducing the air pockets that result in the crumbling. 
After they are done baking, I let them cool (sometimes by putting them in the freezer for about 5-10minutes) then I melt semi-sweet chocolate in a double boiler and drizzle it over the top of the biscotti. Make sure the biscotti is chilled or at least room temp before you drizzle the chocolate otherwise it will get too runny and make a mess. Once I've drizzled the chocolate, I pop them back in the freezer for another 5-10 to let the chocolate set. This step is especially helpful if you plan to package the biscotti for gifts as it keeps the chocolate drizzle from getting messed up and looking like a blob. 

I hope this helps! Enjoy the biscotti!
