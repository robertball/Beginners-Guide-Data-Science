This is very tasty, but nothing fancy. I used 1 lb of boneless chicken tenders figuring that was a good estimate for 4 breasts. I did use jarred, minced garlic instead of fresh - I spread about 1/4-1/2 tsp on each breast.  Instead of fresh tomatoes, I used half a can of diced tomatoes with most of the juice drained. I have to give this five stars because my picky-picky eater hubby and daughter SCARFED this down.  I did things a little different in preparing it though.  I used Fiesta Party Dip mix instead of red peppers and instead of baking it, I sauteed in a frying pan.  I put a Tbsp of butter in the pan, dropped in about a rounded tsp of minced garlic and half of the herb/salt mix.  Spread it around in the pan and then layed my cut up chicken breast in the pan, covered and cooked for five minutes.  Sprinkled the rest of the herb mix on top, flipped the chicken in the pan to brown the other side then transferred to a baking sheet.  I then broiled the herbed chicken breast with shredded mozzerella/asiago cheese on top.  Also instead of slice tomato, I put about a cup full of canned crushed tomatoes in the pan that I sauteed the chicken in and let that cook to get warm and slightly thickened and served a dollop of this tomato sauce on top of each piece of chicken.  I served this over penne rosa and it was a big hit/definite keeper!
