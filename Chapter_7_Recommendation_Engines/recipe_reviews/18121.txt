The pistachio cake recipies I have seen here seem to all mention one problem colapsing.I have found a very simple way to make this cake is to use one box of instant cake mix, one package of pistachio pudding(instant) one small container of sour cream and two eggs. Make sure you grease and flour the bundt pan. I also usualy bake at 300. For frosting use another package of the pistachio pudding and a pint of heavy wipping cream, whip this into a frosting and you are good to go. You can change up and add other ingredients as you wish such as chocolate chips and cherries. Ive also changed the cake and puddng mixes to make other flavors. I also reccomend making double the frosting because it is that good.

Ive been making this recipe now for several months and it always seems to be a winner in the flavor department. I had the same issue as everyone else with the cake falling and turning out with a bit of a gluey texture. So, I asked a baking friend for suggestions. Now, whenever I make this, I separate all the egg yolks from the whites and mix the yolks in with the rest of the recipe as described. Then, I whip up the whites separately to medium peaks and then gently fold them into the batter just before pouring the batter into my prepared pan(s). The other trick that helps is baking the cake for the full time, or longer if needed. Both of these have helped with the falling and improved the texture. So far, the most successful combination has been Devils Food cake mix with Coconut Creme pudding mix.
