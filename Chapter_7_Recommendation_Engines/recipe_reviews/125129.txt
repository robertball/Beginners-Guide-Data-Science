I love these rolls.  Just a hint for mixing any bread dough...combine half the flour and half the water/yeast first until consistant.  Then add more water and more flour a bit at a time until the minimum amount of flour is used up and all the water is gone.  This will prevent having to use all of the flour and will give you lighter rolls or loaves. 

I let the dough rise a long time over an hour *oops*, and I got 36 good size dinner rolls out of the recipe.  They took another 40 minutes to double and filled a large cookie sheet.  Very good!

Oh YUM! I cut this recipe in half to get 12 rolls. I used my Kitchen Aid mixer to make these rolls. I proofed the yeast (two "envelopes", only because I've always had issues getting whole wheat flour to rise so I just threw them both in) with warm buttermilk (instead of water) and sugar for ten minutes, then I added one whole egg, melted butter (I'm low on vegetable oil right now) and all the dry ingredients. I did flip-flop the white flour and the whole wheat flour. I used 2 1/4 cups whole wheat white flour and 1 cup organic AP flour. I did need just a touch more flour than the recipe stated to get it to form into a ball and jump on the bread hook, just under a quarter cup. I kneaded it in the Kitchen Aid with the dough hook for about five minutes. I set it to rise in a greased bowl on my heating pad for an hour, then formed them into rolls and plopped them into greased muffin tins (1/4 cup for each roll), covered them and set them to rise on my heating pad until they doubled. Right before baking, I lightly brushed them with a little melted butter. 400* for about 10 minutes was absolutely perfect. These baked really fast for me. I was thinking fourteen but they smelled done right at ten minutes. Absolutely GREAT whole wheat roll recipe and really can make it all in one day, if you so choose. I'm quite happy with this recipe.
