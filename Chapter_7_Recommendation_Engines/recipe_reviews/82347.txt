It's a good base recipe. I had never made crab cakes before so I was going into this as an experiment.
I am a home cook, but my ambition says I am a professional chef.
I decided to give mine a Mediterranean flair.
I replaced the Mayo with Greet yogurt, used a tablespoon of lemon juice and a teaspoon of cooking wine, and I used a generous amount of sage, orange peel and paprika. I made this, and it had so much filler that I could barely taste the crab.  After reading other recipes, I think the only thing that needs to be tweaked on this one is the process...not necessarily the ingredients.  There was so much dressing relative to the amount of crab meat, I had to add too many cracker crumbs to get it to hold together.

I can see how the size of the egg might make a noticeable difference in the volume of dressing.  And compared to others, this recipe substituted 4 tsp of lemon juice for a TB of the mayo, so it  certainly is runnier by design.

Next time I will put the crab meat into a separate bowl, then add the dressing a little at a time (tossing by hand) to just coat the crab meat, and then add the crackers a little at a time (tossing by hand) to get the desired consistency.

Other recipes called for upping the flavor by adding 1 tsp each Worcestershire sauce and mustard (Dijon, or yellow, or dry).  I'm also thinking that using Ritz crackers might have overwhelmed the crab flavor, so I might try Saltines next time, or perhaps a 50/50 blend.

Usually I would have jumped right back in and tried to make it again with my modifications, but at $16 a pound for crab meat, my appetite for experimentation is not all that great.
