My only "complaint" about this recipe is that I wish it stated at what temperature to "steam" the clams.  My husband and I had quite a disagreement.  He wanted to bring it to a boil and then almost shut the heat off.  I thought it would be hot enough. Our compromise was to turn the heat to medium for about 20 minutes.  49 out of the 50 clams opened.  I was shocked to see how much liquor they make.  I thought the small amount of wine wouldn't be enough.
They were great.  Definitely have a lot of crusty bread on hand.  Linguine or some other pasta is great.  My kids were so brave about trying them.  I can't wait to make it again.  We added some fresh lemon after they were cooked and it really brought out the flavor. Delicious!
Oh, and scrub the clams really well.  I got a lot of sand and gunk off them.
THANKS for a great recipe.

This is very similar to a local dish here in Qingdao, China called 'la gala' 
I have this every couple of days, but we add a little chopped up dried red chillies, a small amount of ginger, and then sprinkle chopped coriander when the dish is finished. When done in a wok at high temperatures, we steam the clams in a very small amount of water 2 minutes & they are all open. The very quickly saute the garlic, ginger & chillies then add the drained clams back in, mix with the garlic etc for 1 minute then add reserved juices back in. Total time 5 minutes at most.

This was really excellent!  I am so glad I made it.  I am a veteran clam cooker, so I'll share my observations/changes.  I used 50 little neck clams.  I used about 9 cloves of garlic.  I saw other reviewers used red pepper flakes, so I added 1/2 fresh minced jalapeno pepper which I sauteed with the garlic.  

I added 2 cups seafood broth from a carton and brought the heat to high for about 2 minutes to reduce to about 1/3.

Reduced heat to medium and added the clams.  I usually steam clams with some liquid at fairly high heat.  Keeping these at medium heat increased the cooking time (duh).  I covered the pot and waited from them to start opening - about 10 minutes.  Added the butter (4 tbsp),  stirred and covered for probably another 8 minutes.

This served 2 with leftovers; could have served 3 reasonable people.

This is a great recipe, thanks Scott!

Mine turned out great! I tossed in cooked linguine before serving. Here are some tips for steaming and getting rid of sand.

To get rid of sand in clams:
1) Soak them neck-high in salt water for about 3 hrs. They will spit out the sand.
2) Gently scoop them out and put them in fresh water (no salt) for 1 hr to desalt them... Unless you like to taste of salty clams.

Steaming clams:
Steaming with medium heat from 3-5 minutes. Time varies depending of type and size of clams. The trick to knowing when they're ALMOST ready is when you observe a burst of steam fromyour pot.
