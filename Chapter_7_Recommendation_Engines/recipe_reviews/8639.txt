This recipe was really good!  I added a good splash of cider vinegar and about 10 dashes of hot sauce to the marinade before boiling.  I also used chicken strips instead of wings. I served the extra sauce for dipping, and the next day, threw the leftovers together into one container.  It microwaved up just as good as the oven version!  The next time I do this, I plan to make rice and serve the chicken and sauce over it!

Update:  Tried this recipe again with much better results.  I ran out of honey after about 1/4 c. so I used light corn syrup and a little maple syrup to make up the rest.  I added about 1 tsp. red pepper flakes and 1 tsp. cayenne pepper and a little extra garlic (2 BIG cloves).  I still could have done with more heat, but it was much more savory and less overpoweringly sweet (maybe the corn syrup was lighter).  Also, I shortened the uncovered bake time to 5 minutes before removing the wings to the heavily oiled rack.  I used the broiler instead of letting them cook for 10 minutes each side just to brown up the skin.  MUCH better result - no charring!  This time I reserved the sauce (checking with a candy thermometer to make sure it got up to 180 degrees) and poured it over white rice on the side.  VERY TASTY!  I may never need to go out for Chinese again.  As written, I still think this recipe gets 4 stars, but with my modifications, I would give it 5 all the way. OLD REVIEW:  Very good sweet flavor, but it needed a little kick.  I tried adding about a half teaspoon of red pepper flakes, but this was no where near enough to cut through the sweetness.  Next time I will try at least a tablespoon of the hot stuff.  They turned out very tender and were not very hard to make, but I thought the last cook time on the rack was a little too long.  Some of my wings got a little charred.  I would keep an eye on them during that last 10 minutes.
