This recipe is extraordinary because it uses very ordinary ingredients that result in the sophisticated taste of eclairs.  

A few hints:  MAKE THE SAUCE FIRST!  It will be nearly cool and ready to spread by the time the other ingredients are assembled.  If you want a more "true" eclair taste, use honey graham crackers instead of chocolate.

You do not need to butter the pan first, and you do not need a full package of graham crackers.  I counted about 20 in my 13x9 pan.  

Take the time to make the chocolate sauce.  It tastes much better than canned, and doesn't have all that heart-clogging palm oil in it.  Also, the sauce needs only 1/2 t. vanilla in my opinion.

This is one of the easiest, best desserts ever.  I have been making this for years with success every time.  It's really hard to screw up something so easy!  Try to make it the day before you want to serve it, if possible.  The graham crackers soften overnight, making it more like a cake.  And my preference is regular graham crackers rather than chocolate.  I also use 4 tablespoons of butter in the topping which makes it really creamy, not hard, as some reviews state.  You should get a thin crisp layer on the top of the icing, like glaze, and it should be creamy underneath.  Please don't ever use canned frosting on this.  I have seen that on many versions of this recipe (and a review of this one), but that is such a disservice to this yummy cake.  The icing is quick and easy enough to make, and it tastes so much better than canned.  I usually make 1.5 times what the recipe calls for!

This is a great recipe, but even better with a few changes. I used low-sugar pudding mix to make it a little less sweet, and I would definitely use regular instead of chocolate graham crackers. The glaze also turns out better if you use less sugar -- makes it less like thick frosting and more like a real eclair glaze. The recipe says to let the glaze cool before you pour it onto the cake... Really delicious! My picky little nephews gobbled it up. Let it sit overnight for the best result.

In my opinion, this is one of the most asked for, and "party-friendly", desserts of all time.  The,  "make the night before" instruction (an absolute MUST to soften the graham crackers) allows you to make it and then move on to other party preparations.....or go to bed as I usually end up doing!
I love the chocolate glaze and believe it finishes off this classy dessert and gives it the look of a true chocolate eclair.....dark and shiny.
As far as the prep for that, I have had to remake the glaze several times before due to ending up with a 'grainy" sugar mess.  I have now changed the prep for that to cutting down the white sugar to 3/4 cup instead of a full cup and allowing the sugar to dissolve in the milk (off heat) for about 5 minutes, stirring often.  Then, I add the cocoa powder, stir well, and slowly bring it all up to a SOFT boil, stirring constantly for 1 minute.  Remove from heat and add the rest of the ingredients as instructed.  Cool for just a little while, stirring gently, and then pour onto the top of the refrigerated cake.  The warmth of the glaze will allow It to spread itself out over the entire cake (with perhaps just a little help to reach the edges).  If you wait until the glaze is too cool, you will have trouble covering the entire surface.....since the coolness of the cake will quickly cool the glaze and turn it into more of a frosting...and there won't be enough to cover the cake!
This glaze recipe makes exactly the right amount...no doubling necessary!

This is a great recipe and so easy to make. Every time I make it for potlucks someone asks me to share the recipe.  The only thing I do different is add extra melted butter to the frosting.  Only using 2 tablespoons make a harder frosting that tends to crack.  If you use 4 to 6 tablespoons you will get a softer glaze like you would find on an eclair.  I think it tastes better with regular honey grahams crackers over the chocolate ones, but it is good either way.  I find that the chocolate graham crackers are sweeter since they are coated with sugar and they tend to get a little mushier than the honey grahams, so the texture is a bit different.  Also, let it sit in the refrigerator at least 8 hours for best results.  I cannot imagine ever substiting this frosting with the store-bought kind.  You must try it!
