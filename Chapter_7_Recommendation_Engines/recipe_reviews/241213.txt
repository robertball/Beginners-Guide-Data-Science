I have steamed my eggs for over a year, ever since I read the instructions online from a professional chef. I steam them exactly 19 minutes & then I dump them into a sink of cold water from the faucet in just enough water to cover them. I crack all of them immediately and I peel them immediately beginning at the fat end where  the air pocket is and you are able to grasp them membrane. the shells and the membrane literally slip off the egg. I rinse them in cold water and place them on a dish towel to dry and place another over them when finished to allow them to cool. this work for 6 or 18. I never have a green film around the yolk. About a week ago I read where you could place them in a pan of cold water bring them to a rolling boil cover them, remove them from the fire & let them sit 15 minutes, crack in cool water peel immediately & you would have perfect eggs. I tried it with 4 eggs and one of them peeled perfectly the others lost part of their whites in the peeling...I will always steam my eggs
