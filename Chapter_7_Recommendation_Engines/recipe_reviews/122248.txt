After making this several times, I've made a few simple changes that made a big difference!  Add chopped onions & bell peppers (about 1/2 cup each) to beef when browning it.  Also, for those who don't like the way the chips get soggy on the bottom, you can omit adding them first, and instead add 1 cup cooked rice.  Then, just add about 2 cups chips (or enough to completely cover the top) during last 5 minutes.  Serve with sour cream, shredded lettuce, chopped tomatoes and refried beans for a complete "Mexican restaurant" style meal!

This was perfect.  I needed to use up some ground beef in the fridge and was not in the mood for heavy duty cooking.

I had 2 pounds of ground beef but only one can of soup, so I added a little chicken broth and some extra shredded cheese to the ground beef to keep it from being dry.  I also added some chili/taco spices to the ground beef as it was cooking (chili powder, garlic, steak seasoning), which I believe added a lot to the flavor.  And finally, I didn't have a good sized pan, so I split it in to two loaf pans.  This worked out great, as one pan fed five people small-to-medium servings.

This is a little heavy for most every day cooking for us, but was very tasty and definitely goes in the keeper file!
