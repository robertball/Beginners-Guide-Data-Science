A few pro-tips that will have you snacking on them in about 2 hours, instead of the next day: you CAN use just one cup of oil, and have it work just fine. Use a 2 1/2 gallon ziplock bag for mixing. Probably the simplest and cheapest gussied-up snack out there.  Here are a few tips for this otherwise tasty recipe:  3T cayenne flakes is more heat than I like; 1T is not enough.  Three different grocery stores did not have whole-GRAIN saltines, so I substituted whole-WHEAT. (A few years ago, I found whole grain crackers in grocery stores, but not yesterday.)  The whole wheat crackers I bought were kind of crumbly.  Next time I'll just use regular saltines.  I will also mix the seasonings with the oil in a 4-cup glass measure and drizzle that onto the crackers after the crackers are in the bag - just smooshing and flipping the cracker bag does not mix the oil among the crackers well.  The crackers need the overnight hours in the bag to absorb the oil.
