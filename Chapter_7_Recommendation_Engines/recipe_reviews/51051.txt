I was looking for something different to do with ground beef other than meatloaf and pasta, and this was it. I cooked the bacon in a large frying skillet, adding brown sugar to both sides, then drained of grease on paper towels and diced up into small pieces and set aside. I drained the bacon grease and browned the ground beef in the same pan. I seasoned the beef with garlic powder, onion powder, chili powder, paprika, worcestershire, soy sauce, a dash of ranch salad dressing, a dash of Frank's  Red Hot sauce, and a dash of Kickin' Cajun jalapeno hot sauce. I used one can of Ranch Style beans, because that's all I had, and I mixed them up in a small bowl with about 1/2 cup Jack Daniels Hickory Brown Sugar bbq sauce, 1/3 cup ketchup, 2 tbsp honey, 2 tbsp brown sugar, 1 tsp liquid smoke, garlic powder, onion powder and chili powder. I poured the beans over the browned ground beef, covered the skillet and simmered on low for about 30 minutes (mostly because I was waiting for my husband to get home from work). When he got home, I tossed in the brown sugar "candied" bacon because I didn't want it to get soggy. Served over corn bread instead of biscuits. My husband liked it a lot, and so did I. Delicious, simple, and a break from the same old same ground beef! =)
