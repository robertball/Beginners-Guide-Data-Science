This is one of the best recipe and easy as well! I had this dish a whole meal by adding potatoes and making a few alterations. My SO couldn't get enough of this dish.
After I boiled the sausage for 20 minutes, I set it aside and added 5 chopped potatoes to the boil in the saucepan until they were only slightly tender. I drained and began the sauce,  adding about a tablespoon of ketchup to the mix. Once that came to a boil, I added the potatoes to simmer for 10 minutes. I added the sausage to a deep baking dish, poured the potato/sauce mixture over and topped that a few seasonings: garlic powder, salt, and pepper. Then comes a layer of a half cup of shredded American cheese (the good deli stuff, don't go cheap on the cheese) and a sprinkle of fresh basil and parsley (mostly for added prettiness).  Bake and watch it disappear!
On a final note, some homemade bread would be the perfect finish to this meal, as the sauce makes an irresistably great dip.

TL:DR  Add potatoes, cheese, seasonings, and homemade bread.

I used UNCOOKED sausage, NOT smoked or precooked. I removed the casing. Loose meat made into balls works best. I slice up my sausage and let it cook in it's own juices for about 15 mins just to brown and cook half way. It'll finish cooking in the sauce. I omit the water but keep the drippings as the base to add all else to. I simmer covered on the stove, stirring occasionally, keeping it at a low boil, for another 30 min or until it thickens to my liking.  Broccoli is a nice add in at the end. I served over mashed potatoes, white rice or wide egg noodles.
For a slightly less 'sweet' and 'richer' flavor, cut brown sugar to 1/4 cup and add 1/4 dark molasses. This is how I usually make it.
Just an old Southern way taught to me by my mom.
