So I will start by saying I have never made Beef Vegetable Soup before and honestly, I haven't eaten it much either. So I can into this not sure what it was really supposed to taste like. At my house, there are 6 so I always add extra meat. I started by using 2lbs of 80% ground beef and 1 large chopped onion and about 1 TBS of minced garlic with a bit of olive oil in my Ninja slow cooker on stove top setting. Browned all the meat, drain some of the grease but not all. I find that just gives soups a meaty flavor. 2 cans of diced tomatoes with juice in(because I hate stewed) I added 6 cups of beef broth, 2 bags of frozen veggies, 2 can of tomato sauces and 1 can of 8 oz tomato paste because I think I added a bit too much beef broth to tomato ratio (my family ended up liking it exactly like that so I will do that again) I also threw in a few beef bouillon cubes because I did not have onion mix and because I wanted a very beefy and tomato-ey flavor. For spices, I did add a bit more garlic powder because I think I went light on the minced garlic, sugar (doubled for the recipe), a dash of red pepper, a good bit of dried parsley( maybe a couple of TBS), some salt and black pepper to taste, and a TBS of Worcestershire sauce. I let it sit on high slow cook for 4 hours. At first I was a bit worried about the soup but over the time it really blossomed in a very hearty rich tasting soup. So I am sorry for completely changing the recipe, but I would not have had any idea where to start!
