Deeeeelicious.  My neighbor gave us fresh figs off his tree and I searched All Recipes to find a recipe to use them.  I struck gold with this!! I read all the reviews and decided to try 2 versions. 1 with just goat cheese, the other with a combo goat/ricotta.  Stick with just goat! The best. Also I REALLY struggled with cooking the bacon all the way through without demolishing the figs in the cooking process. So I tried pre-cooking the bacon in the micro first, but when wrapped around the figs and broiled the bacon ended up burning. The best solution was to just make a little pile of proscuitto on top of the figs!! No wrapping, no toothpicks, lower fat and not greasy, but very similar taste!  I can't wait until next year's fig crop is ripe... and neither can my neighbor!  He loved 'em as much as we did!!

I made it with dried figs (both California Golden and Black Mission) sliced in half, goat cheese and pecan halves.  I want to direct my comment to baking time. Several people comment that it took much longer than 5 minutes.  Maybe that is because the bacon was thick, the oven was not hot enough or they were too far from the flame.
I did a test run in my toaster oven and that did take longer, but on the top shelf of my regular oven they would have burnt had I not rotated them often.  I made 24.  I rotated after 1.5 min, then 45 sec twice and then 30 sec two more times and they came out chrisp.  The other issue I had was with the grease buildup in the baking sheet. I had to drain it twice and plan on using a broiling pan next time. The goat cheese got overpowered, maybe because you can't get much between dried figs, so I plan on using a stronger  flavored cheese next time.  They are delicious and were gobbled up quickly. Thanks for the recipe.
