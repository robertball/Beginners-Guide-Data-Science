Title:
Multi-grain Sour Dough Bread Recipe
Image:
https://images.media-allrecipes.com/images/79590.png
Ingredients:
3/4 cup warm water
1/4 tsp. yeast
1 3/4 cup whole wheat flour
1 cup warm water
1/2 tsp. yeast
1/4 tsp. salt
1/2 cup buckwheat groats
1/2 cup old fashioned oats
1/2 cup rye flour
3/4 cup* whole wheat flour
*More whole wheat flour may be needed in the kneading process, but don't add more than 1/4 cup.

Directions:
Combine 3/4 cup water, 1/4 tsp. yeast and 1 3/4 cup whole wheat flour.  Cover, let sit at least 8 hours, or up to 1 day.
Pour 1 cup water onto the starter in the bowl, mix.  Add the 1/2 tsp. yeast, mix.
Add salt,buckwheat groats, oats, and rye flour; combine.
Stir in 3/4 cup whole wheat flour.  Knead on a surface sprinkled with whole wheat flour.  It might still be a little sticky.
Place in greased bowl. Cover, let rise about 2 hours, until doubled in bulk.  Punch down.
Cover, let rise until about doubled, about 1 hour.
Turn onto parchment paper that has been sprinkled with cornmeal, cover with plastic wrap.  When doubled, in about 45 minutes, place in preheated 400 oven.
Spritz with water every 2 minutes for a total of 10 minutes.  Continue baking another 40 minutes, until it knocks hollow.

Description: 
We've been working on reducing or eliminating sweeteners while increasing whole grain contents of our breads. This bread is crunchy on the outside, chewy on the inside and has a variety of whole grain goodness!
