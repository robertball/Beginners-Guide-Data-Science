Title:
Irish Lamb Stew with Roasted Root Veg Recipe
Image:
https://images.media-allrecipes.com/userphotos/250x250/1072838.jpg
Ingredients:
1 ½ - 2 lbs roasted lamb, cubed
flour, enough to coat lamb
2 tbsp olive oil
2 ½ cups leeks, thinly sliced
1 tbsp olive oil
1 can (540ml) stewed tomatoes, drained
1 ½ L beef stock
2 cups leftover gravy (from lamb roast)
1 can (440mL) Guinness
3 large carrots, peeled and diced (1 inch pieces)
3 parsnips, peeled and diced (1 inch pieces)
1 4” turnip, peeled and diced (1 inch pieces)
2 large russet potatoes, peeled and diced (1 inch pieces)
4  5 garlic cloves, halved
¼ cup olive oil
¼ cup balsamic vinegar
1 tsp ground rosemary
1 tsp coarse sea salt
1 tsp pepper
3 generous tbsp regular Bisto
1 generous tbsp. cornstarch

Directions:
Preheat oven to 400F.
Heat 2 tbsp oil in large pot over medium high heat.
Coat lamb with flour.
Add lamb and cook until sides of cubes start turning golden. Remove from pot.
Add 1 tbsp oil.
Add leeks and cook until transparent and some pieces are golden in colour.
Add lamb to leeks. Stirring together.
Cook for a couple minutes.
Add tomatoes, broth and gravy (if using). Stir to combine.
Reduce heat to low, cover and simmer for 1 – 1 ½ hours (until roasted veggies are done).
Meanwhile, place carrots, parsnips, turnips, potatoes, garlic in glass baking dish. Coat with olive oil and balsamic vinegar.
Sprinkle with rosemary, salt and pepper. Stir until evenly coated.
Place in oven and cook for 1 – 1 ½ hours, until veggies are soft and starting to brown. Stirring every 20 – 30 minutes.
Once veggies are done, add to lamb mixture being careful as contents of both hot.
Bring stew to a boil.
Mix Bisto with approx. ½ cup boiling water and about 1 cup of liquid from pot. Blend until smooth then add to stew and stir.
Mix cornstarch with about 2 – 3 tbsp cold water.
Add to stew and stir.
Enjoy!

Submitter: 
Shantelle
Description: 
Great way to use up roast lamb leftovers. The meat becomes SO tender after being simmered for so long. My Irish husband ate 3 HUGE bowls of this the first time I made it. He said it was better than his grandmas back at home in Ireland.
