Title:
Traditional British Fish 'n Chips Recipe
Image:
https://images.media-allrecipes.com/images/79590.png
Ingredients:
: - Tartar sauce - :
1 cup mayonnaise
1 tablespoon dill pickle relish
1 tablespoon minced onion
1 tablespoons lemon juice
salt and pepper to taste
: - Chips - :
4 large potatoes, peeled and cut into strips
: - Fish - :
1 3/4 cup all-purpose flour
1 teaspoon baking powder
1 teaspoon salt
1 teaspoon ground black pepper
1 cup milk
1 cup Newcastle Brown Ale
1 egg
1 quart vegetable oil for frying
1 1/2 pounds cod fillets
1 lemon cut into wedges

Directions:
Tartar sauce: In a small bowl, mix together mayonnaise, dill pickle relish, and minced onion. Stir in lemon juice. Season to taste with salt and pepper. Refrigerate for at least 1 hour before serving.
Fish: In a separate medium-size mixing bowl, mix together flour, baking powder, salt, and pepper. Stir in the milk and egg; stir until the mixture is smooth. Let mixture stand for 20 minutes (continued below:)
Chips: Place potatoes in a medium-size bowl of cold water. Preheat the oil in a large pot or electric skillet to 350 degrees F. Fry the potatoes in the hot oil until they are tender. Drain them on paper towels.
Fish: (continued from above:) Dredge the fish in the batter, one piece at a time, and place them in the hot oil. Fry until the fish is golden brown. If necessary, increase the heat to maintain the 350 degrees F temperature. Drain well on paper towels.
Plating: Serve one lemon wedge on each plate.

Submitter: 
Michael St. Laurent
Description: 
A family recipe passed down from my oh-so English Grandmother.
