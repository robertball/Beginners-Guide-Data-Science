Title:
Ryan's Famous Buttermilk Southern Fried Chicken Recipe
Image:
https://images.media-allrecipes.com/userphotos/250x250/422712.jpg
Ingredients:
Brine:
12c water
1/2 cup kosher salt
8 bay leaves
1/2 teaspoon baking soda
1/3 cup clover honey
15 unpeeled garlic cloves
1.5 Tbsp whole black peppercorns
3 large rosemary sprigs
1 bunch thyme
2 Tbsp cayenne Pepper
1 bunch parsley
1 Tbsp finely grated lemon peel
1/4 c fresh lemon juice
2 3 pound chickens (preferably free range/run) fryers
Frying:
3 cups flour
1/4 cup panko (Japanese breadcrumbs)
2.5 Tbsp garlic powder
2.5 Tbsp onion powder
2 teaspoons smoked Spanish paprika
3 teaspoons cayenne
1/4 teaspoon ground bay leaves
1/4 teaspoon dried marjoram
1/2 teaspoon freshly ground black pepper
2.5 teaspoon kosher salt
3 cups buttermilk
3 cups peanut oil
3 cups lard
Garnish
2 teaspoons finely chopped parsley
1 teaspoon finely chopped garlic
10 dill pickle slices

Directions:
Brining:
Mix all brine ingredients except chicken in a stockpot.
Boil for 1 minute.
Allow to cool completely and refrigerate until cold. Rinse Chicken  and submerge in brine.
Allow chicken to bring at least 12 hours in the fridge.
Remove chicken from brine.
Cut each chicken into 8 pieces and dry well with a dish towel.
Discard brine.
Frying:
Line a big baking sheet with parchment paper.
Mix all frying ingredients except peanut oil and buttermilk in a large bowl.
Place buttermilk in a big glass bowl.
Place chicken in flour then in buttermilk then back in flour.Be sure to keep one hand dry and one wet. Dust off excess flour and place on baking sheet.
Let chicken sit on baking sheet in fridge for 2 hours.
Place peanut oil and lard in heavy cast iron pan so that when chicken goes in it would come up about 1/2 way.
Heat oil to 320 degrees. Use a thermometer if you need.
Cook dark meat first (legs and thighs) for about 12-13 minutes turning once.
Cook breasts for about 6 to 7 minutes.
Drain in colander with paper towel.
Sprinkle with sea salt and serve with a sprinkle of parsley, chopped garlic and sliced dill pickle.
