Title:
Chalupa - An Americanized version Recipe
Image:
https://images.media-allrecipes.com/userphotos/250x250/731565.jpg
Ingredients:
8oz ground beef Or sliced beef
4oz cheddar cheese, grated
1 cup of lettuce thinly sliced
4 oz of sour cream
4 oz of salsa (Fresh Or Goya Salsa Pico de Gallo)
4 flour tortillas
4 Oz re-fried beans
-
FOR THE RED SAUCE
-
1/4 Teaspoon Cumin
1/8 Teaspoon of Pepper
2 Cups Tomatoes
1 Jalapeno Pepper
5 Cloves of Garlic
1/4 Teaspoon of Oregano
1/4 Cup of Chicken Stock
1/4 teaspoon salt
1 onion, chopped

Directions:
In A Pan Cook Red Sauce (10-15 min), Cumin, Pepper, Tomatoes, Jalapeno Pepper, Garlic, Oregano, Chicken Stock, salt, onion. Put In Blender And Puree, Or Use An Immersion blender, Strain.
as your cooking the sauce brown ground beef in a frying pan, drain Excess oil
on high heat add red sauce to ground beef, and cook down, stirring till sauce is thickened and cooked into the hamburger.
Using A taco Forming tool deep fry tortilla for about 30 seconds, for crisper Chalupa fry tortilla for 1 to 1 1/2 mins. You can also fry the tortilla in a shallow fry pan with oil using tongs, holding the tortilla open as the tortilla cook, but deep fried seems to allow them to puff up more.
heat re-fried beans in a small sauce pan on low or microwave, Remember to stir to prevent burning.
Take tortilla fill with 1 oz of re-fried beans, add hamburger, add Lettuce, top with Salsa, cheese, and sour cream.
Beef Chalupa, Done.

Submitter: 
Mark Farmer
Description: 
An Americanized version of chalupas are sold in Taco Bell restaurants, filled with ground meat, steak or chicken, and topped with cheese, lettuce, sour cream and salsa. This is my version of an Americanized version of chalupas
