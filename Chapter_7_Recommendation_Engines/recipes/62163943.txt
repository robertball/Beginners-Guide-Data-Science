Title:
Huli Huli Pineapple Chicken Recipe
Image:
https://images.media-allrecipes.com/userphotos/250x250/813358.jpg
Ingredients:
3 broken fryers, split or quartered
1/3 cup ketchup
1/2 cup shoyu (Japanese soy sauce) (or use any soy sauce)
3/4 cup brown sugar
1/3 cup red wine or sherry
20 oz can crushed pineapple with juice or 20 oz of fresh shredded pinepple
1 knuckle fresh ginger (finely minced or grated)
1 garlic clove, minced
1/4 cup Worcestershire sauce
4-8 drops liquid smoke (optional)

Directions:
Mix all ingredients except chicken in a pot and set to medium high to full boil.  Stir occasionally
Reduce heat to medium low and simmer for 30 minutes to reduce mixture.  Stir occasionally
Reserve 1 1/2 cup sauce for basting.
Place chicken in large bowl or plastic bag and marinade in remaining sauce 4-24 hrs.
Set marinated chicken on grill to medium high heat and cook 5 minutes each side.
Reduce heat to low and baste often with remaining sauce.
Chicken is done when it reaches internal temperature of 165 Deg F.
Make sure you "turn-turn" the chicken.

Submitter: 
Mark Bender
Description: 
Hawaiian BBQ commonly found at roadside fundraisers. This recipe uses crushed pineapple and brown sugar for caramelized texture and wonderful flavor.  And, if you are wondering, huli is the Hawaiian term for turn.
