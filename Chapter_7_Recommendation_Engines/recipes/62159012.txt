Title:
Blintz Souffle Recipe
Image:
https://images.media-allrecipes.com/images/79590.png
Ingredients:
dough-
1 1/2 sticks sweet butter- room temperature
1/3 cup sugar
2 teaspoons baking powder
1 cup flour
1/8 teaspoon salt
1 teaspoon cinnamon
8 oz container cottage cheese
1/4 cup sour cream
5 eggs (room temp)
1/2 tablespoon vanilla
1/4 cup heavy cream
filling
8 oz package cream cheese, room temp
1/3 cup sugar
16 oz container cottage cheese
2 eggs
1/2 tablespoon vanilla
1/4 cup sour cream
strawberry sauce
1/4 cup orange juice
3 tablespoon cornstarch
3/4 cup water
1/8 teaspoon cinnamon
1/2 cup sugar
1 pound strawberries, cleaned well, hulled, cut 1/4" pieces

Directions:
Butter a 9X13 inch pan
preheat oven to 350
In a large mixing bowl, cream butter and sugar.
In a small bowl, combine dry ingredients: baking powder, flour, salt, cinnamon.
In a medium bowl, beat wet ingredients- cottage cheese, sour cream, eggs, vanilla and cream.
Into the butter mix, add dry and wet ingredients alternately.
Beat until well combined.
divide in two equal parts and set aside.
For filling, in large mixing bowl, beat cream cheese on high speed until fluffy. Add remaining filling ingredients and beat at medium speed for 5 minutes.
Put half the dough on the bottom of pan, put filling on top of that and then the other half of dough on top. It doesn't matter if its even or totally covered.
Bake for 50-60 minutes until golden brown.
Turn off oven and leave door open for five minutes
Remove.
Serve with strawberry sauce or jelly or cinnamon/sugar mix.
strawberry sauce
warm orange juice in a small pan.
sprinkle in cornstarch and whisk vigorously.
Heat water to hot but not boiling.
Add to juice and whisk vigorously, getting rid of lumps.
Turn off heat, add cinnamon and sugar.
whisk until blended.
Add strawberries
Cover pot and simmer for 15 minutes.
Stir every 5 minutes- sauce should be thicker than syrup.

Submitter: 
SANDI
Description: 
A wonderful dairy meal! I've used the store bought blintzes and made the souffle that way. I like this where it's made as a casserole...I added blueberries to the strawberries the last time- YUM!
