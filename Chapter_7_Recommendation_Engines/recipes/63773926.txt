Title:
Bananas Foster Recipe
Image:
https://images.media-allrecipes.com/images/79590.png
Ingredients:
1/4 c  (56 g) butter
1 c (210 g) brown sugar
1/2 t cinnamon
1/4 c banana liqueur
4 bananas, halved length wise, then cross wise
1/4 c dark rum

Directions:
Combine butter, sugar, and cinnamon in skillet or flambe pan over low heat
Cook, stirring until the sugar dissolves
Stir in banana liqueur, then add banana pieces to the pan
When banana sections soften and begin to brown, carefully add rum
Continue to cook until rum is hot, then tip the pan to ignite the rum, or use lighter
When the flame subsides, carefully lift banana pieces out, and use as topping for ice cream, french toast, or other dish
Liberally spoon remaining sauce over bananas and topped dish

Submitter: 
DWCarlson
Description: 
This is the actual Bananas Foster recipe from the original source and creator of this dessert: Brennan's Restaurant. In 1951, Chef Paul Blange created Bananas Foster. The scrumptious dessert was named for Richard Foster, who served with the restaurant's founder, Owen Brennan, on the New Orleans Crime Commission. Richard Foster was a frequent customer of Brennan's and a very good friend of Owen.
