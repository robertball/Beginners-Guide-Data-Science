Title:
KOBBARI ANNAM (COCONUT RICE) Recipe
Image:
https://images.media-allrecipes.com/images/79590.png
Ingredients:
3 cups cooked plain rice (basmati or sona masoori)
2 tablespoons ghee
2 tablespoons coconut oil
4 green chillis, slit in halves
¼ cup cashewnuts
1 cup fresh grated coconut
Salt to taste
2 tablespoons roasted sesame seeds (optional)
For tadka/popu/tempering:
3 dry red chillis
1 tablespoons chana dal (split Bengal gram)
2 tablespoons urad dal (skinned split black lentils)
1 teaspoon mustard seeds (rai, aavalu)
1 teaspoon cumin seeds (jeera, jeelakarra)
¼ teaspoon asafoetida (hing) powder
2 sprigs curry leaves (kadipatta, karavepa aaku)

Directions:
Heat oil and ghee in a large frying pan. Fry the cashewnuts till golden brown. Remove with a slotted spoon and set aside.
For tempering, add the red chillis, chana dal, urad dal, mustard seeds, and cumin seeds to the frying pan. When seeds splutter, add the asafoetida and curry leaves. Add the green chillis and coconut. Fry for 1 minute.  Add rice and salt to taste. Mix well and cook till rice is heated through.
Garnish with cashewnuts and roasted sesame seed powder. Serve warm or at room temperature.

Submitter: 
SUSMITA
Description: 
Kobbari annam is a classic south Indian dish which makes for a quick lunch or light dinner. It is also a great way to use up leftover rice.
