Title:
Meemaw's Jambalaya Recipe
Image:
https://images.media-allrecipes.com/images/79590.png
Ingredients:
Seasoning mix:
2 bay leaves
1 tsp salt
1 tsp gumbo file
1/2 tsp black pepper
1/2 tsp white pepper
1/2 tsp dry mustard
1/2 tsp cayenne (more or less to taste)
1/2 tsp chili powder
1/2 tsp cumin
1/2 tsp thyme leaves
1/4 tsp ground cloves
Jambalaya:
3 tbsp butter
2 cups chopped onion
1 cup chopped green bell pepper
1 cup chopped celery
1 tbsp minced garlic
2 tbsp minced parsley
1 cup finely chopped tasso ham
1 pound lean pork, cut into 1 inch cubes
6 smoked andouille sausages
1 1/2 cups uncooked long grain white rice
1 (16 oz) can whole tomatoes
3 cups chicken stock
1 lbs seafood (shrimp, cleaned crawfish tails, oysters, whatever looks good that day)

Directions:
Make seasoning mix - grind the bay leaves and mix with the remaining spices and herbs
In a large heavy pot, melt the butter.
Add onions, bell pepper, celery, garlic, Tasso and pork and brown over low heat, stirring constantly for 15 minutes.
Add sausage and seasoning mix, mix thoroughly. Continue cooking for 20 minutes, stirring constantly.
Add rice and raise heat to medium and cook for 5 minutes, toasting rice.
Add tomatoes and stock and mix gently. Raise heat to high and bring to a boil and cook, uncovered, 5 minutes.
Cover and turn to low and let cook about 30 minutes. About 5 minutes before serving, nestle the shrimp into the hot rice and cover, allowing the shrimp to cook but not overcook.

Submitter: 
MomZilla (Evin)
Description: 
My grandmother was an Irishwoman who married a Cajun. After living on the bayou for a few months she discovered Cajun cooking is way tastier than Irish cooking. This is her jambalaya!
