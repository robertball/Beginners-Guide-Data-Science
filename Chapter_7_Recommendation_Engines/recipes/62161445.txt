Title:
Mexican Sauteed Noodles Recipe
Image:
https://images.media-allrecipes.com/images/79590.png
Ingredients:
1 fresh mild green chile (also called California or Anaheim chile) or 1/3 C chopped canned green chile
8 oz. Fideos (coiled thin noodles) or vermicelli
4 or 5 Tbs. Vegetable oil
1/3 C minced onion
2 medium garlic cloves, minced
1 lb. Ripe tomatoes, peeled, seeded, chopped, or 1 (28oz) can whole plum tomatoes, drained, chopped
1 tsp. Dried leaf oregeno, crumbled
About 1.5 C unsalted chicken stock or broth homemade or packaged
Salt & freshly ground pepper to taste
Toppings, opposite
4 Tbs. Chopped cilantro (fresh coriander) leaves or fresh parsley leaves

Directions:
If using a fresh chilé, discard seeds & ribs. Chop finely & set aside. Break noodles in 2 or 3-inch pieces. Heat 4 Tbs. Oil in a large skillet over medium heat. Add noodles & Saúte, stirring constantly & turning over from time to time, until golden, about 7 minutes. Transfer to a bowl with tongs or a slotted spoon. (A few noodles may remain in skillet) Add 1 Tbs oil if skillet is dry & heat over low heat. Add onion & chilé. Cook, stirring often, about 5 minutes or until onion softens. Add garlic, tomatoes & oregano; stir well & bring to a boil. Add pasta & stir. Reduce heat to low, cover & simmer, stirring occasionally, about 12 minutes, or until liquid is absorbed & pasta is tender, add a few Tbs. Stock or water.
Meanwhile, prepare toppings. Add 3 Tbs. Cilantro or parsley to pasta. Taste & adjust seasoning. Sprinkle with remaining 1 Tbs. Cilantro or parsley & serve in a heated bowl with toppings if desired.

Submitter: 
Kris10 in CA
Description: 
Extremely easy to prepare & kids love it. It's mild & full of flavor, makes a great side dish or main.
