Title:
Poblano Chicken Enchilada Casserole Recipe
Image:
https://images.media-allrecipes.com/images/79590.png
Ingredients:
Cooking spray
4 boneless/skinless chicken thighs, chopped
1/3 cup chopped fresh cilantro
2 cups frozen corn kernels, thawed
1/3 cup (3 ounces) 1/3-less-fat cream cheese, softened
1 can refried beans (reserve 1/2 of can for sauce)
1/2 teaspoon chili powder
1/2 teaspoon ground cumin
Texmex grinder spice mixture
1/4 teaspoon kosher salt
1/4 teaspoon black pepper
1 1/2 cups chopped onion (reserve 1-2 tbsp for the enchilada sauce)
4 garlic cloves, minced
1 poblano pepper chopped
2 tablespoons diced jalapeño pepper
12 (6-inch) corn tortillas
1 cup shredded sharp cheddar or Colby jack cheese
Black olives, sliced
3 green onions, sliced
Sauce
1 cup canned tomato sauce
1 cup salsa verde
1/2 can refried beans
1-2 tbsp minced onion
Salt, pepper, texmex grinder, & cumin
1 tbsp minced cilantro

Directions:
Preheat oven to 425°.
Heat a large skillet over medium-high heat. Coat pan with cooking spray. Add onions, garlic, poblano pepper, and 1 tbsp of the minced jalepenos and cook until softened. Place in a medium bowl. Coat pan again with cooking spray and add chicken to pan; sauté until cooked through. Add onion pepper mixture, 1 1/2 tablespoons cilantro, corn, 1/2 can of refried beans, cumin, texmex grinder, chili powder, salt, pepper, and cream cheese toss to combine.
Combine remaining 1-2 tbsp onion, salsa, tomato sauce, 1/2 can of refried beans, and spices in a medium saucepan over medium-high heat; bring to a simmer for 5 minutes, stirring occasionally. Remove from heat and add 1 tbsp of cilantro.
Heat a large skillet over medium-high heat after spraying with oil. Add 3 tortillas; cook 1 1/2 minutes on each side. Remove tortillas from pan; repeat procedure with remaining tortillas.
Spread 1/2 cup salsa mixture in the bottom of an 9x11-inch glass or ceramic baking dish coated with cooking spray. Arrange tortillas over salsa mixture. Spoon half of chicken mixture over tortillas. Repeat layers, ending with tortillas. Pour remaining salsa mixture over tortillas; sprinkle evenly with cheddar cheese. Bake at 425° for 15 minutes or until bubbly and lightly browned. Top with remaining cilantro, olives, green onions, and remaining tablespoon of jalapeño.
