Ingredients:
2 tablespoons olive oil
1 medium white onion, diced
1 yellow bell pepper, diced
5 cloves garlic, crushed
1/2 teaspoon salt
1/2 teaspoon fresh ground black pepper
4 teaspoons chili powder
1/4 teaspoon chipotle powder
1 1/2 teaspoons cumin
1 1/2 teaspoons oregano
6 cups vegetable broth
1 4oz. can Hatch green chiles, undrained
1 pound dry black beans, rinsed
garlic salt to taste
Garnish:
1 cup prepared pico de gallo
Lime wedges
Bell pepper slices

Directions:
According to manufactures instructions set cooker to saute. Add the oil and once shimmering, add the onion and cook, stirring, until translucent and soft, about 5 minutes.
Add the garlic, salt, pepper, chili powder, chipotle powder, cumin, and oregano ,stirring, until fragrant, about 1 minute. Add the beans and 6 cups of vegetable broth.
According to the manufacturer's guide, lock the lid and prepare to cook. Set to pressure cook on high for 40 minutes. Allow 10-15 minutes for pressure to build. After cooking time is complete allow for natural pressure release (about 25 minutes).
Unlock the lid and use an immersion blender to blend the black beans with until thick and creamy.  If you want texture to your bean soup remove 1 cup of beans with a slotted spoon and replace after blending.
Season with garlic salt to taste prior to serving.

Submitter: 
bd.weld
Description: 
Cut time by not having to soak the beans when using an electric pressure cooker. Adjust the spices to your liking in this healthy, easy to make soup that freezes well.
