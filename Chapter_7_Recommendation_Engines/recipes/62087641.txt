Title:
Salt Pork Portuguese Bread Recipe
Image:
https://images.media-allrecipes.com/images/79590.png
Ingredients:
--Dry Mix Ingredients:
4 cups bread flour
2 cups white corn
1 tbsp. salt
--Wet Mix Ingredients:
2 cups water (120 degrees)
3 packets of dry active yeast
2 tbsp. honey (sugar may be substituted)
2-3 pinches of dry herbs
--Miscellaneous Ingredients:
1 ½ cups cubed cheese (white cheese work the best)
Seasoned breadcrumbs
1 “brick” salt pork
Grated parmesan cheese

Directions:
Mix yeast, honey, and dry herbs of your choice (oregano, basil, rosemary, etc) in 1 cup of tepid water (Wet Mix)
Let stand for about 10 minutes until yeast has fully activated.
Mix Dry Mix Ingredients in a stand alone bowl or mixer (Dry Mix).
Slowly work in Wet Mix into the Dry Mix.
Slowly add the rest of the tepid water until dough is formed properly.
Fold in the cubed cheese.
Form the dough into a ball and slit the top.
Cover dough with plastic and let rise in a warm area for about 1 hour or until it has doubled in size.
Oil a large 1” deep pan and coat the bottom with the breadcrumbs.
Place risen dough in pan and gently flatten dough to fill the entire pan.
Slice the salt pork into ¼ inch thick slices and press evenly over dough.
Let dough rise again for 1-2 hours or until it doubles in size again.
Punch down any large air bubbles in the process.
Spread grated parmesan on top of risen dough
Bake for 12-20 minutes in a preheated 350 degree oven (place a glass bowl of water in the oven to prevent cracking).
Dough is done when thumping it sounds hollow.

Submitter: 
RoRoCo
Description: 
This bread has Portuguese roots, but I have added a bit of flair to it.  This is a great mid-meal bread.
