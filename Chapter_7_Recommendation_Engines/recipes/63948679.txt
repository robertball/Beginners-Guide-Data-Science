Title:
South of the Border Venison Skillet Recipe
Image:
https://images.media-allrecipes.com/images/79590.png
Ingredients:
2 tablespoons butter or margarine
1 pound ground venison
2 teaspoons minced garlic
1 onion, chopped
2 tablespoons butter or margarine
1 1/2 c. uncooked white rice
1 tsp. chili poweder
1 tsp. ground cumin
1 tsp. paprika
1/2 tsp. chipotle chili powder
1/4 tsp. cayenne pepper, if desired
1 diced jalepeno, if desired
1 14 oz. can beef broth
1 (14.5 ounce) can petite diced tomatoes
1 (10 oz.) can Rotel Tomatoes and chilies
1/2 cup salsa
1 (15.5 ounce) can black beans, rinsed and drained
1 (15.5 ounce) can sweet corn, drained

Directions:
Melt butter in a large skillet over medium-high heat. Add venison and cook until no longer pink, stirring to break up. Saute garlic and onion, until the onion starts to soften, then add venison and cook till no longer pink.
Meanwhile, melt remaining 2 tablespoons butter in a saucepan over medium heat. Stir in rice and spices, and cook until lightly golden, about 5 minutes. Add cooked venison mixture, beef broth, tomatoes, salsa, and black beans; bring to a boil. Reduce heat to medium-low and simmer for 15 minutes. Stir in corn, and continue cooking until the rice is tender, about 5 minutes.
