Title:
Bullseye Ham and Bean Soup Recipe
Image:
https://images.media-allrecipes.com/images/79590.png
Ingredients:
2 20 oz. packages soup bean mix, soaked overnight
4-5 cups ham stock leftover from baked ham
(stock is best if removed from ham immediately after baking, then cool overnight in fridge, remove fat from top before using.)
6 quarts water
1 leftover ham bone with meat attached (1-2lb.)
6 cups cubed ham
2 med. onions  finely chopped
8 carrots, sliced
5 stalks celery, chopped
1 tsp. garlic powder
1 tsp. paprika
3 Tbsp. Worcestershire sauce
1 Tbsp. Chili powder
4 bay leaves
2 Tbsp. parsley
4 Tbsp. Lemon juice
3 tsp. sea salt
2 14.5 oz. cans garlic/basil diced tomatoes
4-5 cups low sodium chicken broth.

Directions:
Soak beans overnight according to package directions. then rinse.
In a large stock pot, combine soaked beans, ham bone, and water. Bring to a rolling boil, then reduce heat and simmer about 30 minutes, or until beans are soft.
Remove ham bone from pot. Remove 1/3 beans and puree in blender. Return back to pot. Add ham stock, ham, vegetables and seasonings, tomatoes, and chicken broth. Simmer on very low heat for 3 hours. Stirring occasionally.
Remove bay leaves before serving. Goes well with corn bread.

Submitter: 
Megan
Description: 
This soup is a great hit with my family, even for my 12 yr. old daughter who doesn't care for ham. An old fashioned recipe with a few extra tastes of my own added. This recipe goes a long way. Easy to freeze for later servings.
