Title:
Greek Stuffed Tomatoes and Peppers (Yemista) Recipe
Image:
https://images.media-allrecipes.com/userphotos/250x250/880964.jpg
Ingredients:
8 large garden fresh tomatoes
4 large bell peppers
2 lbs of ground chuck
1 medium onion, diced
1/2 stick butter
1 tablespoon seasoned salt
1 tablespoon black pepper
2 tablespoons soy sauce
1-1/2 cups Uncle Ben's converted rice
1/2 cup water
1/2 cup olive oil

Directions:
Cut top of tomatoes to make a lid, but leave attached at one end. Scoop out insides into large bowl, squeeze out juices by hand, and set aside.
Cut tops of peppers completely, and reserve for later. Clean out and discard insides of peppers.
Place tomatoes and peppers in 17x11 pan and set aside.
In large frying pan, sautee' onion and garlic in butter. Add ground chuck, seasoned with seasoning salt, pepper and soy sauce.
Add squeezed tomatoes to browned meat. Add water and let simmer 15 minutes. Add rice, let come to a boil and remove from heat.
With soup ladle, stuff tomatoes first with meat and rice mixture, and place lid side down in pan. Stuff peppers, replace lids and lay sideways in pan. Pour olive oil over tomatoes and peppers and season with salt and black pepper.
Bake in 375 degree oven for 1 hour. Turn peppers after first half hour. Let rest 30 minutes before serving.

Submitter: 
Denise Phillips Sarigianopoulo
Description: 
This is my favorite! The best time to make it is in August when the garden tomatoes are ready for harvest. Hothouse tomatoes are just not the same. My mom made this every year, and when she did, I thought I died and went to heaven. Enjoy!
