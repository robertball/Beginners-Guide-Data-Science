Title:
Spicy Southwestern Egg Casserole Recipe
Image:
https://images.media-allrecipes.com/images/79590.png
Ingredients:
10 eggs
1/2 cup all-purpose flour
1 teaspoon baking powder
1/8 teaspoon salt
1/2-1 tsp Cajun seasoning
1  cup of fajita chicken-diced
1/2 cup diced onion
2 cups shredded Monterey Jack cheese
2 cups shredded sharp Cheddar
2 cups sour cream
1/3 cup butter or margarine, melted
1-2 (4 ounce) cans chopped green chilies
Salsa, fresh diced tomatoes, avacado, or other garnishes

Directions:
In a large bowl, lightly beat eggs, set aside. Combine flour, baking powder, cajun seasoning and salt; stir into eggs (batter will be lumpy). Add chicken, onion, cheeses, butter and chiles. Stir unitl blended. Pour into a greased 13-in. x 9-in. x 2-in. baking dish. Bake, uncovered, at 400 degrees F for 20 minutes.  Then reduce heat to 350 degrees and continue baking for 35-40 minutes or until a knife inserted near the center comes out clean. Let stand 5 minutes before cutting. Serve with salsa and/or other garnishes if desired.

Submitter: 
Jacque Keller
Description: 
This is a really tasty and easy egg dish for dinner--or brunch.  My family likes to change things up every now and then and have an egg dish for dinner...this one really works for us!Modified from: Southwestern Egg Casserole
