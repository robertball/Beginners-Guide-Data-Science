Title:
Pickled  Beets, a colorful addition to any meal. Recipe
Image:
https://images.media-allrecipes.com/userphotos/250x250/4561404.jpg
Ingredients:
3 pounds of beets with 1 inch tops intact.
Water to cover.
BRINE:
2 cups white vinegar
2 cups water
1 cup white sugar
1 teaspoon tablesalt.

Directions:
Cook beets in water in a large pot until tender.
Cool in cooking water until you can comfortably put
your hands in the water.
Slide off beet skins.
Leave tiny beets whole and cut bigger ones in chunks to
get them all the same size.
Put in sterilized jars within 1 inch from top.
BRINE:
Combine vinegar, water, sugar and salt in a saucepan.
Heat on medium and stir often until it reaches a boil.
Pour over beets in jars to within  1/4 inch from top and seal.
After you put the lids on leave on counter until you hear the lids seal.
After they are sealed put in a cool place, like the basement.
They keep a long time in a cool place, we are just finishing last years beets.
TO STERILIZE THE JARS:
Wash in hot soapy water and rinse really well.
Put jars on a cookies sheet along with lids in a 250 degree oven for 1/2 hour.
Be careful taking them out as they are very hot.
Cool before filling.
This can be doubled or tripled  if you have lots of beets.
Try to keep the beets the same size so they pickle evenly.
This recipe yields 3 pints.

Submitter: 
manella
Description: 
This recipe was given to me  many years ago by a neighbour who taught me how to do beets  and other pickles. There are no spices added to this, they have a nice tang to them. The recipe can be doubled which I always do.
A very colourful addition to your plate.
