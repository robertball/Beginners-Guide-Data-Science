Title:
Beef Tinaktak Recipe
Image:
https://images.media-allrecipes.com/userphotos/250x250/3694347.jpg
Ingredients:
1 lb. lean ground beef (cooked separately and drain excess oil)
2 tbsp. olive oil
1 medium onion, diced
3 cloves garlic, minced
1 cup cherry tomatoes, cut in half
1 cup green onion, cut into 1.5-2-inch pieces
1/2 tsp. black pepper
1 beef bouillon cube (optional)
1 cup fresh green beans, cut into 1.5-2-inch pieces
8 oz. can sliced water chestnuts, cut further in half
1 13.5 or 14 oz. can coconut milk
4 tbsp. soy sauce (lite or regular Kikkoman brand preferred), divided
1 lemon, cut in half
Donne’ sali (or any hot pepper you prefer), optional

Directions:
1.  Cook beef, drain, then put on paper towel to soak any excess oil.
2.  At medium high heat in standard cooking pan, add 2 tbsp. cooking oil then sauté onions, garlic, green onions, tomatoes, 3 tbsp. soy sauce, black pepper and squeeze in half a lemon.  You may opt to drop in a beef bouillon cube at this point (optional). Stir and cook for about 2-3 minutes.
3.  Add green beans and water chestnuts and cook for 8-10 minutes or until green beans are nice and tender.
4.  Add drained ground beef, 1 more tbsp. of soy sauce and the other half of lemon.  Slowly stir in the coconut milk  and then add donne’ sali or any hot pepper if desired (optional).  Reduce the heat and simmer on low for 2-3 minutes or until the coconut milk is heated through.  Don't boil.
5.  Turn off the heat and serve over hot rice (steamed white or brown) and ENJOY!

Submitter: 
Sheila Baker Shedd
Description: 
Tinaktak (pronounced as tee-nack-tack) may have originated from the sound of chopping meat: tak! tak! tak!  It is one of Guam's favorite comfort food and definitely a favorite of mine since it is so simple to make yet yields a tasty dish!
