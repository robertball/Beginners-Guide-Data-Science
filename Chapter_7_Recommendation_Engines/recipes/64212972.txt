Title:
Joan's Cabbage Patch Soup Recipe
Image:
https://images.media-allrecipes.com/images/79590.png
Ingredients:
1 tablespoon olive oil
a few shakes dark sesame oil
2 large onions
1 1/2 pound ground beef
5 ribs celery
1 can kidney beans, not drained
28 ounces stewed tomatoes, blended until no big chunks remain
2 cups beef broth or water with 2 bouillon cubes
15 ounces tomato sauce
1 cup frozen corn
1/2 medium head of cabbage
1/4 teaspoon chili powder
1/8 teaspoon ground pepper

Directions:
Chop all vegetables.  Heat oils in heavy kettle, add chopped onion & saut√©, stirring often, until it begins to turn brown (caramelize).
Add chopped celery and cook until it starts to look translucent.
Add ground beef, and brown until no pink remains.
Remove from heat and pour off fat.
Add remaining ingredients and cook at low heat until cabbage is soft.
Refrigerate overnight.  Skin off fat, and reheat to serve.

Submitter: 
Yomama
Description: 
I got this recipe from my dear friend Joan, several years ago.  I have made a few changes, but it is very close to her original recipe.  I love to serve it with homemade bread - biscuits, focaccia, whatever you like.
