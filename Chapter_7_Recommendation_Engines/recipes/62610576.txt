Title:
As Slow As It Gets Chili Recipe
Image:
https://images.media-allrecipes.com/userphotos/250x250/565846.jpg
Ingredients:
1 tablespoon vegetable oil
1-pound lean ground beef or veal
2-3 pound sirloin tip roast chopped into bite size pieces
1 1/2 cups tequila
1 teaspoon Jamaican scotch bonnet sauce
3 medium onions  chopped
3 Jalapeno peppers  chopped
1 Hungarian or Italian hot pepper
1-teaspoon kosher salt
3.5 tablespoons chili powder
3 cloves garlic -- minced
2 bay leaves
2 teaspoon Worcestershire sauce
2 teaspoon dried oregano
2 teaspoon cumin
2 teaspoon hot Hungarian paprika
1 ½ cans no salt added tomato sauce
1 can tomato paste
1 can dark red kidney beans drained
1 can standard white beans
1 bottle/can of dark beer

Directions:
Place sirloin roast pieces in large container & marinate in tequila & teaspoon of scotch bonnet sauce for a minimum of 8 hours (ideally 18 hours or more)
Remove sirloin roast pieces from liquid, and discard liquid
In a skillet, heat vegetable oil on medium, brown sirloin roast pieces with one chopped jalapeno pepper, one chopped onion and one minced clove of garlic and place in slow cooker, in same skillet break up ground beef with fork, add one chopped jalapeno pepper, one chopped onion and one minced clove of garlic and cook until lightly browned.
Pour off excess fat and add to slow cooker
Cut slit in Hungarian or Italian hot pepper and heat in skillet
While pepper is heating add remaining ingredients to slow cooker
Make well in mixture and insert heated pepper into the mix
Pour beer into chili and stir to mix ONLY.
Cover and cook on low for a minimum of 16 hours (ideally 20 hours!!)
Turn up to high for two hour before serving, then back to low to serve.

Submitter: 
Brian Slowleigh
Description: 
I got tired of the various chili recipes out there.  I toyed with many different ones, and came across this one almost by accident.  The longer it cooks, the better it tastes.  It takes a LONG time, but I've discovered it's worth it!
