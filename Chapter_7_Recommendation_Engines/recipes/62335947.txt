Title:
Roganjosh Recipe
Image:
https://images.media-allrecipes.com/images/79590.png
Ingredients:
*** Meat mixture:
- 2 Tablespoons ghee (clarified butter)
- 675 gms (1 1/2 pounds) mutton, cut into 2.5 cm (1 inch) cubes
- 4 bay leaves
*** Spice mixture:
- 2 brown cardamoms
- 6 cloves
- 6 black peppercorns
- 1 teaspoon black cumin seeds
- 2 cinnamon stick pieces, each around 2.5 cms (1 inch)
*** Vegetable mixture:
- 2 Tablespoons ghee (clarified butter)
- 1 onion, chopped
- 4 green cardamoms
- 4 Tablespoons tomato purée (tomato paste)
- 140 ml (5 fluid ounces) plan natural yoghurt
*** Paste mixture:
- 1 onion, chopped
- 6 cloves garlic
- 1 Tablespoon coriander seeds, roasted
- 1 ounce (28 gms) fresh root ginger (that's about 1 1/2 inches - 4 cms), peeled (with a teaspoon!) and grated or very finely chopped
- 1 teaspoon red chilli powder
- 1 1/2 teaspoons turmeric powder
*** For the final phase:
- 600 ml (2 1/2 cups) hot water
- 1 Tablespoon salt
- 1 teaspoon garam masala, as garnish

Directions:
In a saucepan, melt the ghee, then add both the meat and the spice mixtures and cook gently for 15 minutes. Remove all the contents to a plate and reserve.
Then cook the vegetables in the same pan, by melting the fresh ghee first.
(See next step as well!) Fry the onions and the cardamom together until the onions are golden, at which point stir in the tomato purée, mixing well.
While the onions are frying, mix up the paste by grinding all the paste ingredients together until quite smooth.
Add the paste to the vegetable mixture and cook, stirring continuously) for 5 minutes.
Add the yoghurt and again cook, stirring continuously, for another 5 minutes.
Finally, add back the reserved meat and its juices, then add the salt and the water.
Bring to a boil, reduce the heat and gently simmer for 2 1/2 hours, or until the meat is tender.
Serve, sprinkled with the garam masala, with rice and/or chapattis and a nice cooling Raita.

Description: 
Held to be the epitome of Kashmiri cuisine, the master chefs of the royal Moghul kitchens boasted about perfecting this dish. I take pride in presenting to you my version as taught to me in the kitchens of a 5-star hotel in India.
