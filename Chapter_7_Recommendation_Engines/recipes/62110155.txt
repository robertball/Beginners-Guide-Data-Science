Title:
Mexican stuffed steaks Recipe
Image:
https://images.media-allrecipes.com/images/79590.png
Ingredients:
1 pound boneless round steak, pounded to 1/4 inch thin
1/2 tsp. salt
1 tsp. dried oregano
1/2 tsp. black pepper
4 oz. thinly sliced cooked ham
1 large tomato, finely chopped
1 (4oz) can mild chopped green chilies, drained
1 medium onion, finely chopped
1 clove garlic, minced or 1 tsp. garlic powder
1/4 cup dry bread crumbs
1 medium carrot, jullienned
2 TB vegetable oil
3/4 cup water (1-1/4 cup if cooking in pressure cooker)
1 tsp. white wine vinegar
1 tsp. Worcestershire sauce
1 bay leaf

Directions:
Sprinkle beef with salt, oregano and pepper.
Arrange ham slices evenly on top of beef.
Sprinkle tomatoes, chilies, onion, garlic, carrots and bread crumbs on top of ham.
Carefully roll up jelly-roll style and tie with string.
Heat oil in Dutch oven or pressure cooker and brown meat on all sides.
Add water, vinegar, Worcestershire sauce and bay leaf to meat.
Cover and cook for approximately 1-1/2 hours or until tender in a 325 degree oven or in a pressure cooker at 15 pounds for 30 minutes.

Submitter: 
COOKINGQUEEN75
Description: 
These rolled steaks are so tender and flavorful.  I like the way the green chilies give it zing and I love the taste of oregano. They can be prepared in the oven or I like to fix them in my pressure cooker.  This is extremely good with rice and a nice green salad.
