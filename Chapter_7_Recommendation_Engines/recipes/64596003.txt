Title:
Chicken Chop Suey Recipe
Image:
https://images.media-allrecipes.com/userphotos/250x250/3866341.jpg
Ingredients:
1/2 pound boneless skinless chicken breast sliced into bite size pieces
1 Tbsp. oyster sauce
1 Tsp. soya sauce
1 Tbsp. corn starch
1/2 Tsp. Sesame Oil
1/2 pound fresh bean sprouts, rinsed and drained
1 stalk of celery sliced on the length finely then cut into 2 inch pieces
1/2 onion sliced finely
1/2 small red pepper sliced finely and cut into 2 inch pieces
1/2 cup chicken broth
1 Tbsp. corn starch
2 Tbsp. water
1/2 Tsp. Soya Sauce
2 Tbsp. peanut oil
1/2 large clove of garlic slivered

Directions:
About an hour before cooking place cut up chicken into a bowl, mix in next four ingredients (oyster sauce, soya sauce, corn starch and sesame oil).
Place in refrigerator to blend flavors.
Mix 1 Tbsp. corn starch, 2 Tbsp. water and 1/2 Tsp. Soya Sauce in a small bowl and set aside.
When ready to cook, heat frying oil over medium/high heat in non-stick wok
Add slivered garlic to oil and infuse oil until garlic is browned...then remove garlic
Stir-fry celery, red pepper and onion for 2 minutes
Remove vegetables to bowl
Stir-fry chicken until just cooked and lightly browned
Add bean sprouts and stir-fry for another two minutes stirring constantly
Add Vegetables back into pan along with chicken broth, stir-frying for another 3 minutes ensuring vegetables remain crisp.
Stir in cornstarch/water/soya sauce mixture until the juices become slightly thickened.
Serve immediately.

Submitter: 
NB Roy
Description: 
A dish my mother used to make albeit with canned vegetables.  Fresh veges takes this recipe up several notches.  She would have loved it.  I serve it over rice or with fried rice.
