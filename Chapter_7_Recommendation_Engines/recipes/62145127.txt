Title:
Deep South  Eggnog Cake Recipe
Image:
https://images.media-allrecipes.com/images/79590.png
Ingredients:
1/2 cup butter, softened
1-1/4 cup sugar
3 eggs
1/4 tsp finely grated lemon peel
1 tsp vanilla extract
2 cups all-purpose flour
2 tsp baking powder
1 tsp salt
*1 cup eggnog
2 Tb bourbon or dark rum
Frosting:
1/4 cup all-purpose flour
1/4 tsp salt
*1-1/2 cups eggnog
1 cup butter, softened
1-1/2 cups sugar
1-1/2 tsp vanilla extract
dash rum extract
1/8 tsp finely grated lemon peel
1/2 cup finely chopped, toasted pecans (optional)
*If you don't have any eggnog, you can make it for this recipe. Whisk two eggs in the blender. Add 2 Tb sugar, 1-1/2 cups milk, 1 cup heavy cream, and a dash each of vanilla and rum extracts. Blend until smooth.

Directions:
CAKE:
Grease and flour two 9" round baking pans.
In a large mixing bowl, cream butter and sugar until light and fluffy.
Add eggs, one at a time, beating well after each addition.
Add extract and lemon peel, mixing well.
Combine flour, baking powder and salt.
Add to creamed mixture alternately with the eggnog.
Stir in bourbon or rum.
Bake at 350 degrees for 30-35 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted in the center of comes out clean. Be sure to test both layers before removing from the oven.
Allow to cool in pans for 10 minutes.
Turn out onto a rack to finish cooling completely.
FROSTING:
Combine flour and salt in a saucepan.
Gradually stir in eggnog and whisk until smooth.
Bring to a boil over medium heat, whisking for 2 minutes until thickened.
Remove from heat and cool completely to room temperature.
In a large mixing bowl, cream butter and sugar until light and fluffy.
Add eggnog mixture, extracts and lemon peel. Beat on high speed until fluffy.
If desired, remove 1/4 cup to decorate your finished cake. May be tinted with food color.
Spread plain frosting between layers and over top and sides of the cake.
Decorate the sides with small handfuls of toasted pecans, if desired.
Store in fridge until serving time.

Description: 
You can really taste the eggnog in this lovely, rich, moist  cake. No eggnog? No problem. You can make enough for the recipe in a jiffy. Nothing says southern hospitality like this impressive cake.
