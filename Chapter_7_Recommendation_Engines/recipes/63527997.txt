Title:
Gooey Cinnamon Rolls - Rapid Rise Recipe
Image:
https://images.media-allrecipes.com/images/79590.png
Ingredients:
4 1/2 cups all-purpose flour, or more if needed
1/3 cup sugar
2 tsp Rapid Rise yeast (or 1 packet)
1 teaspoons salt
1 1/4 Cup Milk (or 1/2 cup buttermilk and 3/4 cup milk)
1/3 cup butter
1/3 cup mashed potato flakes
1 egg
'
Filling:
1/3 cup room temp. butter
1/3 cup white sugar
1/3 cup light brown sugar
2 tsp. Cinnamon
1/8 tsp. Nutmeg
'
Icing:
Duncan Hines Classic Vanilla or the following:
2 cups powdered sugar
5 tablespoons butter OR margarine, softened
2 tablespoons cream cheese
2 to 3 tablespoons heavy cream
1 teaspoon Vanilla Extract

Directions:
In large bowl, combine 1/2 cup flour, sugar, un-dissolved yeast, and salt.
In a small sauce pan heat milk, and butter until very warm (120o to 130oF) stirring often.
Stir potato flakes into liquid and let soften 1 minute.
Stir liquid into dry ingredients and stir until combined.
Stir in 1 egg and enough remaining flour to make a soft dough.
Knead on lightly floured surface until smooth and elastic (about 4 to 6 minutes), OR knead with electric mixer using dough hook (5 minutes on 2nd lowest setting using Classic KitchenAid).
Cover with damp paper towel; let rest on floured or non stick surface 10 minutes.
While dough is resting, mix filling ingredients.
Roll on a lightly floured surface to a 12 x 18-inch rectangle.
Spread with butter sugar mixture. Roll up loosely lengthwise, sealing edges.
Cut into 12 slices with a sharp serrated knife using a seesaw motion. Place in greased 13 x 9-inch pan. Proof in a warm area. (To proof in an over; pre-heat oven to 200, half fill a glass ovenproof dish with boiling water. Turn off oven. Place pan of rolls in the preheated oven along with the dish of boiling water. Close oven door. Remove rolls and water from oven before increasing heat to 350.)
Let rise 30 to 45 minutes until nearly doubled.
Bake in preheated 350 degrees F oven for 25 minutes.
Cool for 5 minutes and spread with butter if desired. Combine icing ingredients and drizzle over rolls. If using purchased icing thin first with a couple of tablespoons of milk.
