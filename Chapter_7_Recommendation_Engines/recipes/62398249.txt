Title:
Miso Marinated Pork Tenderloin with cantaloupe and cucumber slaw Recipe
Image:
https://images.media-allrecipes.com/images/79590.png
Ingredients:
1/4 cup Mirin
1/4 cup dry white wine
2 garlic cloves minced
1/2 teaspoon minced ginger
2 teaspoons chile paste
1/2 cup miso paste (white or red)
1/3 cup sugar
2 pork tenderloins trimmed of silver skin(2 lbs)
1/2 cantaloupe
1 english cucumber
2 shallots
1/4 of a small napa cabbage
1/2 bunch cilantro
2 limes juiced
1 Tablespoon honey
1/2 serrano chile seeded
3 Tablespoons olive oil

Directions:
For the pork:
In a saucepan bring the Mirin, white wine, garlic, ginger and chile paste to a boil.  Add miso and stir to combine then add sugar and cook till dissolved.  Pour into a glass dish to cool.  Add the pork and turn to coat.  Refridgerate overnight or at least 4 hours.  Heat oven to 400 degrees.  Add pork to wire rack over a sheet pan and cook for about 25 mins or 150 degrees internal.  Remove and loosely cover for 10 mins.
For the slaw:
Remove the seeds and rind from the cantaloupe.  Peel the cucumber and slice in half the length of the cucumber and remove the seeds.  Thinly slice the cantaloupe, cucumber, shallots and cabbage then add to a bowl.  Roughly chop the cilantro and add to the bowl.  Add lime juice, honey, serrano and oil to a blender and puree-set aside.
To Plate:  Toss slaw with the dressing and mound in the center of a plate.  Slice the pork and arrange  around the slaw.  Enjoy!

Submitter: 
Ken from CA
Description: 
This is an easy dish that people will think you slaved over.  Marinate overnight for best results, but works great with as little as 4 hours in the marinade.
