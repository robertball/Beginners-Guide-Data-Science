Title:
Chicken Corn Pie Recipe
Image:
https://images.media-allrecipes.com/images/79590.png
Ingredients:
1-1/2 c. diced, peeled potatoes
1-1/2 c. cooked, cubed chicken
2 Tbsp. bacon bits (optional)
1/3 c. milk
2 eggs
2 c. fresh corn, cut from cob (or frozen, thawed)
1 tsp. sugar
1/2 tsp. salt
1/2 tsp. black pepper
1/4 tsp. garlic powder
1/4 tsp. onion powder
1 pkg refrigerated pie crusts (for 2 crust pie)

Directions:
Preheat oven to 375 deg F.
Set out refrigerated pastry and bring to room temperature (about 15 min)
Put potatoes in small saucepan and cover with water.  Bring to a boil then reduce heat, cover and simmer for 5-6 min, until tender.
Meanwhile, in blender, combine milk, eggs, corn, sugar and spices and blend until relatively smooth (about 20 seconds).
Drain potatoes, mix in chicken and bacon bits, set aside.
Follow directions for 2-crust pie shell on package, placing potato/meat mixture into bottom crust.
Pour blended corn mixture on top of potatoes and chicken (shell will be pretty full).
Cover with second crust, pinch together and flute edges (see pkg direcions), cut about 4-6 slits in top to vent.
Place pie dish onto baking sheet and cook at 375 deg F for 35-40 minutes, or until crust is golden brown.
Serve (warm or cold) with favorite side salad.

Submitter: 
Teri Carrillo-Fox
Description: 
This is a terrific way to use fresh corn from the cob when it becomes available in the Spring and Summer.  It's even great cold and transports well for BBQ's and get-togethers.  Omit the meat and you've got a great side-dish.
