Title:
Four-Bean Turkey Chili Recipe
Image:
https://images.media-allrecipes.com/userphotos/250x250/324502.jpg
Ingredients:
1 lb ground turkey
1 Tbsp canola oil
1 can or bottle of beer
2 Tbsp chili powder
1 tsp ground cumin
salt and pepper to taste
1 medium onion, diced
2 cups chopped peppers (sweet, hot, or mixed)
1 16 oz can tomato sauce
1 28 oz can crushed tomatoes
1 28 oz can diced tomatoes
1 15 oz can chili beans with hot sauce
1 15 oz can kidney beans, drained
1 15 oz can black beans, drained
1 15 oz can pinto beans, drained
Sour cream, shredded cheddar cheese, and tortilla chips for garnish

Directions:
In an 8 quart pot heat canola oil over medium high heat, tilting pan to allow oil to cover bottom of pan (you can add a little more if you need to.
Add turkey to pan and chop up with a wooden spoon. Add diced onion to pan. Stir often and until turkey is browned. Drain off any grease and return pan to stove.
Add chili powder and cumin to pan and stir to coat. Pour beer into pan and scrape bottom to deglaze.
Add peppers to pan, reduce heat to medium-low, cover, and cook 10 minutes to soften peppers.
Add tomatoes, tomato sauce, and beans. Rinse each can with an inch or so of water and add to pan. Simmer for an hour or longer to allow flavors to blend. Stir frequently and adjust seasoning adding salt and pepper to taste.
Garnish with your choice of toppings.

Submitter: 
Pam Ziegler Lutz
Description: 
This dish was created to use some garden peppers as well as clean out the pantry. My husband liked it better than my regular recipe. I hope you enjoy it, too!
