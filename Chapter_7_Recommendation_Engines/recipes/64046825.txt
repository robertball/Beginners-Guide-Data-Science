Title:
Sweet Sour Spareribs Hawaiian Style Recipe
Image:
https://images.media-allrecipes.com/images/79590.png
Ingredients:
4-5 lbs. Spareribs or mixed with pork butt; cube size
1 TBS. Sea Salt
8-1/4"slices of fresh ginger root
1 med/lg size pot filled halfway or just above meat
2 c. Flour
2 c. Canola Oil
1 med/lg size non-stick pot
Mixture: medium bowl
1 1/2 c. brown sugar
1 1/2 c. pineapple juice; reserve the pineapples
3/4 c. apple cider vinegar
3/4 c. water
1 1/2 tsp. ground ginger
2 cloves of garlic; minced
1 1/2 TBS. Soy Sauce - Kikoman
salt to taste
2 TBS Soy Sauce
1/2 c. cornstarch for thickening the gravy

Directions:
1. Fill pot halfway with water. Bring to boil. Add pork meat, Sea Salt and fresh ginger root slices. Bring to second boil, then on med/high, boil for an additional 45 minutes.
2. In medium bowl, add brown sugar, pineapple juice, cider vinegar, water, ground ginger, minced garlic and soy sauce. Mix till dissolved.  Set aside.
3. After meat is cooked, drain into calendar.  After five minutes, coat all pork meat with flour and begin frying to golden brown.  Continue frying till all pork meat are browned on the outside.  Throw all the meat back into the pot with drippings.  Bring heat up to high, throw sauce mixture in the pot and toss for about 10 minutes. Turn down to low-med.
4. Add little water to cornstarch in a cup, mix well and pour into the pot. Mix well. As it thickens, add pineapples and gently toss evenly.  Ready to serve. Enjoy!

Submitter: 
Gabriella's
Description: 
This dish is one of my favorites growing up in Hawaii.  There was always a pot of sweet sour spareribs for after school, everyone's favorite!
