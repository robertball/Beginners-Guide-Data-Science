Title:
Southwest Chicken Pot Pie Recipe
Image:
https://images.media-allrecipes.com/userphotos/250x250/506430.jpg
Ingredients:
3 refrigerated pie crusts
4 boneless, skinless chicken breast halves, cubed
4 chipotle peppers in adobo sauce, rinsed, seeded and chopped
2 cans corn
2 cans black beans
1/2 cup butter or margarine
1 medium onion, chopped
2 cloves garlic, minced
2/3 cup flour
2/3 cup milk (lowfat OK)
2/3 cup plain non-fat yogurt
4 tsp chicken base or 4 bouillon cubes
1 1/2 tsp cumin
1 Tablespoon fresh cilantro, chopped
1/2 tsp salt
1/2 tsp black pepper

Directions:
1. Preheat oven to 425. Lightly grease 9 x 13 baking dish. Spread two pie crusts in bottom of pan, stretching the dough to fit the pan. Trim away excess dough and save.
2. Place chicken in large dutch oven. Add enough water to cover the chicken. Add 3 chopped chipotle peppers. Bring to a boil. Boil until chicken is cooked through. Drain chicken, reserving 4 cups of the water. Mix chicken, beans, and corn.
3. Melt butter in pan. Add onions, garlic, and remaining chipotle pepper. Saut√© until onions become translucent.
4. Add flour and stir until mixture becomes thick. Whisk in water. Add milk, yogurt, chicken base, cumin, cilantro salt, and pepper. Cook and stir until thick.
5. Spread chicken/corn mixture in pan. Cover with sauce.  Stretch third crust over top. Trim away excess crust. Use scraps to cover gaps. Make a small slice in top center of crust.
6. Cover edges of crust with foil. Bake in 425 oven for 20 minutes. Remove foil and cook 15-20 more minutes or until crust is golden brown. Rub crust with butter and let stand for 10 minutes before serving.

Submitter: 
Lisa
Description: 
Classic Chicken Pot Pie with a southwestern twist. The chipotle peppers give it a smoky flavor with a little bit of heat. It's a little labor intensive but the result is worth it.
