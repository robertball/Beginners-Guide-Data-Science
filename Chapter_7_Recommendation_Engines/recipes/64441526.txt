Title:
Vegetarian Sweet Potato Lasagna Recipe
Image:
https://images.media-allrecipes.com/userphotos/250x250/2361361.jpg
Ingredients:
Cashew Ricotta:
3/4 cup raw cashews, soaked for 2-3 hours
1/4 cup water + additional water for soaking
1/2 tbsp lemon juice
1/2 tbsp nutritional yeast
dash of onion powder
dash of salt
dash of pepper
Lasagna Ingredients:
9 whole wheat lasagna noodles, uncooked
1 medium sweet potato, cooked with skins
1 medium carrot, finely chopped
1 cup yellow onion, diced
1 cup bell pepper, diced
water for sautéing (you may use oil instead)
1/2 tsp dried oregano
1/2 tsp dried basil
1/2 tsp onion powder
1/4 tsp garlic powder
1/2 cup frozen spinach, thawed, undrained
1-1.5 (26oz) jars tomato basil pasta sauce (or about 4 cups)
1-2 cup mozzarella cheese, shredded

Directions:
Cashew Ricotta:
Cover cashews with water and soak for 2-3 hours, at room temp.
After soaking drain cashews.
Combine cashews, 1/4 cup water, lemon juice, nutritional yeast, onion powder, salt & pepper in a blender.
Blend until smooth and resembles ricotta cheese.
Refrigerate for 1-2 hours or until firm.
Lasagna:
Preheat oven to 400 F.
Prepare a 6x10 (1.5 QT) dish with cooking spray, set aside.
In a blender or with a fork mash the sweet potato with skins, you may need to add about 1-2 tbsp water if using the blender.
Water sauté carrots for 5-7 minutes, add bell pepper and sauté an additional 5-7 minutes, adding more water if necessary.  Then add onion and sauté until tender.
Add the mashed sweet potato, 1/2 cup pasta sauce, and seasonings: oregano, basil, onion & garlic powder to the sauteed vegetables.
In a bowl combine spinach with prepared cashew ricotta.
Add about 1/2 cup of pasta sauce to the bottom of the dish.
Add 3 noodles to the bottom of the pan, top with 1/2 cup pasta sauce, 1/2 of the sweet potato mixture, then 1/2 of the cashew ricotta mixture, sprinkle a handful of cheese, top with about 1/2 cup pasta sauce.
Repeat one more time, starting with step 15: a layer of 3 noodles.
Place last layer of noodles on top.  Cover with remaining pasta sauce (about a cup).
Cover dish with foil bake 45 minutes, remove foil add remaining mozzarella and bake until bubbly, uncovered, about 15 minutes.

Submitter: 
laceyworks
Description: 
This is a great vegetarian lasagna!  I love that the noodles do not need to be pre-cooked!  I created this recipe when I was in a pinch and out of ricotta.  My husband loved it and says it tastes just like regular lasagna!
