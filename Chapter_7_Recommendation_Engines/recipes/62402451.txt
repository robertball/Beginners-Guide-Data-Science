Title:
Disappearing Chocolate Pie Recipe
Image:
https://images.media-allrecipes.com/images/79590.png
Ingredients:
1 9-inch pie crust
1 1/2 cups sugar plus 6 additional tablespoons
3 tablespoons unsweetened cocoa powder
1 1/2 cups milk
4 eggs, separated
2 teaspoons vanilla
1/4 cups unsalted butter
1/8 teaspoon cream of tartar

Directions:
Prepare  and bake 9-inch pie crust as directed.
Preheat oven to 350 degrees
Mix 1 1/2 cups sugar, flour, and cocoa in a heavy saucepan.  Stir in just enough milk to make a paste; stir in remaining milk.
Cook over medium heat, stirring 10 to 12 minutes or until thick.  Remove from heat.
Beat egg yolks until thick and yellow.  Carefully whisk in a little of the hot cocoa mixture to temper the eggs; then whisk mixture back into the cocoa mixture.  Bring back to a boil.
Remove from heat and add butter and vanilla.
Pour into pie shell.
Beat egg whites until stiff peaks forms adding sugar slowly, two tablespoons at a time.
Spread meringue over pie filling so it touches all around the edge.
Bake at 350 degrees for 12 to 15 minutes or until lightly browned.
Cool on a wire rack until completely cooled.

Submitter: 
Patricia Canerdy
Description: 
I have made this pie for as long as I can remember....tried many other recipies but always come back to this one.  I call it a disappearing pie because if there is any left after dinner it seems to disappear during the night.
