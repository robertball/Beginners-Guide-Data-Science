Title:
Judy's Not-so-traditional English Trifle Recipe
Image:
https://images.media-allrecipes.com/images/79590.png
Ingredients:
For the Pastry Cream:
8 egg yolks
1/2 cup sugar
3 Tablespoons cornstarch
2 1/2 cups milk
2 Tablespoons butter (not margarine)
1 teaspoon vanilla
2 cups heavy whipping cream, whipped
For the Raspberry Sauce:
1 12 oz package whole frozen red raspberries, not in sugar
1 cup sugar
For the trifle:
2 loaf size angel cakes
2 10 oz packages frozen sliced strawberries in juice, thawed
2 Tablespoons dry sherry
1/2 cup chopped pecans

Directions:
To make Pastry Cream:
In large heavy saucepan, combine egg yolks, 1/2 cup sugar, and cornstarch until smooth.  Add milk.
Cook and stir just until mixture comes to boiling.
Remove from heat.
Stir in butter and vanilla.
Cool, cover and chill for 2 hours.
Fold in freshly whipped cream.
To make Raspberry Sauce:
Heat raspberries over low heat until thawed.  Stir in sugar.
Cook uncovered over medium-low heat until berries thicken to consistency of egg whites.
Cool 30 minutes.
Sieve to remove seeds.  Refrigerate until serving time.
To layer trifle:
Cut first cake into 1 1/2 inch pieces.  Arrange pieces in bottom of trifle bowl.
Sprinkle 1 Tablespoon sherry over cake.
Spoon 1 package strawberries over cake to cover.
Repeat with second cake, sherry and strawberries.
Smooth pastry cream over dessert.
Cover and chill up to 24 hours.
Spoon into individual bowls to serve and top with raspberry sauce and chopped nuts.

Submitter: 
Dutchgirl
Description: 
This is a great dessert when you need to impress.  A friend gave me a similar recipe over 20 years ago.  I've tweaked it over the years to come up with this.  It takes some work but the end result is really delicious.
