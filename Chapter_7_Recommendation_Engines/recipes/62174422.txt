Title:
Pfefferneuse (Peppernuts) Recipe
Image:
https://images.media-allrecipes.com/images/79590.png
Ingredients:
2 1/4 c all-purpose flour
1/2 tsp salt
1/2 tsp freshly-ground black pepper
1/2 tsp crushed aniseeds
1/2 tsp ground cinnamon
1/4 tsp baking soda
1/4 tsp ground allspice
1/4 tsp ground nutmeg
1/4 tsp ground cloves
1/2 c unsalted butter, at room temp
3/4 c light brown sugar, firmly packed
1/4 c light molasses
1 egg
2 c confectioners sugar (aprox, for dusting)

Directions:
In a medium bowl, sift together flour, salt, pepper, aniseeds, cinnamon, baking soda, allspice, nutmeg, and cloves. Set aside.
In a large bowl, using an electric mixer set on medium speed, beat together the butter, brown sugar, and molasses until light and fluffy, about 4 mins. Beat in the egg. Reduce the mixer speed to low and beat in the flour mixture. Cover and refrigerate for several hours.
Position a rack in the middle of an oven and preheat to 350 F. Butter 2 baking sheets.
Scoop up pieces of the dough and roll between your palms into balls 1 1/2" in size. Place the balls 2" apart on baking sheet.
Bake until the cookies are golden brown on the bottom and firm tot he touch, about 14 mins. Transfer the baking sheets to racks and let the cookes cool slightly on the sheets.
Place the confectioners' sugar in a sturdy paper sack. Drop a few cookies into the sack, close the top securelyand shake gently to coat the warm cookies with the sugar. Transfer  to racks and let cool completely. Repeat with the ramining cookies.
Store in a airtight container at cool temps for up to 1 week.

Description: 
This German version of the traditional Northern European pepper nut cookies have been a family favorite at christmastime.Modified from: Pfefferneuse (Peppernuts)
