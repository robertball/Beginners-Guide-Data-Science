Title:
Chae's Sassy Corn Salad with Citrus Dressing Recipe
Image:
https://images.media-allrecipes.com/userphotos/250x250/504324.jpg
Ingredients:
4 packages of (frozen) whole kernel corn thawed and drained of excess water. *(can corn can be substituted)*
1/2 c. red bell pepper chopped
1/2 c. green bell pepper
1/2 c. yellow/orange bell pepper chopped
1/2 c. purple/red onion chopped
1 c. sliced black olives
1 cucumber - peeled, seeded and diced
3 med. roma tomatoes - seeded and diced
1 lg. jalepano pepper - seeded and diced
3 Tbsp. fresh cilantro rough chopped
1 c. smoked ham diced
Shrimp boil*
1/2 lb. Seasoned boiled shrimp, medium 15-20 count deveined.
1 package Zatarans Crawfish/shrimp boil
1/2 lemon and 1 Tsp Salt to taste
3-4 c. mixed salad greens (optional)
Citrus Dressing
1 1/2 c. fresh squeezed orange juice
2 Tbsp. Fresh Lime juice
3 Tbsp. Extra Virgin Olive Oil
1/2 Tsp. Kosher salt
1/2 Tsp. Fresh ground black pepper
3 Tbsp. Honey

Directions:
To boil shrimp - place 4 cups of water in pot and  bring to a rolling boil.
Add Zataran's seafood boil for crab/shrimp/crawfish and 1 Tsp. salt and 1/2 lemon wedge - return to boil
Add shrimp, let cook for no more than 10-12  minutes.
Remove from heat and let stand for 15 minutes. Discard the liquid and let shrimp cool in cold in ice water, this will make them easier to peel.
Peel and devein then set aside in refrigerator.
After corn has been drain in a collander proceed with tossing all the above ingredients for the salad together in a chilled salad bowl. Chill for about 1  hr or overnight if you like.
Whisk together the above ingredients for the dressing, make any necessary adjustments that might be needed (i.e more or less honey, depending on your taste)
Serve corn mixture on a bed of mixed salad greens, add citrus dressing and enjoy.

This salad can be eaten alone without the salad greens it's up to you. It's fresh colorful and delicious. It's simple to put together and it keeps in the refridgerator for at least three days tops....that is if you have any left over?...I never do. Bon appetite'!
