Title:
Recado de Pepian Rojo (Guatemalan “Pepian Rojo” Sauce) Recipe
Image:
https://images.media-allrecipes.com/images/79590.png
Ingredients:
6 Tomatoes
8 Tomatillos
1 Onion
2 Garlic cloves
2 Tablespoons of Sesame Seeds
2 Tablespoons of Pumpkin Seeds
1 thick slice of toasted Baguette
4 sprigs of Cilantro
1 teaspoon of Salt
2 Black Peppercorns
6 cups of Broth
4 tablespoons Olive Oil
1 Chayote Squash cut in eight pieces
4 Potatoes cut in thick slices
1 cup of whole fresh corn

Directions:
Place tomatoes, onion, and garlic on a hot dry skillet, and stir until slightly charred. Remove and place aside.
Place the pumpkin and sesame seeds in the skillet, cover, and stir until slightly browned. Remove and place inside a blender, and purée with the tomatoes, onion, garlic, toasted baguette, cilantro, salt, peppercorns and 3 cups of broth.
Strain sauce in a sieve and pour over a cooking pot with the oil on medium high heat. Let the sauce bubble for three minutes.
Add the chayote, potatoes, and corn to the sauce. Season sauce with salt, and add the rest of the broth. When the vegetables are cooked and soft you can add your choice of previously cooked chicken, pork, or beef. Cook for another 10 minutes and serve with white rice.

Submitter:

Description: 
Pepian is among the 4 national dishes of Guatemala. Pepian is hot, hearty, simple yet complex. In Guatemala, pepian has as many variations as there are cooks; Here is my recipe for a simpler version of this sauce.
