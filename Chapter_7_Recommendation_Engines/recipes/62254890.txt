Title:
Waikiki Sweet and Sour Pork Recipe
Image:
https://images.media-allrecipes.com/userphotos/250x250/334772.jpg
Ingredients:
Sweet and sour sauce
1 cup water
1/2 cup brown sugar
1/2 cup cider vinegar
reserved juice from canned pineapple
1 cup ketchup
2 tablespoons low sodium soy sauce (Kikkoman preferred)
1/2 teaspoon each,  granulated onion and garlic
1/4 cup water
2 tablespoons corn starch
-------
Pork
1 pound pork cut into 1" cubes  (pork roast or tenderloin)
2 eggs
2 tablespoons water
1 teaspoon sea salt
1/2 teaspoon black pepper
1 teaspoon granulated onion
1 teaspoon granulated garlic
1 1/2 cups flour
2 cups canola oil for deep frying  (amount approximate)
------
Vegetables
1 red bell pepper, cut into 1" pieces
1 green bell pepper, cut into 1" pieces
1 cup carrots, cut into 'coins'
1 large onion, cut into 1" cubes
1 can pineapple in juice,  drain and reserve juice
1 cup cherry tomatoes (optional)
1 - 2 cloves minced garlic
1/4 cup olive oil
1/2 teaspoon sea salt
1/2 teaspoon black pepper
-------
Steamed white rice

Directions:
Heat canola oil in  deep sauce pan over medium high heat.
Make and egg wash by whisking eggs and water in shallow pan.
Place flour, salt, pepper, onion and garlic in a large plastic bag and shake well to blend.
Moisten cubed pork in egg wash, then put into the flour mixture and shake well until each cube is well coated.
Gently place flour coated pork cubes into hot oil and fry for about five minutes or until browned and cooked through.
Set aside
Heat olive oil in a wide deep skillet over medium high heat
Stir fry peppers, onion and carrots with salt, pepper and garlic until veggies are crispy tender (do not over cook).
Stir in drained pineapple and cherry tomatoes.
Add pork and sauce, stir well and serve with rice.
