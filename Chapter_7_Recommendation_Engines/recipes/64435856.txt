Title:
Southwestern Style Lentil Salad for Lentil Sceptics Recipe
Image:
https://images.media-allrecipes.com/images/79590.png
Ingredients:
1/2 cup tri-colored quinoa
1 cup water (to cook quinoa)
1/2 cup dry whole lentils, soaked for an hour prior to cooking
3 cups water (to cook the lentils)
1 bay leaf
1/2 cup carrots, diced
1/2 cup cooked corn
1/2 cup bell pepper, diced
1 green onion, sliced into thin wheels
2 TBSP olive oil, divided
1/4 tsp red pepper flakes, optional
1/4 tsp ground cumin
1/4 tsp chili powder
2 TBSP fresh cilantro, chopped

Directions:
Rinse quinoa in a mesh strainer.
Cook the quinoa in 1 cup of water by bringing it to a boil for 1 minute then simmer for another 10-12 minutes or until the sprouts being to separate and the water is almost cooked away. Cover and set aside until water is absorbed. Fluff the quinoa and set aside.
Cook the lentils in 3 cups of water until just tender. About 20 minutes.
Drain the lentils and add to the quinoa, gently mixing the two until well combined.
Saut√© the bell pepper, onion and carrot in the oil until slightly tender, then add the corn and mix well with the vegetables until corn has warmed through.
Add the veggie mixture to the quinoa and lentils. Add crushed red pepper, cumin, chili powder, cilantro, and 1 Tbsp oil stirring together until all the ingredients are well coated.

Submitter: 
Buckwheat Queen
Description: 
I made this salad combining lentils and quinoa with a Southwestern flair for Bibi who says she never eats lentils. A hearty side dish or a main course for a light meal, this can be served warm or allowed to rest in the refrigerator and served cold.Full of flavour and color, this healthy salad will change lentil sceptics' minds for sure!
