Title:
Baja fish and cabbage Recipe
Image:
https://images.media-allrecipes.com/userphotos/250x250/6691654.jpg
Ingredients:
Pickled Red Onion:
1 large red onion, halved lengthways, thinly sliced
2 jalapeños, sliced
2/3 cup rice vinegar
1 tbsp lime juice (juice from half a lime)
1 tsp sea salt
Baja Cream:
1/2 cup mayonnaise
1/2 cup sour cream
2 tsp lime juice (2/3 juice from other half...)
1 tsp finely grated lime zest
Pinch of sea salt
Baja Cabbage Slaw:
2 tbsp mayonnaise
1 tsp lime juice (1/3 juice from other half)
2-4 shakes Green Tabasco sauce
1/2 small head green cabbage, thinly sliced
Sea salt and ground black pepper
Marinated Fish:
1/4 cup olive oil
1/2 tsp chili powder, or to taste
1 1/2 tsp dried oregano
1/2 tsp ground cumin
1/4 cup cilantro, chopped
1 jalapeño, seeded andchopped
1 lb Mahi-Mahi fillets
Sea salt and ground black pepper
1 tomato, diced for garnish

Directions:
Pickled Red Onion:
Place the onion and jalapeño in a heatproof medium bowl
In a small saucepan, combine the vinegar, lime juice and salt, and bring to the boil over high heat, stirring until the salt dissolves
Pour over the onion and jalapeño.
Allow to stand at room temperature for at least 1 hour before using. (Leftovers will keep up to 1 week in the fridge.)
Baja Cream:
Whisk mayonnaise, sour cream, lime juice, lime zest and salt in a small bowl
Refridgerate
Baja Cabbage Slaw:
In a bowl, mix together the mayonnaise, lime juice and Tabasco
Toss the cabbage with the mayonnaise mixture, season to taste and refrigerate.
Marinated Fish:
In a large bowl, mix olive oil, chili powder, oregano, cumin, coriander and jalapeño
Add the fish and marinate for 20 minutes
Heat a non-stick frying pan over medium–high heat. Remove the fish from the marinade, place in the hot pan and season with salt.
Cook the fish for 4 minutes, then turn over, season again with salt and cook for another 2 minutes or until just cooked through, depending on the thickness.
Serve fish with cabbage as side, and pickled onion as garnish
