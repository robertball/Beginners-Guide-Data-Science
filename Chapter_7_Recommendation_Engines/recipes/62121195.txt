Title:
Anytime Of The Year Marinated Fall Off The Bones BBQ Ribs or Brisket Recipe
Image:
https://images.media-allrecipes.com/images/79590.png
Ingredients:
5-8 pounds pork ribs with bones, rinsed, patted dry, and any membrane and excess fat removed or 5-8 pound beef brisket.
1 cup plain prepared yellow mustard
1 tablespoon worcestershire sauce
2 tablespoons Liquid Smoke seasoning
2 tablespoons paprika
3 tablespoons garlic powder
1 tablespoon onion powder
1 tablespoon celery salt
2 tablespoons packed dark brown sugar
2 tablespoons ground cinnamon
2 tablespoons ground cumin
1 tablespoon red chili powder
1 tablespoon dried oregano
1 tablespoon sea salt
1 tablespoon freshly ground black pepper
1/2 cup water
16 ounces of your favorite BBQ sauce, bottled or homemade

Directions:
After rinsing, patting dry, and removing any membrane or excess fat, place ribs/brisket in a shallow roasting pan.
Mix the mustard, worcestershire sauce, and liquid smoke with a fork or whisk.
Rub the mustard mixture into the ribs/brisket. Make sure to coat all over and evenly.
In a small bowl combine the paprika, garlic powder, onion powder, celery salt, brown sugar, cinnamon, cumin, chili powder, oregano, salt, and pepper.
Place the dry seasonings in an empty shaker.
Liberally coat all sides of the ribs/brisket with the seasoning mixture heavily, pressing it into the mustard.
Cover and let marinade in the refrigerator overnight or up to 2 days.
Preheat oven to 300 degrees F.
Add the water to the shallow roasting pan, cover and seal tightly with aluminum foil.
Slowly bake ribs/brisket for 2 1/2 hours.
Uncover and add BBQ sauce, and bake for 30 minutes, or until starting to brown/blacken slightly.

Submitter: 
JOYCE
Description: 
Ribs or Brisket that are marinated overnight in a scrumptous seasoning mixture, and baked or grilled/smoked to pefection, then glazed with YOUR favorite BBQ sauce. Serve up with some baked beans, cole slaw, and some wet wipes.
