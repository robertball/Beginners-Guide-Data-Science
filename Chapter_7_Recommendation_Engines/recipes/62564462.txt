Title:
Carnitas Enchiladas Recipe
Image:
https://images.media-allrecipes.com/images/79590.png
Ingredients:
2 lb	Pork shoulder (trimmed of fat)
1 T	Olive Oil
1 	Large Sweet Onion (sliced)
1	Red Bell Pepper (sliced)
1	Yellow Bell Pepper (sliced)
3	Garlic Cloves (minced)
6 oz	Pineapple with juice
½ 	Orange (juiced)
1	Lime (juiced)
2	Red Jalapenos (diced)
1	Green Jalapeno (seeded and diced)
2 c	Chicken Stock
¼ c	Hot Sauce
½ t	Kosher Salt
1 t	Pepper
10	Flour tortillas
2 c	Pepper Jack Cheese (shredded)
½ c	Ranchero Cheese (crumbled)
Green Chili Cream Sauce (recipe below)
Rub
2 T	Sweet Paprika
1 T	California Chili Powder
2 t	Kosher Salt
1 t	Pepper
1 t	Cumin
2 t	Garlic Powder
2 t	Mexican Oregano
1 T	Orange Zest
1 t	Lime Zest
½ t	Cayenne
Green Chili Sauce
2	Anaheim Peppers (roasted, seeded, de-skinned)
1	Poblano Pepper (roasted, seeded, de-skinned)
4 oz	Cream Cheese (softened)
¼ t	Cumin
½ t	Multi Color Pepper (fresh ground)
1 c	Chicken Stock
½ t	Garlic Powder
½ c	Onion (diced, sautéed)
¾ c	Sour Cream
½ 	Lime (juiced)
Kosher Salt to taste
Olive Oil

Directions:
Preheat a heavy bottom pan over high heat. Add the olive oil.
Cut the pork into 2 inch chunks and put into the rub, covering all of the pork.
Place the pork into preheated pan, and let sear. Flip and sear all sides, about 2 minutes each side.
Place all remaining ingredients, up to tortillas in a crock pot. Add pork, cover and cook on low 6 hours.
Shred the pork and cook 1 more hour.
Put 1 cup green chili sauce on bottom of a 9x12 dish.
Put the pork into flour tortillas, add jack cheese, roll up. Place in dish. Repeat with all tortillas.
Add remaining sauce on top, sprinkle with ranchero cheese.
Cover and bake at 350 for 20 minutes. Remove foil and bake 5 minutes more.
For the green chili sauce
Preheat the oven to broil on high.
Put the peppers on a baking sheet. Drizzle with olive oil. Sprinkle with salt and pepper.
Put the peppers under the broiler about 15-20 minutes, flipping once. Let them char all around.
Remove from oven and place peppers in a bowl immediately and cover with saran wrap for 20 minutes.
Peel off the skin, slice and remove most seeds.
Add to a blender.
In a pan, over medium heat, put a drizzle of olive oil. Add the onions and cook until translucent, about 8 minutes. Remove from heat and add to the blender with all remaining ingredients.
Blend all ingredients til smooth. Season with salt, add about half a teaspoon, gradually adding more to taste.

Description: 
These are delicious enchiladas that can be put in a crockpot before work and easily finished when you get home. A true crowd pleaser!
