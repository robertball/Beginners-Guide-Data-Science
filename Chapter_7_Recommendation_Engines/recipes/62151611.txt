Title:
Cajun Blackened Catfish Recipe
Image:
https://images.media-allrecipes.com/images/79590.png
Ingredients:
4 medium size catfish fillets (skinned)
1 tsp. black pepper
1 tsp. garlic powder
1 tsp. onion powder
1 tsp. paprika
1 tsp. parsley flakes
1 tsp. cayenne pepper
1 tsp. Kosher salt
1/2 tsp. dried oregano
1/2 tsp. dried thyme
1 1/2 sticks unsalted butter (NO margarine)

Directions:
This recipes is for cooking OUTSIDE only because of the Intense smoking.
Mix all of the dry ingredients in a small bowl
Coat the fillets evenly with the seasoning mix.
Place a larhge cast iron skilletr that has been seasoned on a Butane Cooker and heat the skillet until the center starts to turn white in the center of the skillet. Melt 1/2
a stick of butter in a metal bowl and set aside.
Add the remaining butter to the skillet (intense smooking will take place).Add the fillets to the skillet and cook for about 3 minutes per side. Check for doneness with a fork. Remove fillets from skillet and keep warm in oven before serving. Fillets will have a burned look. Pour the
balance of hot melted butter over the cooked fillets before
serving.

Submitter: 
Paul Schultz
Description: 
This is a recipe that I obtained from a very good Cajun Friend who is a native of Lafayette, Louisiana. His family 
adopted me (Friendly) many years ago and we have swapped Cajun and Creole recipes through the years.
