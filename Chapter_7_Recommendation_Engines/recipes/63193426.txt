Title:
Canadian Brie Mini-Pot Pie “Poppers” Recipe
Image:
https://images.media-allrecipes.com/userphotos/250x250/825933.jpg
Ingredients:
2 Tbsp flour
1 Tbsp butter
2/3 cup small dice leeks (use white part only)
1/2 cup white wine
1 cup chicken broth
2 small chicken breasts, boneless, skinless
1 celery stalks, chopped very small dice
1 carrot, chop very small
1/2 small onion, chop very small
pinch salt & pepper
1 tsp rosemary, chopped
1/2 cup Canadian brie, cut into small pieces
Make your own pie dough or buy pre-made dough

Directions:
In a skillet melt  butter, then add chicken and cook for about 5 minutes. Add chopped onions and leeks to the skillet and continue to cook until the chicken is no longer pink and the onion becomes translucent. Add the chopped carrots and celery to the skillet.
Sprinkle the flour over the chicken and vegetables and stir. Slowly add the white wine and chicken stock and season with salt and pepper while stirring.
Bring to a boil and simmer for about 15 minutes or until the carrots and celery are tender. While the chicken mixture is cooking, cut the dough circles for bottom and top, place pie bottom circles into mold (you can use mini muffin pan, place inside the chicken mixture and brie, lightly egg wash the pie top and place on top of mixture, pinch the edges with tip of pairing knife make a small vent in middle of top.
Pre-heat oven to 375 F and bake for about 15 to 20 minutes, until the pies are nice and golden brown.

Submitter: 
The Homestretch
Description: 
Liana Robberecht, Executive Chef of The Calgary Petroleum Club brings us these savoury chicken, leek and brie pot pie poppers. She says they pair wonderfully with a Chardonnay.
