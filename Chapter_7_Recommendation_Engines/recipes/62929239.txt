Title:
Grandma Rita's Chili Recipe
Image:
https://images.media-allrecipes.com/userphotos/250x250/716303.jpg
Ingredients:
1 can of pinto beans (16 oz.)
3 medium potatoes peeled and chopped (optional-can make fried potatoes instead)
1 can diced tomatoes blended (14.5 oz.)
1 cup sweet onions diced small
1 lb. ground beef
3 Pueblo green chilies (roasted, peeled, and chopped- more if you like it hotter)
2 cups of water or chicken stock if needed***	    2 Tbsp. of flour if needed***
1 tsp. garlic powder
1 tsp. onion powder
1-2 garlic clove minced
½ tsp. garlic salt
½ tsp. salt
½ tsp. cumin
¼ tsp. black pepper

Directions:
Peel and chop potatoes and  roasted green chilies.
Dice onions.
On medium to medium high heat, brown hamburger, onions in a large pan.
Add spices. Drain grease.
If you like thicker chili, thicken with 2 Tbsp. of flour.
Add potatoes, blended tomatoes, beans (do not drain,) and chilies to the pan.
Add 2 cups of chicken stock or 2 cups of water if mixture is too thick.
Bring to a boil. Simmer 20-30 minutes or until potatoes are fork tender.

Submitter: 
Lela
Description: 
My Grandma Rita's Chili is a favorite dish my family requests on a regular basis. The roasted Pueblo chilies add a spicy flavor. Grandma's unique twist on this recipe was adding potatoes. There is never any left over because it is just good old home cooking delicious.
