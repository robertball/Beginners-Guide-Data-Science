Title:
Sweat Mexican Paradise you'll be in love...... Recipe
Image:
https://images.media-allrecipes.com/userphotos/250x250/419244.jpg
Ingredients:
3 cups of water
3 to 4 Pilloncillos (if not available you can substitute 1 1/2 cups brown sugar)
3 to 4 oz raisins
4 bolillo rolls (found in Mexican markets) or 1 loaf french bread, cut into pieces
1 cup shredded cheddar cheese

Directions:
In a saucepan, bring water, sugar to a boil then reduce and simmer for 10 minutes.Place bread in a baking dish and sprinkle with raisins and half of the shredded cheese. Strain the syrup liquid, removing the cinnamon sticks, and pour the syrup over the bread until well absorbed. Top with remaining cheese and bake at 350 degrees. This dish may be served warm or cold and topped with fresh whipped cream or ice cream. Enjoy!

Description: 
This recipe was originated with Jews living in the New World, namely Mexico around the region of Monterrey,Nuevo Leon where a large contingent of Jews fleeing the Spanish Inquistion settled. Traditional this is prepared around the Lenten season or Passover. Very rich in flavors and you can not go wrong with this.
