Title:
Eggplant Thalippu Recipe
Image:
https://images.media-allrecipes.com/images/79590.png
Ingredients:
1 European style; OR 8 small oval; or 2 to 3 long thin oriental style eggplant
1 tbsp lemon juice
1 onion, chopped or minced, to taste
4 cloves garlic, minced
3 fresh green chilies, sliced
1 T high quality curry powder (NOT American brand)
½ tsp turmeric
1 tsp salt
5 tbsp gingelly oil (light sesame oil)
A sprig curry leaves (about 8 to 12 leaves)
½ tsp mustard seeds
1 lemon size ball of tamarind pulp
1 T milk
1 tsp cumin seeds
1 T sesame seeds
¼ tsp fenugreek seeds

Directions:
Cut the eggplant into wedges or rounds.
Toss with lemon juice
Thoroughly mix together the turmeric, salt and curry powder
Toss the eggplant again and let it marinate for few minutes.
Soak the tamarind in a cup of hot water.
Work the pulp thoroughly until it has dissolved as much as possible
Strain and press through a small hand held sieve.
Retain the liquid and discard the remaining fibers.
Dry roast (NO OIL!) the cumin, fenugreek and sesame seeds until you can smell them roasting.
Grind in a spice mill or small coffee grinder (which you have never ground coffee in!).  Set aside.
Fry the popu: Heat oil, add mustard seeds
When they start to pop, add the curry leaves
Stir for just a minute, then immediately add the onion, garlic and green chilies and stir until the onions soften.
Add the marinated eggplant.
Cover and let cook for a 2-3 minutes, until the eggplant just barely begins to soften around the edges.
Add the tamarind liquid
Cover and let simmer for a few minutes longer, until the eggplant is cooked through
Remove the cover and let the sauce reduce and thicken.
Add the milk, return to a high heat
When the milk boils, add the roasted masala powder
Stir well and remove from the heat
