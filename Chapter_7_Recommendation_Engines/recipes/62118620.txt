Title:
Light Leftover Turkey Tetrazzini Recipe
Image:
https://images.media-allrecipes.com/images/79590.png
Ingredients:
8 ounces egg noodles
1/4 pound fresh mushrooms, sliced
1 1/4 cups chicken broth
1 1/4 cups 1% milk
4 tablespoons flour
1/2 teaspoon poultry seasoning
salt and pepper to taste
2 cups cooked, chopped turkey
1 cup fat free sour cream
1/4 cup chopped fresh parsley
1/2 cup grated fresh parmesan cheese

Directions:
Preheat oven to 350 degrees and grease a 7 by 11 inch baking dish.
Cook noodles in lightly salted water according to package directions.  Drain and return to pot.
Spray large sauce pan with no stick spray and saute mushrooms over medium high heat until soft.
Stir chicken broth and 3/4 cups milk into sauce pan the mushrooms are in.  Stir the flour into the remaining milk until smooth.
When the liquid in the sauce pan has come to a boil, stir constantly as you pour the flour and milk mixture in.  Continue to boil for 1-2 minutes while stirring.  Season with poultry seasoning and salt and pepper to taste.
Stir cooked turkey into sauce and continue to cook until the turkey is warm.
Remove sauce from heat and wait for it to stop boiling, then stir in fat free sour cream and 3 tablespoons of the parsley.
Pour sauce into cooked noodles in pot and stir to coat.  Taste and add more salt and pepper if needed.  Sprinkle parmesan cheese and reserved parsley over casserole.
Bake uncovered 20 minutes or until hot and the cheese is melted.

Submitter: 
The Bunny Chef
Description: 
I made up this lighter version of turkey tetrazzini to use some of the leftover Thanksgiving turkey.  It turned out wonderfully!  The sauce doesn't take much work to make and tastes much better than the canned soup alternative.
