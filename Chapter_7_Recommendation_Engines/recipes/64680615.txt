Title:
Skillet Salmon Chowder Recipe
Image:
https://images.media-allrecipes.com/userphotos/250x250/4882432.jpg
Ingredients:
2 strips of thick-sliced bacon, diced
3 T unsalted butter
1 cup halved and sliced leeks
1/2 cup diced carrots
1/2 cup diced celery
1/8 tsp cayenne pepper
1/2 cup dry white wine
2-1/4 c seafood stock
3/4 cup heavy cream
2 tsp lemon juice
1 tsp minced fresh thyme
4 cups frozen hash brown potatoes, thawed
1 lb salmon fillet, cut from thick end, skinned and cut into 2" chunks
1 cup panic crumbs
1 Tbs unsalted butter
Zest of 1/2 lemon, minced
Fresh parsley, chopped

Directions:
Preheat oven to 350 degrees.
Cook bacon in 12" cast iron skillet until crisp. Transfer to paper towel-lined place. When cool, chop into smaller pieces.
Melt 3 T of butter in pan with bacon drippings over med-low heat.  Stir in leeks, carrots, celery and cayenne pepper. Season with salt and pepper and sweat until leeks begin to soften--about 8 minutes.
Sprinkle flour over vegetables  and cook, stirring, for about a minute; increase to medium-high heat.
Deglaze skillet with wine, scraping up any brown bits; cook one minute.
Stir in cream, stock, lemon juice and thyme; bring to a simmer.
Stir in hash browns and set salmon on top. Return chowder to a simmer.
Place skillet on a baking sheet and bake chowder until bubbly around the edges, about 20 minutes.
Combine panko bread crumbs, lemon zest, bacon and melted butter.
Remove skillet from oven and reheat broiler to high, with rack 6 inches from element.
Sprinkle crumbs over chowder and broil until golden, about 2 minutes.
Garnish with parsley.

Submitter: 
Sudie B
Description: 
A delicious variation on chowder that is more like a casserole.  No spoons needed--just a fork. Use your cast iron skillet for this recipe.
