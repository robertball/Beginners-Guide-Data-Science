Title:
LOG CABIN INN PEANUT BUTTER PIE Recipe
Image:
https://images.media-allrecipes.com/images/79590.png
Ingredients:
For the oatmeal crust
•1 1/2 cups oatmeal
•1/3 cup brown sugar
•3 tablespoons flour
•1/3 cup melted butter
For the chocolate filling
•1/2 cup butter, softened
•1/2 cup sugar
•1 egg (we substituted pasteurized liquid egg)
•3/4 cup semi-sweet chocolate chips, melted
•3/4 teaspoon vanilla
•1/2 cup chopped peanuts plus 1/4 cup for the filling (Planters cocktail peanuts coarsely chopped)
For the peanut butter filling
•8 ounces cream cheese
•1/2 cup creamy peanut butter
•1 cup powdered sugar
•1 egg
•1 1/2 cups frozen whipped topping, thawed
•1/4 cup chopped peanuts

Directions:
Combine oats, brown sugar and flour; mix in melted butter. Generously spray a deep 9-inch pan with nonstick spray. Press in pan. Bake at 350 degrees for 20 minutes. Cool completely.
Beat together butter and sugar. Add egg and beat at medium speed for 2 to 3 minutes. Blend in melted chips and vanilla. Spread cooled crust with 1 cup chocolate filling. Sprinkle with peanuts.
Beat together cream cheese and peanut butter. Add powdered sugar and beat until smooth and creamy. Add egg and blend thoroughly. Fold in thawed whipped topping.
Spoon peanut butter filling over chopped nuts. Top with remaining chocolate filling. Sprinkle 1/4 cup chopped peanuts around the outer edge of pie. Refrigerate at least 3 hours. Store in refrigerator.

Submitter: 
Cindy in Pensacola
Description: 
While researching this recipe, Kitchen Mailbox found a Post-Gazette story written in 1995 by the late Betsy Kline. Here is an excerpt: Diners at the Log Cabin Inn in Harmony know to save room for dessert when Linda Stowell's peanut butter pie is on the menu. Stowell says she bakes up to six pies for Friday and Saturday nights. (She also slices them generously: six cuts to a pie.) The double filling came from a cookbook, Stowell says, but it was her inspiration to pair it with the oatmeal crust perfected by her mother, the late Dora Druschel.
