Ingredients:
1 cup butter
2 cups celery, diced
2 cups white onion, diced
1 teaspoon rubbed sage
1/4 teaspoon thyme
1/4 teaspoon fresh ground black pepper
1 1/2 cup low sodium turkey or chicken broth (2 cups for more moist results)
2 oz. water chestnuts, diced
20 oz. cubed seasoned stuffing
1 cup water

Directions:
Add butter to the inner liner and set pressure cooker according to manufacturer's instruction to saute.  When butter is melted add celery, onions, sage, thyme and pepper. Saute for 2 minutes.  Pour in broth and mix well. Turn off pressure cooker.
Add stuffing and water chestnuts to a large mixing bowl and pour in sauteed celery, onions, sage, thyme, pepper and broth. Stir to mix.
Butter a 1.5 quart 7.5 inch round casserole dish and pour in stuffing mixture.   Don't pack.
Add 1 cup of water to the inner liner of the pressure cooker and place the trivet in the pot. Place  casserole dish on top of trivet.
Close, lock the lid and seal the vent. Set cooker on high pressure according to manufacturer's instructions and set timer for 10 minutes. Allow 10-15 minutes for pressure to build. When time is up use the natural pressure release method per manufacturer's instructions, 15 to 20 minutes.
Serve as is, or place in oven and bake until top is slightly brown.

An electric pressure cooker can help during the holidays when you are pressed for stovetop or oven space. This dressing is flavorful and is sure to please the traditionalist.
