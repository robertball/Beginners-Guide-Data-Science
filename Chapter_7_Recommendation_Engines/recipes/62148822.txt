Title:
Port Huron Style Coney Sauce Recipe
Image:
https://images.media-allrecipes.com/images/79590.png
Ingredients:
1 lb Ground Chuck
1.5 t. chili powder
.5 t. cinnamon
2 t. Paprika
.5 t. black pepper
.25 t. onion powder
.25 t. garlic powder
.25 t. cumin
.25 t. cayenne pepper
.25 t. allspice
2 t. yellow mustard
1 t. beef base (Better Than Bullion)
2-3 cups of water
3 T. finely crushed Club Crackers

Directions:
In a large saucepan mix: beef, spices, mustard, beef base, and 1 cup of water.
Mash all ingredients together with a potato masher until everything is blended and the beef in broken up into fine pieces.
Bring ingredients to a simmer and reduce heat to medium low.
Simmer for 2 to 3 hours adding water a 1/2 c. at a time as needed to keep it moist and saucy.
When the beef is very soft add the cracker crumbs to thicken the sauce.
Serve over steamed hot dogs in steamed buns with mustard and chopped raw onion if you wish.

Submitter: 
Sarah Travis Stacey
Description: 
A style of Coney Sauce that is found in the Greek restaurants and Coney Islands around the Detroit, MI area.  Very unique and different from New York Coney Sauce.
