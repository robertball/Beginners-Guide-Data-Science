Title:
Pork with Three Onions Recipe
Image:
https://images.media-allrecipes.com/images/79590.png
Ingredients:
1/2 cup bottled teriyaki sauce
3 cloves garlic, minced
1 pound pork tenderloin
2 T peanut or vegetable oil, divided
1 small red onion, cut into thin wedges
1 small yellow onion, cut into thin wedges
1/2 tsp red pepper flakes (or to taste)
1 tsp sugar
1 tsp cornstarch
4 green onions, cut into 1-inch pieces

Directions:
Combine teriyaki sauce and garlic a shallow bowl.  Cut pork across the grain into 1/4-inch slices; cut each slice in half.  Toss pork with teriyaki mixture.  Marinate at room temperature for 10 minutes.
Heat a large skillet over medium heat.  Add 1 tablespoon oil; heat until hot. Drain pork, reserving marinade.  Stir fry pork 3 minutes, or until no longer pink.  Remove the meat from the pan and set aside.
Heat remaining tablespoon oil in skillet; add red and yellow onions and red pepper flakes.  Reduce heat to medium.  Cook 4 to 5  minutes until the onions are softened, stirring occasionally.  Sprinkle with sugar, cook 1 minute more.
Blend cornstarch into reserved marinade until smooth.  Stir into skillet.  Stir fry 1 minute or until sauce boils and thickens.
Return pork allong with any accumulated juices to skillet; heat through.  Stir in green onions.  Serve over bean threads or steamed rice.

Description: 
Slices of marinated pork tenderloin are tossed with three kinds of onions in a teriyaki style sauce.  Delicious served over fried bean threads or rice.
