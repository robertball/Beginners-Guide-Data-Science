Title:
Iowa Beef Stew Recipe
Image:
https://images.media-allrecipes.com/userphotos/250x250/1084861.jpg
Ingredients:
3 T extra virgin olive oil
2 T butter
2 pounds stew meat
1 med onion, diced
4 cloves garlic, minced
3 T tomato paste (~ 2/3 small can)
4 T worchestershire sauce
5-6 cups beef stock
1 cup red wine
1 can light beer
2 tsp sugar
¼ tsp cloves
1 tsp celery salt
1 tsp paprika
Kosher salt (to taste)
Fresh ground black pepper (to taste)
1 small can sliced mushrooms, undrained
4-5 carrots, peeled and cut into bite size pieces
4-5 new potatoes, washed and cut into bite size pieces
1 small rutabaga, peeled & diced

Directions:
Melt butter in oil in large pot over med high heat.  Add stew meat to brown (in two batches). Remove meat, cut into bite size pieces, and set aside.
Add onion and garlic to pot and sauté for 3-4 minutes.
Add tomato paste, worchestershire, beef stock (start with 5 cups), wine and beer, along with sugar & seasonings.  Bring to boil then reduce heat to simmer.
Return meat to pot, cover and simmer for 1 hour.
Add vegetables to pot, recover and simmer for at least 1 hour. Can keep warm until serving.
Serve with crusty bread or corn muffins.

Submitter:

Description: 
This recipe was created during an unprecedented cold spell in Iowa (i.e. wind chill of -50!).  Great for staying in by the fire with a good book and glass of wine.
