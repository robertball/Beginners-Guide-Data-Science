Title:
Shrimp, sausage, fish Jambalaya Recipe
Image:
https://images.media-allrecipes.com/images/79590.png
Ingredients:
4 tablespoons butter or olive oil
10 ounces or so Andouille or any smoked sausage sliced into 1/4 inch rounds and then halve the slices
1 cup diced onion
1 cup diced celery
1 cup diced green pepper
1 1/2 teaspoon's minced garlic
1 6 oz can tomato paste
1 14.5 oz can diced tomatoes
2 teaspoons Worcestershire Sauce ("Lee & Perrins" of course)
1/2-1 tablespoon Cajun Seasoning such as "Tony Chachere's"
1/2 teaspoon of fresh ground black pepper
4 cups (32 0z) low salt Chicken broth
1/3 cup chopped fresh or 2 tablespoons dry parsley
2 cups medium grain rice
3/4-1 pound uncooked shelled shrimp
3/4 lb cod fillets cut into 1 1/2-2 inch chunks (optional)

Directions:
In a large heavy bottomed covered dutch oven or paella or similar pan melt butter or heat oil over medium high heat and brown Andouille sausage. Remove sausage from pan with slotted spoon and reserve
Add onion, celery and green pepper to same pan  and saute for 5- 10 min till onion becomes translucent.
Add garlic and saute for one minute
Add tomato paste and mix thoroughly with vegetables and saute for 3-5 minutes stirring constantly
Add reserved sausage, undrained can of tomatoes, Cajun seasoning, pepper and Worcestershire sauce and mix thoroughly
Add 4 cups low salt chicken broth, increase heat to high and bring to a boil
Add rice and mix thoroughly bring to a boil and then cover and reduce heat to simmer and cook undisturbed for 25-30 minutes or until rice is almost done.
Add shrimp and fish chunks and increase heat to medium high till mixture returns to boil then reduce heat and cook covered or uncovered at simmer for about 10 more minutes stirring occasionally till it is the right consistency.
Taste and add salt if necessary

Submitter: 
Baron
Description: 
Did a lot of research on Jambalaya and reviewed a number of recipes and came up with the following.  I like whitefish and while not traditional added chunks of cod fillet to this dish.
