Title:
Turkey "Wild" Rice Soup Recipe
Image:
https://images.media-allrecipes.com/images/79590.png
Ingredients:
6 T butter
2 cloves garlic, minced
1 cup finely chopped carrots
1 large yellow onion, chopped
1 1/2 cup minced celery
1/2 cup flour
4 cups chicken broth
1 cup wine of your preference (not cooking wine)
1 cup uncooked Lundberg Wild Blend (mixture of wild and brown rice)
3 cups chicken or turkey, cooked and cubed
2 tsp dried chives
1 tsp salt
1/2 tsp pepper (white or black depending upon preference)
1 1/2 tsp fresh rosemary
1 tsp dried thyme
2 cups half-and-half

Directions:
Melt butter in a large pot. Add garlic, carrot, onion, and celery. Cook until tender crisp. Blend in flour and stir for 2 minutes. Add broth, wine, and rice. Bring to a boil and cook until slightly thickened. Stir in turkey, chives, salt, pepper, rosemary, and thyme.
Reduce heat and simmer for 1 hour or until rice is cooked.
Add half-and-half and heat through. Serve hot.

Submitter: 
Jill Fisher
Description: 
Comfort food at its best! I remember having my mom's version of this soup as a great way to use up turkey after Thanksgiving.
