Title:
Roasted Poblano Corn Chowder Recipe
Image:
https://images.media-allrecipes.com/images/79590.png
Ingredients:
3-4 medium potatoes
4-5 poblano peppers, halved and seeded
3-4 ears fresh corn
1 T. olive oil
Â½  onion, chopped
2 cloves garlic, minced
14.5 oz. can chicken broth
14.75 oz. can creamed corn
1/4 cup finely chopped cilantro
1 tsp. ground cumin
1 c. milk
Salt, to taste
Shredded cheese and sour cream (optional)

Directions:
Peel potatoes and cut into small cubes. Place potatoes in a saucepan, and fill the pan with water until the potatoes are covered.  Bring to a boil over medium-high heat, reduce heat to medium, and simmer until tender (about 15-20 minutes).  Drain and set aside.
Place peppers in baking dish skin sides up, drizzle with olive oil, and bake until skins begin to blister (about 15 minutes).  Set aside.
Shuck and rinse corn and cut off of cob.  Spread kernels on a greased baking sheet and sprinkle with salt.  Bake until tender (about 15 minutes), stirring every 5 minutes.  Set aside.
Heat olive oil over medium heat.  Add onion and garlic and cook until onion is translucent, stirring frequently.
Remove skins from peppers and coarsely chop.  Add to saucepan, along with chicken broth, creamed corn, cilantro, and cumin.  Blend with hand blender (or in small batches in a regular blender) until mixture is smooth and uniform.
Bring mixture to near boiling, then add milk, potatoes,  roasted corn, and salt.  Simmer 5-10 additional minutes.
Serve garnished with sour cream and shredded cheese.
