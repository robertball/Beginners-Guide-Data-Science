Title:
Minnesota Pasta-Pea Salad Recipe
Image:
https://images.media-allrecipes.com/images/79590.png
Ingredients:
8 oz cooked medium shell pasta; cooked al dente’, drained and rinsed under cold water
1¼ cups frozen peas; cooked, drained and rinsed under cold water
1 egg, hard boiled and chopped
1/4 cup green onions, sliced; white and light green parts only
1/2 cup celery, chopped
1/2 cup mayonnaise, use more if adding a meat option
1/2 cup sour cream, use more if adding a meat option
1 cup sharp cheddar cheese, shredded
1/2 teaspoon dried dill weed, use up to 1 teaspoon if desired
Dry roasted sunflower seed kernels, salted variety
See NOTES for making it into a meal by adding a meat option.

Directions:
In a large bowl, blend the mayonnaise and the sour cream together. Mix the celery, chopped egg, and dill weed into the mayonnaise and sour cream mixture. Fold the cooked pasta into the bowl, followed by folding in the cheese and then gently folding in the cooked peas. Cover and chill for at least 1 hour or up to 4 hours before serving. After 4 hours you may need to add more mayonnaise and sour cream mixture. Top each serving with 2 teaspoons of sunflower kernels.

Submitter: 
Dale
Description: 
Beat the summer heat with this refreshing salad that can be served as a side dish or as a main course meal. The sunflower kernels give it crunch and a light nutty flavor. Optional meat additions makes it into a delicious main course meal.
