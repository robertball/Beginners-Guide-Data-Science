Title:
Kermit's Low Country Boil Recipe
Image:
https://images.media-allrecipes.com/images/79590.png
Ingredients:
Old Bay Seasoning TM to taste
5 pounds new potatoes
3 (16 ounce) packages cooked kielbasa sausage, cut into 1 inch pieces
8 ears  fresh corn, husks and silks removed
4 pounds whole crab, broken into pieces
5 pounds fresh shrimp, peeled and deveined
6 cloves of garlic
2 yellow onions
2 spanish onions
3 lemons
1 bay leaf
1 bass ale
newspapers
beer, texas toast, and drawn butter

Directions:
Heat a large pot of water over an outdoor cooker, or medium-high heat indoors. Add Old Bay Seasoning (8 oz to every 6 quarts of water) bass ale and bay leaf then bring to a boil. Add potatoes, lemons and sausage, and cook for about 10 minutes. Add the corn, onions, and crab; cook for another 5 minutes, then add the shrimp when everything else is almost done, and cook for another 3 or 4 minutes.
Drain off the water, and pour the contents out onto a picnic table covered with newspaper. Grab a paper plate and a beer and enjoy!

Submitter: 
Caveman Cook
Description: 
Famous in the Low Country of Georgia and South Carolina. This boil is done best on an outdoor cooker. It has sausage, shrimp, crab, potatoes and corn for an all-in-one pot all-you-can-eat buffet!Modified from: Dave's Low Country Boil
