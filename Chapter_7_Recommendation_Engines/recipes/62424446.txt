Title:
Shrimp Newberg Recipe
Image:
https://images.media-allrecipes.com/images/79590.png
Ingredients:
1 1/2C brown rice
1Tbsp plus one 1tsp unsalted butter
2lbs large shrimp-cleaned and cut into thirds, shells reserved
1 1/3C milk
2tsp cornstarch
1 onion, finely chopped
2Tbsp flour
1/4C sherry wine
Salt and pepper
1/3C finely chopped chives

Directions:
In a small sacepan,combine the rice with 1 1/2C water. Bring to a boil, cover, lower the heat and simmer until tender, about 20min; fluff with a fork. Cover to keep warm.
Meanwhile, in a large saucepan melt 2 tsp butter over medium heat. Add the shrimp shells and cook, stirring, until cris, about 5 minutes. In a small bowl, whisk the milk with the cornstarch, then whisk into the shrimp shells. Bring to a simmer, whisking, then cook for 1 minute. Strain the mixture into a bowl, pressing the shrimp shells to get all the liquid; discard the shells. Wash and dry the saucepan.  In the saucepan, melt the remaining 2tsp butter over medium-low heat. Add the onion and cook, stirring frequently, until softened, about 5 minutes. Stir in  the flour for 2 minutes. Slowly whisk in the milk mixture and bring to a boil. Lower the heat and simmer for 1 minute. Stir in the shrimp and sherry and cook, stirring, until the shrimp is firm, about 2 minutes; season with salt and pepper. Stir in the chives. Serve over the rice.
