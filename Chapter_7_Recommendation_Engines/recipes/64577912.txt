Title:
Adam's Picadillo Recipe
Image:
https://images.media-allrecipes.com/userphotos/250x250/3626731.jpg
Ingredients:
1 tbsp  extra virgin olive oil
3 cloves garlic, minced
1 large white Onion, diced
1 Green Bell Pepper, diced
1 lb ground beef
1/2  to 1 lb chorizo sausage (for an optional extra flavor & spiciness, otherwise substitute with additional ground beef)
1 cup raises, chopped
1/4 cup capers, drained
1 tomato, diced
1 cup green olives with pimiento, pre-sliced or rough sliced/quartered
1 4oz jar, diced pimiento
1 tbsp cumin
1/2 tbsp ground coriander
1 tsp salt
1/2 tsp white pepper
1 tbsp paprika
1/2 tbsp garlic powder
1/2 tbsp onion powder
1 /2 tsp ground cinnamon
1 tbsp cilantro, dried
1 cup red wine
1/4 cup apple cider vinegar, optional
1 bushel of fresh cilantro, chopped
1/2 lime

Directions:
1. In large cast iron skillet on medium to medium-low heat, brown ground beef (and optional chorizo sausage), and drain. Then set aside until step 4.
2. To the now empty skillet, add olive oil and cook onions, bell pepper, and garlic until translucent. Approx 10 mins. Add wine and stir and continue to cook, letting wine reduce a bit. Approx 10-15 mins.
3. Add dry seasonings (cumin, coriander, salt, white pepper, paprika, garlic powder, onion powder, ground cinnamon, dried cilantro) and stir well until incorporated and then continue to cook for 10-15 mins.
4. Add previously cooked ground beef (and optional chorizo sausage) to mix, along with vinegar, capers, tomato, pimiento, and raisins. Then cover and let the mixture simmer for up to 60 mins.
5. Add juice of 1/2 lime, and chopped fresh cilantro and serve.
Serving suggestion:  Good over white rice, and/or with cornbread.

Submitter: 
Adam Caskey
Description: 
Cuban inspired flavors come together in this version of a classic dish, Picadillo.
