Title:
Briased Tofu with Eggplant and Shitakes Recipe
Image:
https://images.media-allrecipes.com/images/79590.png
Ingredients:
1/4 cup peanut or corn oil
1 cup sliced shitakes
Salt & peper to taste
1 Tbsp chopped garlic
1 Tbsp peeled and minced fresh ginger
1-1/2 lbs eggplant trimed , cut into 1-1/2" chunks, salted, rinsed and dried
1 Tbsp chili paste
1/2 cup vegetable or chicken stock
2 Tbsp soy sauce
1 lb firm tofu, cut into 3/4" cubes
1 Tbsp dark sesame oil for garnish
Chopped cilantro for garnish
1 Tbsp toasted sesame seeds for garnish
2 Tbsp minced greenonions for garnish

Directions:
Place half of the peanut oil into a deep skillet over medium high heat.
When hot, add shitakes and some salt and pepper.
Cook mushrooms 5 - 10 minutes stirring occasionally until the mushrooms are crisp.  Remove with a slotted spoon and set aside.
Add remaining oil, garlic and ginger.
Once garlic and ginger are sizzling, add eggplant.
Cook, stiring every minute or so, for 5 - 10 minutes until eggplant browns.
Add the chile paste and stock.  Stir scraping the bottom of the pan to release any eggplant still stuck to pan.
Cook eggplant 10 - 15 minutes more until very tender.
Stir in the soy sauce and tofu and cook for about 5 minutes until tofu is heated through.
Stir in the reserved shitakes and turn off heat.
Adjust seasonings, garnish and seve.

Submitter: 
Bill Alexander
Description: 
A more or less traditional Sichuan preparation, creamy and delicious with soft-cooked eggplant, made crisp by the addition of sauteed shitakes.  From Mark Bittman's How to Cook Everyting Vegetarian.
