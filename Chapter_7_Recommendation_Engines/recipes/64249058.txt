Title:
Tex-Mex Turkey Soup Recipe
Image:
https://images.media-allrecipes.com/images/79590.png
Ingredients:
1 tablespoon olive oil
1 onion, diced
6 cloves garlic, minced
2 teaspoons chili powder
1/2 teaspoon cumin
1/2 teaspoon oregano
4 cups low sodium chicken broth
1 (10.75 ounce) can condensed Fiesta tomato soup
1 (14.5 ounce) can Italian diced tomatoes
1 can Rotel
5 cups shredded cooked turkey
1 tablespoon dried parsley
2 Tbsp. Lime Juice
2 (14 ounce) cans black beans, rinsed, drained
4 cups frozen corn
2 oz. Neufchâtel cheese
2 oz. Taco seasoning
1T + 1t dried cilantro
*Can Sub homemade turkey broth for the chicken broth. - Boil down the carcass with a chopped onion, a bay leaf, salt, & a wedge of lime (the acid helps extract the calcium from the bones). Put this through a strainer to get 4 cups of broth
Toppings:
6 cups corn tortilla chips
3/4 cup chopped green onion
1 cup shredded Cheddar-Monterey Jack cheese blend
1/2 cup sour cream

Directions:
Heat olive oil in a small skillet over medium heat. Add diced onion and cook until it begins to soften, about 4 minutes. Add garlic, chili powder, cumin and oregano and cook, stirring, for 1 minute. Transfer to crock pot.
Stir in water, tomato soup, diced tomatoes, rotel, shredded turkey, parsley, lime juice and bouillon cubes. Add black beans, corn, Neufchâtel cheese, taco seasoning and cilantro. Cook on high for 2 hours.
Serve soup with crushed tortilla chips, chopped green onion, shredded cheese and additional cilantro and sour cream.

Description: 
Shredded turkey in a tomato-based soup is jazzed up with herbs and spices for a rich and warming alternative to leftover turkey sandwiches.Modified from: Tex-Mex Turkey Soup
