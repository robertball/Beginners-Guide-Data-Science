Title:
Brisket Empanadas Recipe
Image:
https://images.media-allrecipes.com/userphotos/250x250/554365.jpg
Ingredients:
2 Medium potatoes, diced 1/4" cubes
1 tablespoon salt
2 tablespoons butter
1/2 a yellow onion finely chopped
2 teaspoons minced garlic
1 teaspoon Goya Adobo seasoning
2 cups brisket, shredded and chopped
2 cans (7.5ozs) Pillsbury® buttermilk biscuits
1 cup monterrey jack cheese, finely shredded
1 cup sharp cheddar cheese, finely shredded
1 egg white beaten with a tablespoon of water

Directions:
Place the potatoes and salt in a pot of water and bring to a boil for 6 minutes.
Meanwhile melt butter in a frying pan, add onions and garlic and sautee till tender.
Strain potatoes and add to the onions and garlic along with the adobo seasoning mixing well, cook together for 5 minutes.
Add brisket to pan, mix everything well, cook for additional 5 minutes or till potatoes are tender stirring occasionally. Set aside to cool.
Preheat oven to 350°F.
Separate dough into 10 biscuits. Press each into 5" round. Divide filling evenly onto half of each biscuit round to within 1/4 inch of edge. Top with a mix of the shredded cheese.
Fold dough round over filling; press edges together with a fork to seal.
Place on ungreased cookie sheet.
Brush the egg white with water over each empanada and sprinkle cheese on top.
Bake at 350°F for 15 minutes till golden brown.
Enjoy

Submitter: 
Raven
Description: 
I had quite a bit of brisket left over and was wanting some empanadas so I just put 2 & 2 together. They can either be fried or baked as in this case.
