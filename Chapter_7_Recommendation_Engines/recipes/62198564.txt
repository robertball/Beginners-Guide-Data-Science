Title:
Pennsylvania Dutch Potato Filling Recipe
Image:
https://images.media-allrecipes.com/userphotos/250x250/309854.jpg
Ingredients:
10 lbs potatoes
3 onions
1 bunch celery
1 loaf white bread(approx 20 slices of white bread)
2 Cups butter
1 1/2 Cups milk
3 Tbsp Seasoned Salt

Directions:
Peel potatoes and cut into 1 inch cubes and boil in a large pot of salted water for 25 min.
Meanwhile, dice onions and celery.  Saute onions and celery in 1/2 Cup butter until cooked down and browned.
Drain potatoes, mix potatoes and 1/2 Cup butter with stand mixer.
Now you are going to add in the ingredients to the mixer in steps:
Add: 1 Cup milk, 1 Tbsp Seasoned Salt and 4 slices of bread (torn in pieces), continue mixing.
Add: 1/2 Cup milk, 4 slices bread(torn into pieces), continue mixing.
Add: 2 slices of bread(torn into pieces), continue mixing.
Add: 1/2 Cup butter, 1 Tbsp seasoned salt, all onions and celery from skillet, 4 slices of bread(torn into pieces), continue mixing.
Add: 2-6 more slices of bread until desired texture.
Bake: potatoes in 2 glass casserole dishes--top with remaining butter in slivers and sprinkle with 1 Tbsp season salt, cover, bake at 350 degrees for 1 hour.

Submitter: 
Shauna Rhoads
Description: 
These potatoes are perfect for Thanksgiving and Christmas!  They are my husband's family tradition in Pennsylvania. This is the actual recipe from My husbands great-grandmother.  I cut it down by a third for my family of 4.  But I have given you the full recipe so you can cut it how ever you would like.
