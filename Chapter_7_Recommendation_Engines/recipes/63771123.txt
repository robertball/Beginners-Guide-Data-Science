Title:
Grandma's Peanut Butter Cookies Recipe
Image:
https://images.media-allrecipes.com/userphotos/250x250/1045237.jpg
Ingredients:
1/2 cup soft shortening (half butter)
3/4 cup peanut butter (extra crunchy)
1 1/4 cup brown sugar (packed firmly)
1 egg
1 3/4 cups Gold Medal flour
3/4 teaspoon baking soda
1 tablespoon vanilla extract (or more to taste)
3/4 teaspoon salt (optional)

Directions:
Mix peanut butter, shortening, sugars, and egg thoroughly.
Measure flour by dip-level-pour method.
Mix rest of ingredients and stir in.  Chill dough (until oven reaches temperature)
Heat oven to 375 degrees F.
Roll dough into balls the size of large walnuts.
Place 3" apart on lightly greased baking sheet.
Flatten criss-cross fashion with a fork dipped in flour.
Bake 10 to 12 minutes or until set, but not hard.

Submitter: 
markfernandez
Description: 
These are the peanut butter cookies my grandmother used to make and are the best PB cookies ever!  Not gooey, chewy, or cracker-crisp.  Just the right amount of soft and crunch, and they melt in your mouth with peanut-buttery goodness.
