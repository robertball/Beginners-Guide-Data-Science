Title:
Alsatian Pork and Sauerkraut Recipe
Image:
https://images.media-allrecipes.com/images/79590.png
Ingredients:
4 slices of bacon, cut into 1 inch pieces
1 medium onion chopped
1  16 oz. can of sauerkraut, drained
1 & 1/2 tblsp. dark brown sugar
3 medium potatoes quartered
1 large sized or 2 small granny smith apples
cord and sliced.
12 juniper berries ( you can find them in the spice section of the grocery store)
6 whole pepper corns
2 whole gloves
1 sprig fresh parsley
1 bay leaf
4 1/2 inch boneless pork chops
1 or 2 rings of kilbasa cut into 3 inch sections(turkey kilbasa works too)
4 cups chicken broth or enough to cover ingredients in large pot or crockpot.

Directions:
In large pot cook bacon until crispy. Drain on paper towel and wipe out pan of bacon grease, keeping brown bits in bottom. Stir in saurkraut and brown sugar and chicken broth. Add potatoes and apples.
With cheese cloth (or use a tea ball), make a spice bag with juniper berries, peppercorns, cloves, parsley an bay leaf and add to pot. Add pork chops and kilbasa. (add more chiken broth at this time if needed to cover all ingredients. Heat to the boil and then reduce to a simmer. Simmer an hour (or adjust time for slow cooker). Cook until potatoes are done. Remove saurkaurt and potatoes and apples to large platter with slotted spoon. Arrange meat around edges.

Description: 
This is a French recipe! Very easy and a real comfort food. An all in one pot meal! I have gotten people who say they don't like sauerkraut to try this dish. They couldn't believe how good it is and asked for more.
