Title:
Cpt Duarte's Salt Cod Cakes Recipe
Image:
https://images.media-allrecipes.com/images/79590.png
Ingredients:
1-1 1/2 lb boneless salt cod
2 large baking potatoes peeled and med dice
2 eggs slightly beaten
1 large onion finely chopped
1/2 cup fresh parsley chopped
1 tsp old bay seasoning
Pepper to taste
Flour for dusting
Olive oil for frying

Directions:
Place cod in deep dish and cover with cold water. Place in fridge for 8 hrs.
Change water every 2 hrs or as often as possible to rehydrate and reduce salt content. Discard  water.
Rinse fish, place fish and potatoes in large pot with enough water to cover. Cook until fish flakes with a fork and potatoes are tender. Drain liquid. Roughly mash potatoes and fish. Add the onion,eggs,chopped parsley and seasonings to fish/potatoe mixture and gently mix.
Form into size of golf ball and slightly flatten with your palm.
Lightly dust with flour.
Fry in enough olive oil to cover bottom of pan over med/high heat until lightly golden.
Drain on paper towels.
Serve with a good tartar sauce.

Submitter: 
NainInCandia
Description: 
Based on my father-in-laws recipe, these fish cakes use salt cod. Trust me, it is worth the effort to find the salt cod. The slight saltiness of the cod brings out a whole new dimension of flavor. Who knows fish better than a portuguese fishing Capt? Even Emeril would be proud!
