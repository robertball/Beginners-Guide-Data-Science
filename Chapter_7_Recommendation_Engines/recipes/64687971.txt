Ingredients:
1 lbs extra lean stewing beef, cut into bite size pieces
2-3 tbs olive oil
1 lg onion, finely diced. About 1 cup
3 lg cloves garlic, finely chopped
3 cups carrots cut into bite size
2 cup celery, cut into bite size.
1 1/2 cups diced tomatoes, caned
salt and pepper to taste
splash of dry red wine, optional
1 1/2 chicken broth
1 sprig of fresh thyme or 1/2 tsp dried
1 bay leaf
1/3 cup chicken broth, second amount
2 tbs corn starch

Directions:
Heat the pressure cooker, using the saute option, and add the oil until it sizzles.
Add the beef cook until all sides are brown.
Add the onion and cook, stirring constantly, for 3-5 minutes or until the onions are soft.
Add the garlic and cook until fragrant, about 1 minute.
If using add the wine and deglaze the pot.
Add the carrots, celery, and tomatoes and stir well.
Add the chicken broth and stir.
Add the thyme and bay leaf .
Put the lid on the pot and press the meat/stew option, adjust time to 35 minutes if necessary.
Allow the pressure to come down naturally for at least 10 minutes.
Quick release the pressure.
Add the cornstarch to the 1/3 cup of broth and stir well.
Use the saute option to bring the mixture to a boil, slowly add the slurry. Simmer this until the stew thickens slightly 3-5 minutes.
Serve with lots of crusty bread and enjoy!
Note: The chicken broth is not a typo
