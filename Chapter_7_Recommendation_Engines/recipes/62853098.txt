Title:
Tomato Sauce for the  Freezer Recipe
Image:
https://images.media-allrecipes.com/images/79590.png
Ingredients:
3 Tablespoons olive oil
4 medium onions, chopped
3 small garlic cloves
13 pounds of fresh tomatoes, peeled and cut into large chunks
4 6 ounce cans of tomato paste
1 pound mushrooms, sliced (optional)
1 cup chopped parsley
3 Tablespoons sugar
3 Tablespoons salt (adjust to taste)
4 teaspoons oregano leaves
2 bay leaves

Directions:
In a large saucepot over medium-high heat, heat olive oil and add chopped onions and garlic cloves.  Cook until tender, about 10 minutes.  Discard garlic cloves.  Add remaining ingredients, heat to boiling, then reduce heat to low.  Cover and simmer 2 hours.  Discard bay leaves.  Ladle sauce into pint or quart containers, leaving 1/2" of head space.  Cover and refrigerate until chilled.  Label and freeze.

Submitter: 
Bibi
Description: 
Savor summer's abundance by freezing your garden tomatoes in a sauce base for quick dinners later on!  I have used this in spaghetti and lasagna with good results.  When tomatoes are plentiful, I double the recipe and use a large stock pot.
