Title:
Korean Pork Curry Recipe
Image:
https://images.media-allrecipes.com/images/79590.png
Ingredients:
1 1/2 lbs. boneless pork chop, cubed (beef or chicken can also be used)
1 large yellow onion, peeled and cubed
2 large russet potatoes, peeled and cubed
3 large carrots, peeled and cubed (or 1/2 lbs. baby carrots)
4 Tbs. olive oil
4 cups water
Curry powder*

Directions:
1. Cube pork (think bite size pieces) and saute in large skillet with 2 Tbs. olive oil until cooked. Meat should be white on outside.
2. Cube onion, carrots, and potatoes and saute in large pot with 2 Tbs. olive oil.
3. Put pork in pot with vegetables and mix well.
4. Add water to pot and bring to a boil.
5. Turn down heat to simmer and cover until potatoes are tender but still solid.
6. Turn off heat and add 1 Tbs. curry powder to pot and mix until dissolved. Continue adding powder in this fashion to taste.
7. Turn heat back on and cook until sauce is thick and onions look transparent.
Serve over rice.

Submitter: 
Cristina Marie Winchester
Description: 
Mild curry dish popular in Korea, includes ingredients you can find in your local grocery store and Korean/Asian market. The Ottogi version of the curry simply leaves out the meat, making this a great dish for vegetarians. Feel free to add bell peppers
