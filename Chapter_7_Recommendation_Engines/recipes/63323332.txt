Title:
Greek Rice  (Yemisee) Recipe
Image:
https://images.media-allrecipes.com/userphotos/250x250/880254.jpg
Ingredients:
3/4 stick butter
1 medium onion, diced
1 tablespoon minced garlic
3 lbs ground chuck
1-1/2 tablespoons seasoned salt
1 tablespoon black pepper
1 tablespoon soy sauce
1-8 oz. can tomato sauce
1-8 oz. can water
1 tablespoon dried parsley
1 teaspoon dried basil
1-15 oz. can chicken broth
2 cup Uncle Ben's converted rice

Directions:
In dutch oven, sautee' onion in butter with garlic.
Add ground chuck and season with salt, pepper and soy sauce.
When meat is browned, add tomato sauce, water, basil and parsley. Let simmer 15 minutes with lid.
Transfer meat mixture to small roaster, and add chicken broth and rice.
Place in preheated 375 degree oven without lid for 1/2 hour, then cover for additional 15 minutes. There should be no liquid remaining, but rice should be moist.
Remove from oven, cover, and let steam for at least 15 minutes. Stir and serve.

Submitter: 
Denise Phillips Sarigianopoulo
Description: 
This is not to be confused with rice pilaf, but is also very tasty. We use it as a stuffing, a side dish, or even as the main dish. The italian side of our family insisted my mom make this at every family reunion. No matter your nationality, this rice will be a big hit!
