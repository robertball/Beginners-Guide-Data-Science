Ingredients:
4 tablespoons olive oil, divided use
3 pounds beef chuck roast
1 1/2 teaspoon garlic salt
1 teaspoon fresh ground black pepper
2 cups beef broth
1 pound baby potatoes
1 1/2 cups baby carrots
1 medium onion, quartered
1 (1 oz.) package of onion soup mix
2 1/2 tablespoons corn starch mixed with 1/4 cup cold water

Directions:
Add two tablespoons of olive oil to inner liner and set pressure cooker according to manufactures instruction to saute. When oil becomes hot add roast and sear for 4-5 minutes each side.
Add beef broth to roast.
In a seal-able plastic food bag add 2 tablespoons olive oil, soup mix, potatoes, carrots and onion. Shake until vegetables become evenly coated.
Place potatoes, onions and carrot mixture in inner pot and spread evenly around roast.
Close, lock the lid and seal the vent. Set cooker on high pressure according to manufactures instructions and set timer for 60 minutes. Allow 10-15 minutes for pressure to build. When time is up use the quick release method per manufactures instructions.
Remove roast from pot, slice and serve with potatoes and carrots.
Use the extra dripping from the pot to make gravy if desired by selecting the saute setting, bring to a boil; stir in cornstarch and water mixture and cook, stirring frequently, until slightly thickened, about 2-3 minutes or until desired consistency is reached.
