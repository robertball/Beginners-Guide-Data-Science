Title:
Resturant Style Albondigas Recipe
Image:
https://images.media-allrecipes.com/userphotos/250x250/773222.jpg
Ingredients:
1 whole large onion, diced
4 cloves garlic, minced
14 cups of water
8+ small chicken bullion cubes (5+ of the large ones)
2 small cans tomato paste
1 tsp dried oregano
2 tbs salt (+more at the end to taste)
2 tbs pepper (+more at the end to taste)
1 whole stalk celery, large chunks
2 lbs carrots (approx 8), peeled and in large chunks
1 head of cabbage, cut into into large chunks
6 medium potatoes, skinned and cubed
1 15oz can whole kernel corn drained,or 12 cobbettes
1 7oz can Embasa brand salsa mexicana
1 7oz can Embasa brand salsa verde
Meatballs
1 eggs
1 tbs garlic salt
1 tbs pepper
1/2 cup uncooked  white rice
1 tsp oregano
1/4 cup minced white onion
1/4 cup minced cilantro
2 lbs 80/20 ground beef

Directions:
In the largest soup pot you have add onion and garlic and saute until tender, about 5 minutes. Add 14 cups of water, bullion cubes, and tomato paste. Stir until dissolved. Add oregano, salt, pepper and vegetables.
To make the meatballs, in a separate bowl whisk eggs, salt, pepper, rice,cilantro, oregano and onion together. Add in ground beef and mix with hands until well combined. Roll into meatballs approx 1 1/2 inches round. Drop into soup as you roll. Boil for 20 minutes and check seasoning, add seasoning to taste. Boil 10 minutes more or until vegetables are tender and meatballs are cooked through. Cook on low for 60 minutes. Skim the fat at the top of the pot and serve.
optional: top with diced onion, lemon or lime and cilantro

Description: 
Tastes just like a traditional Albondigas soup from my favorite Mexican restaurant! Full of bold flavor with a hint of spice. This recipe makes more then enough to entertain with.
