Title:
Lamb stew Recipe
Image:
https://images.media-allrecipes.com/images/79590.png
Ingredients:
3/4 cup all-purpose flour
1 tablespoon and 1 teaspoon salt
1 tablespoon ground black pepper
7 pounds cubed stew meat
14 ounces thickly sliced bacon, finely diced
1/2 cup butter plus large knob of butter
2 onions, chopped
7 carrots, coarsely chopped
3 1/2 cloves garlic, minced
4 cups red wine
4 bay leaves
2 teaspoons chopped fresh thyme
2 cups coarsely chopped mushrooms
2 1/2 cups beef stock
2 cups beef jus
1 1/2 teaspoons cornstarch

Directions:
1. Place bacon in a large, deep pan. Cook over medium heat until even brown, stirring often. Drain and set aside. Retain bacon fat.
2. Put lamb,  1 teaspoon salt, pepper and flour in a large bag and toss to coat evenly. Brown meat in pan with bacon fat. Set browned meat to one side.
3. Add butter to pan and allow to melt. Add onions and soften over a low heat. Add garlic part way through. Saut√© slowly until onions begins to turn golden.
4. Add meat to softened onions. Add the beef stock, beef jus, bacon, carrots, red wine, chopped thyme, bay leaves and tablespoon salt. Stir we'll and cook uncovered over a low heat, stirring occasionally.
5. After 1 hour add the mushrooms. Stir we'll and continue to cook over a low heat.
6. After approx 2 hours if the stew requires thickening, add a small amount of water to the cornstarch in a small cup. Add resultant loose paste to the stew and stir well. Cook for a further 15 minutes.
7. Add large knob of butter to stew just before serving and stir well until melted.

Description: 
A tasty lamb stew that gets better the longer it is cooked. Perhaps best if prepared as described and reheated the next day.
