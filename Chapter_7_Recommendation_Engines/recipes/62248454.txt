Title:
It's too sweet, sour, salty, now what do I do? Recipe
Image:
https://images.media-allrecipes.com/images/79590.png
Ingredients:
It's too sour
It's too sweet
It's too salty
It's bitter
It's too hot and spicy

Directions:
The four senses of taste are sour, sweet, salty and bitter and way too hot and spicy.
If you alter one of the tastes, it will affect the others.
If it is too sour, add something sweet or add a litle salt, depending on what your are preparing.
You add sugar to lemonade to cut the sourness of the lemons; same principle works with other things.
It it's too sweet, add somethng sour. Add citrus juice, vinegars, an acid.
If it is to salty, increase the amount of sweet and sour and it will alter the taste of the salt.
If it tastes bitter, changing the sweet, sour, salty tastes will change the bitter taste.
If it is too hot, you added too many peppers or too much hot sauce, adding a dairy product will help calm the heat. That's why dished that have added heat usually have a dairy product accompaning it.
