Title:
Oak Smoked Beef Brisket Recipe
Image:
https://images.media-allrecipes.com/images/79590.png
Ingredients:
3-5 pounds Beef Bisket (flat cut)
1 cup Cowboy Steak Rub
1 cup minced onion
2 cups beef both
1 cup Barbeque Sauce

Directions:
The night before, trim excess fat to approximatly 1/4 to 3/8 inch thick. Rub the brisket thouroughly with the Cowboy Steak Rub. Wrap in plastic wrap and refrigerate overnight.
6-7 hours before serving, prepare smoker with Oak and bring to 150 to 180 degrees. Place brisket, fat side up and smoke for two hours.
Meanwhile add beef broth, bbq sauce and onion to a saucepan and heat to make a thin mop.
4 hours before serving, raise temperature to 250-290 degrees. Place brisket in a foil pan and generously mop with the mop sauce. Cover with foil and return to smoker.
Contunue to add mop sauce as a braising liquid as necessary to keep brsket moist and maintain approximatly 1/2 inch of liquid in the pan.
After 6-7 hours on the smoker, remove pan from heat and let cool for 30 minutes to allow juices to redistribute.
Remove brisket and cut into 1/4 inch slices.

Beef Brisket cooked with Oak. Slow smoked all day for an extremely tender cut of beef
