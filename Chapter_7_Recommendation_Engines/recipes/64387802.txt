Title:
Lion’s Head Meatballs Recipe
Image:
https://images.media-allrecipes.com/images/79590.png
Ingredients:
1 cup of “lite” coconut milk
• 2 1/2 tablespoons of reduced-sodium soy sauce
• 1 tablespoon of curry powder
• 1 pound of lean ground pork or beef
• 1/2 cup of chopped scallions
• 1/4 cup of minced leek, white and pale green part only
• 2 tablespoons of cornstarch
• 1 tablespoon of all-purpose flour
• 1 tablespoon of toasted sesame oil
• 1 tablespoon of finely chopped fresh ginger
• 2 teaspoons of seeded and minced fresh chile pepper
• 1/2 teaspoon of salt
• 1/4 teaspoon of ground white pepper
• 2 teaspoons of extra-virgin olive oil
• 1 medium head of Boston or iceberg lettuce
• 1/4 cup of chopped fresh basil or Thai basil
• 1 tablespoon of freshly grated lemon zest

Directions:
Combine coconut milk, soy sauce and curry powder in a large saucepan. Set aside.
2. Place pork (or beef), scallions, leek, cornstarch, flour, sesame oil, ginger, chile, salt and pepper in a large mixing bowl. Knead by hand until thoroughly combined and the mixture becomes sticky. Divide into 10 equal portions, about 1/4 cup each. Roll each portion into
3. a ball.
4. Heat olive oil in a large nonstick skillet over medium heat, swirling to coat the sides. Add the meatballs and cook, turning occasionally, until browned on all sides, 8 to 10 minutes. Transfer to a plate lined with paper towels.
5. Bring the coconut-milk mixture to a boil over medium-high heat. Add the meatballs; cover, reduce heat to low and cook for 8 minutes.
6. Line a serving bowl with lettuce leaves. Arrange the meatballs on top. Garnish with basil and lemon zest. Serve hot with the coconut-milk sauce drizzled over the top or on the side for dipping.

Submitter: 
Jill Sander
Description: 
This is a famous dish along the Yangtze, with regional variations. In Shanghai, the sauce will be sweet and sour. In Chongqing, it will be hot and spicy. And in Wuhan...well, it will depend on the season and the mood of the chef.
