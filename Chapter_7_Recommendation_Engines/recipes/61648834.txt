Title:
Healthier Downeast Maine Pumpkin Bread Recipe
Image:
https://images.media-allrecipes.com/userphotos/250x250/281261.jpg
Ingredients:
1 (15 ounce) can pumpkin puree
4 eggs
1/2 cup unsalted butter, melted
1 1/2 cups low-fat buttermilk
1 1/2 cup packed brown sugar
2 cups white whole wheat flour
1 1/2 cups all-purpose flour
2 teaspoons baking soda
1 1/2 teaspoons salt
1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
1 teaspoon ground nutmeg
1/2 teaspoon ground cloves
1/4 teaspoon ground ginger
2/3 cup raisins
2/3 cups pumpkin seeds
raw sugar for sprinkling on top (optional)

Directions:
Preheat oven to 350 degrees F (175 degrees C). Grease and flour three 7 x 3 inch loaf pans or place 24 paper liners in muffin pan cups.
In a large bowl, mix together pumpkin puree, eggs, butter, buttermilk and brown sugar until well blended. Combine dry ingredients, then stir into pumpkin mixture until just blended. Gently stir in raisins and pumpkin seeds. Pour into prepared pans or muffin cups. Lightly sprinkle tops with raw sugar if desired.
Bake loaves for about 50 minutes in the preheated oven. Loaves are done when toothpick inserted in center comes out clean. For muffins, bake about 25 minutes.

Submitter: 
MakeItHealthy
Description: 
We updated this great old Maine recipe to be healthier by using white whole wheat flour, less sugar, and butter instead of oil. We also added pumpkin seeds and raisins. It's still moist and spicy, and wonderful for healthy holiday gift giving as bread or muffins!Modified from: Downeast Maine Pumpkin Bread
