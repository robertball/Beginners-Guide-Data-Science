Title:
Puerto Rican 1-2-3-4 Cake Recipe
Image:
https://images.media-allrecipes.com/images/79590.png
Ingredients:
1 cup of evaporated milk
2 sticks of butter
2 cups of granulated sugar
3 cups of self-rising flour (sifted)
4 eggs (separated)
optional - vanilla and or almond extract to taste

Directions:
Preheat oven to 350 degrees. Separate eggs.
Beat the butter and sugar at high speed until pale and fluffy.
Add egg yolks one at a time  and flavoring, until well mixed
Lower speed of mixer and add flour and milk, alternating by starting and ending with flour.
Whisk egg whites to stiff peaks then fold into the batter.
Pour into cake pans (2 9x2 in round pans, 3 9x1 inch round pans or a 9x13 inch rectangular pan) and bake until a tooth pick inserted in the middle comes out clean.
Remove from oven and cool. When cool prick the top of the cake with a fork or a tooth pick and brush or spray with soaking syrup. Set aside for four hours or overnight until the cake has absorbed the liquid. Frost as desired.

Submitter: 
MKCortes (Latin Goddess)
Description: 
This is a basic yellow cake recipe that serves as the basis for many Puerto Rican desserts. The result is a light and fluffy cake that is very buttery and delicious!
