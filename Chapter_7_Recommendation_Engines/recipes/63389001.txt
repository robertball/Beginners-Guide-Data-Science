Title:
Chile Verde Recipe
Image:
https://images.media-allrecipes.com/images/79590.png
Ingredients:
2 Tbsp vegetable oil
3 lbs pork tenderloin or lean pork butt cut into 1-1/2" cubes, or 3 lbs of boneless/skinless chicken thighs
2 medium white onions, thinly sliced
3 cloves garlic, minced
1-1/2 tsp salt or to taste
1-1/2 tsp ground cumin or to taste
1-1/2 tsp dried leaf oregano or to taste
8 small fresh husked or 1 cup drained canned tomatillos, finely chopped
3 or 4 fresh Anaheim chilies or canned green chilies, seeded, de-veined and finely chopped
1 large tomato, peeled and coarsely chopped
1/4 cup fresh cilantro
2 tsp lime juice
1 cup chicken broth
Quartered limes, sliced radishes, chopped cilantro and toasted almond slivers for garnish

Directions:
Heat oil in a 6 quart dutch oven.
Add 1/3 of pork in single layer, cook till brown. Set aside and repeat with balance of pork.
Saving 2 tbsp of drippings in dutch oven, add onions and garlic and saute until soft.
Stir in salt, cumin and oregano.
Add tomatillos, chilies, tomato, cilantro.
Stir in chicken broth.
Heat to boil, cover, then reduce to simmer for 1-1/2 to 2 hours or until pork is tender.  If using chicken reduce cooking time to 1 hour.
Uncover dutch oven and continue to cook on a low boil until sauce is thickened.
Stir in lime juice and serve immediately over rice.

Submitter: 
Bill Alexander
Description: 
Pork Tenderloin cubed and slowly cooked to tender perfection in a a tomatillo and chile sauce. Yum!  You can also substitute boneless and skinless chicken thighs.  It is delicious either way.  From the Editors of Consumer Guide Mexican Cooking Class Cook Book.
