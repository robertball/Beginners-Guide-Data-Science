Title:
Jen's Spicy Turkey Chili Recipe
Image:
https://images.media-allrecipes.com/images/79590.png
Ingredients:
3 T. olive oil
1 lb ground turkey
1 green pepper, chopped
1 zucchini, sliced and chopped
1 large onion, chopped
4 oz. mushrooms, chopped
1 small stalk celery, sliced
1 15 oz. can black beans, rinsed and drained
28 oz can diced tomatoes (I love Muir Glen's Fire Roasted tomatoes)
2 c. tomato sauce
1 jalapeno pepper, finely chopped
2 t. chili powder
1 t. cumin
salt and pepper

Directions:
Heat 1 T. olive oil in a skillet.
Add ground turkey, salt and pepper, and cook until browned.
Place browned turkey into slow cooker.
Add the remaining 2 T. of olive oil to skillet.
Lightly brown the onion, pepper, mushroom, and celery.
Add vegetables to the slow cooker.
Add the beans, diced tomatoes, tomato sauce, jalapeno pepper, chili powder and cumin to the slow cooker and mix well.
Cook on high for 4 hours or low for 8 hours.

Submitter: 
Jen Cardenas
Description: 
This spicy yet healthy chili is full of veggies.  You can make it less spicy by using less chili powder and jalapeno pepper.  I serve it on a bed of white rice.  You can also top it with cheese or sour cream.  Enjoy!
