Title:
Herdez Zesty Chili with Guajillo Chile Sauce Recipe
Image:
https://images.media-allrecipes.com/userphotos/250x250/1004918.jpg
Ingredients:
1 tablespoon olive oil
1 10-oz strip steak, diced
salt and pepper to taste
1/2 onion, chopped
1/2 cup chopped red pepper
1 cube tomato bouillon
2 cups hot water
1 cup beef broth
2 tablespoons tamarind paste
1 tablespoon brown sugar
1 cup Herdez Guajillo Chile Sauce
2 tablespoons unsweetened cocoa powder
1 teaspoon ground cumin
1/8 teaspoon cinnamon
1 cup cooked rice
1 (14-ounce) can red kidney beans, drained and rinsed

Directions:
Lightly season steak with salt and pepper.
Heat oil over medium-high heat in a small skillet. Sear beef cubes and set aside.
Add onions and peppers to same skillet. Saut√© until tender.
Dissolve tomato bouillon in hot water.
Transfer vegetables to medium saucepan. Add bouillon, beef broth, Herdez Guajillo Chile sauce, tamarind and brown sugar.
Simmer for 15 minutes, until vegetables are soft and flavors are melded.
Reserve 2 tablespoons of vegetables from the saucepan, placing them with the steak.
Blend the remaining vegetables and stock, creating a thick soup.
Return puree to saucepan. Stir in the steak and reserved vegetables along with the cocoa, cumin and cinnamon.
Bring soup to a gentle boil. Lower heat and simmer for 45 minutes.
Add beans and rice. Simmer an additional 15 minutes, or until beans are heated through.

Description: 
Warm up when the weather's cold with this unique chili soup. Tamarind is not a traditional ingredient for chili, but it gives this Herdez dish a zesty bite. Serve with warm corn bread for a tasty comfort food meal.
