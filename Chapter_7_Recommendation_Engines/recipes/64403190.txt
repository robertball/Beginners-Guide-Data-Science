Title:
Maple, Mustard and Balsamic Pork Tenderloin Recipe
Image:
https://images.media-allrecipes.com/images/79590.png
Ingredients:
1 - pork tenderloin about 1 lb
2 T - vegetable oil
2 - carrots sliced
1 - leek, white and green parts only, halved and sliced
2 - cloves of garlic, minced
1 t - dried rosemary crumbled
1/2 t - salt
1/4 t - pepper
1/3 c - maple syrup
1/4 c - sodium reduced chicken broth
2 T - grainy mustard
1 t - balsamic vinegar
1/2 t cornstarch

Directions:
Cut pork tenderloin into 1 inch thick slices.  In a large non stick skillet, heat half the oil over medium high heat, brown pork slices on both sides, transfer to plate and set aside
in same skillet, heat remaining oil over medium heat, cook carrots, leek, garlic, rosemary, salt and pepper until leeks are softened, about 5 minutes.  Add maple syrup, broth, mustard and vinegar, bring to a boil.
Add pork, reduce heat, cover and simmer, stirring occasionally until juices run clear, about 20 minutes
in small bowl, stir in cornstarch and 1 t cold water until smooth, stir into skillet and bring to boil, reduce heat and simmer until thickened, stirring constantly
