Title:
Mandarin Chicken Chow Mein Udon Recipe
Image:
https://images.media-allrecipes.com/images/79590.png
Ingredients:
8 oz chow mein udon
3 chicken breasts
1 mandarin orange
2 tbs lemon juice
2 tbs rice vinegar
1 tbs soy sauce
3 cloves of garlic
2 tbs olive oil
1 medium sweet onion
1 yellow bell pepper
1 carrot
2 cloves of garlic; minced.
2 cups fresh broccoli florets
1 small can mixed stir fry vegetables, drained of liquid.
(mushrooms, bean sprouts, bamboo shoots and water chestnuts)
2 tbs peanut butter
2 tbs Teriyaki sauce

Directions:
Cut chicken breasts into bite sized cubes and place into a ziploc bag.
Cut the mandarin orange in half and squeeze the juice into the bag.
To the bag add lemon juice, rice vinegar and soy sauce.
Smash the garlic with the side of a knife and remove the husk. Add to chicken/marinade mixture.
Close the bag tightly and mix the marinade into the chicken. Place into refrigerator and let rest for one hour.
Chop onion and pepper into large chunks.
Grate carrot into small shreds.
In a large skillet or wok heat olive oil over high heat.
Once oil is hot, add the chicken and marinade.  Bring to a boil- cook for about 6 minutes; uncovered stirring occasionally.
While chicken is cooking, bring a pot of water to a boil and add chow mein udon.  Cook for 6 minutes and drain.
To skillet add onion, pepper, carrot and garlic.  Reduce heat to medium and cover.  Cook an additional 6 minutes.
Add broccoli florets cover and continue cooking 3 minutes.
Add the mixed vegetables and mix well.
Next add the peanut butter and Teriyaki Sauce- mix together.
Once the sauce is mixed well and the canned vegetables are heated through add the chow mein udon.
Heat until noodles are hot.
Serve immediately.
