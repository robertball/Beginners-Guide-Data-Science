Title:
Real Philly CheeseSteak Sandwich Recipe
Image:
https://images.media-allrecipes.com/images/79590.png
Ingredients:
1 pound beef round roast chipped -shaved
1-2 tablespoons olive oil
1/2 teaspoon salt
1/4 teaspoon black pepper
1/4 teaspoon onion powder
1/4 teaspoon garlic powder
1 small onion, sliced thin
6-8 mushrooms sliced
1 Italian frying pepper, sweet or hot sliced
3 ounces Provolone, Gryuere, American cheese thinly sliced or "cheese whiz"
4 Hoagie rolls, slit halfway trough lengthwise, gut rolls and toast if desired

Directions:
Usually done on large grills, but good results can be had with x-large frying pan.
Have all ingredients room temp and ready to cook.
Heat  preferably griddle/flat grill or heavy  x-large non-stick frying pan  on med-high to high heat
Add oil, onions, mushrooms and peppers and saute 5 min.
Add chipped beef to pan, season with salt, pepper, onion and garlic powders
IMPORTANT! With two spatulas, shred meat as it cooks flipping and shredding on high heat
When meat is not pink anymore, and liquids are mostly boiled away spread mixture evenly in pan and cover with cheese.
Cover with lid and lower heat, just till cheese starts to melt.
Divide between the 4 rolls, and serve with ketchup and hot Italian oil peppers.

Submitter: 
Steve Fairhurst
Description: 
I've lived in the philadelphia area for over 40 years
Some of the best steaks were from street vender trucks. This is how I've seen them do it at most places. Some places don't shed and chop meat, but I prefer this method. I prefer ProvoloneModified from: Philly Steak Sandwich
