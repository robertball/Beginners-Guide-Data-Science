Title:
Meatballs, Criniti Style Recipe
Image:
https://images.media-allrecipes.com/images/79590.png
Ingredients:
6 cups dried bread cubes
Milk to cover crumbs
1 pound ground beef chuck (or a mix or beef, pork and veal)
1/3 cup finely chopped yellow onions
4 TBS freshly grated Parmesan or Romano cheese
2 cloves garlic crushed
1/2 tsp dried marjoram or basil
2 TBS chopped fresh parsley
2 tsp salt
1 tsp freshly ground black pepper
2 eggs, beaten
2 TBS olive oil
Tomato Sauce
Sliced Mozzarella

Directions:
Place dried breadcrumbs in a bowl and cover with cold milk.  Let soak and squeeze to drain with your hands.
Place squeezed bread crumbs in a large bowl with the remaining ingredients (except the sauce and mozzarella), and mix with your hands.
Place mixture in a food processor with 2 TBS of olive oil and process mixture until very fine, almost emulsified.  Scraping down sides.  May have to do in two batches.
Return all to big bowl and form into golf ball sized meatballs.
Place in a preheated oven and bake at 375 degrees for 25 minutes.
Once cooked place in a baking dish, cover with tomato sauce and top with sliced mozzarella.  Bake again at 375 degrees until bubbly and lightly browned on top.

Submitter: 
Fantasy Gardener and Chef
Description: 
Criniti's of Philadelphia, a small Sicilian restaurant makes these unusual meatballs by grinding the meat so fine it is incredible.  Enjoy!
