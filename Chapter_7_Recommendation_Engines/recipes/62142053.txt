Title:
Southwestern Cornbread Casserole Recipe
Image:
https://images.media-allrecipes.com/images/79590.png
Ingredients:
1 15oz can pinto or black beans in seasoned sauce
1 small can chopped black olives
2 tablespoons minced fresh garlic
1 sweet red bell pepper, chopped
1/4 teaspoon dried oregano
1/4 teaspoon dried basil
1 cup cornmeal
1/2 cup plus 1 tablespoon unbleached flour or whole wheat flour
1 tablespoon baking powder
1/2 teaspoon powdered chicken bouillon
2 teaspoons sugar
1 16.5oz can no-salt-added creamed-style corn
1 can mexicorn, drained well
1/2 teaspoon smoked paprika
1/4 teaspoon chili powder
1 large egg
1 egg white
1 4oz can mild green chilies
1 cup low-fat shredded cheddar or colby-jack cheese

Directions:
Pre-heat oven to 375 degrees f.
Combine beans with liquid, black olives, garlic, green peppers, oregano and basil in a large 2 to 3 quart baking dish.
Combine cornmeal, flour, baking powder, bouillon and sugar, mixing well.
In separate bowl cream together creamed corn, mexicorn, paprika, chili powder, egg, egg white and green chilies.
Blend into dry ingredients just until dry mix is moistened.
Spoon mixture over top of bean mixture. Sprinkle top with shredded cheese.
Bake for 25 to 35 minutes.
Let stand to set for 10 minutes before serving.

Submitter:

Description: 
This is one of my fall favorites. It's quick and easy to prepare, and is a healthy one dish meal at less than 300 calories per serving. Unbelievably delicious.
