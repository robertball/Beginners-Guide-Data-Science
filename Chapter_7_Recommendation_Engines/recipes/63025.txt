Title:
Pulled Pork Barbecue Recipe
Image:
https://images.media-allrecipes.com/userphotos/250x250/984413.jpg
Ingredients:
1 (16 ounce) whole pork tenderloin, all fat trimmed
1 teaspoon chili powder
1/2 teaspoon garlic powder
Vegetable cooking spray
1/2 cup finely chopped onion
1 1/2 teaspoons minced garlic
1 (14.5 ounce) can crushed tomatoes, undrained
1 tablespoon cider vinegar
1 tablespoon prepared mustard
1 teaspoon chili powder, or to taste
1/4 teaspoon maple extract
1/4 teaspoon liquid smoke
1/3 cup Equal® Spoonful*
Salt and pepper
6 multigrain hamburger buns, toasted

Directions:
Rub pork with 1 teaspoon chili powder and garlic powder; place in baking pan. Bake in preheated 425 degrees F oven 30 to 40 minutes or until pork is well browned and juices run clear. Let stand 10 to 15 minutes. Cut into 2 to 3-inch slices; shred slices into bite-size pieces with fork.
Spray medium saucepan with cooking spray; heat over medium heat until hot. Saute onion and garlic about 5 minutes or until tender. Add tomatoes, vinegar, mustard, chili powder, maple extract, and liquid smoke to saucepan; heat to boiling.
Reduce heat and simmer, uncovered, 10 to 15 minutes or until medium sauce consistency. Stir in Equal(r). Season to taste with salt and pepper. Stir pork into sauce; cook 2 to 3 minutes or until hot. Spoon mixture into buns.

Submitter: 
EQUAL®
Description: 
Slow oven cooked pork in a homemade barbecue sauce combining tomatoes, garlic, onion, vinegar, mustard and chili powder with a hint of EQUAL® sweetness. Serve on crusty whole grain rolls for a real taste treat.
