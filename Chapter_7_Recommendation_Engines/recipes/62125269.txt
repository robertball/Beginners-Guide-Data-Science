Title:
Heart Healthy Breakfast Muffins Recipe
Image:
https://images.media-allrecipes.com/images/79590.png
Ingredients:
1 C. old fashioned oats
1 C. white flour
1/2 C. whole wheat flour
3/4 tsp. baking powder
1/2 tsp. baking soda
3/4 tsp. cinnamon
1/2 tsp. fresh grated nutmeg
1/2 tsp. sea salt
1 C. unsweetened applesauce (any flavor)
2 egg whites
1/2 C. milk (2% or skim)
1/2 C. brown sugar
2 T. canola or safflower oil
2 tsp. vanilla extract
1 C. fresh berries (raspberries, blueberries or blackberries)        or substitute
2/3 C.dried cranberries, cherries, chopped apricots or chopped flavored dried plums for berries

Directions:
Preheat oven to 400F.
Line muffin pan with 12 paper or foil liners or lightly grease muffin cups if not using liners.
In one bowl, combine oats, flours, baking soda, baking powder, cinnamon, nutmeg, salt, applesauce, egg whites, milk, brown sugar, oil and vanilla.
Mix just until ingredients are blended.
Fold in berries or dried fruit.
The batter will be lumpy and thick.
Divide the batter among muffin cups.
Bake for 15 - 17 minutes or until done when tested with toothpick.
Let cool 5 minutes in pan and remove to cooling rack.

Description: 
This delicious muffin makes healthy eating fun. This muffin is low in fat, has good carbs and lots of fiber. It's great for heart health and wonderful for diabetics because of the oats,fiber and low fat. It's also not too heavy like most high fiber muffins and it tastes great. It just uses one bowl which make everyone's life easier!
