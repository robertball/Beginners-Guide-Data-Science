Title:
Old-Fashioned Apple Crisp Recipe
Image:
https://images.media-allrecipes.com/images/79590.png
Ingredients:
fruit mixture:
5lbs apples (fuji, granny smith, or macintosh) combo is best flavors
1 lemon (can use liquid if necessary)
1/2 cup granulated sugar
1tsp ground cinnamon
1/2 tsp ground nutmeg
topping:
1c all-purpose flour
3/4c granulated sugar
3/4c dark brown sugar
1tsp salt
1tsp ground cinnamon
1/2tsp ground nutmeg
1 1/2 cups old-fashioned oatmeal (do NOT substitute quick oats)
1/2lb (2sticks) cold butter, diced (not margarine)
vanilla ice cream

Directions:
preheat oven 375 F. butter 9x13 in baking dish
For the fruit mixture place the granulated sugar, spices, and grated zest and juice of the lemon in a large bowl.  Peel, core, and slice the apples and toss in the bowl with the other ingredients.  Pour the fruit mixture into the prepared baking dish.
For the topping mix the flour, sugars, and spices in a medium bowl.  Add the diced cold butter and cut into the flour mixture until crumbly.  Stir in the oatmeal.  Scatter topping evenly over the apples gently pinching some of the topping into clumps.
Bake at 375Â°F for about 45 minutes until the apples are tender and the topping is brown and crisp.
Serve warm topped with a scoop of vanilla ice cream.
