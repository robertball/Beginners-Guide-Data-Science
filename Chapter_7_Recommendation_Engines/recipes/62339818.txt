Title:
savory or sweet bannock (fried bread) Recipe
Image:
https://images.media-allrecipes.com/images/79590.png
Ingredients:
2cups all puropse flour
2T baking powder
1/8 tsp baking soda
1/8 tsp salt*
1/8 tsp white sugar
3T margarine or butter
3/4c milk (cold)
1 egg lightly beaten

Directions:
Mix together the flour, baking powder, baking soda, salt and sugar.
Cut in margerine or butter (as you would with pie crust)
* If you are making them Savory, add your favorite herbs eg, chopped cooked bacon, or ham, finley chopped fresh garlic, oregano, basil, green onions) Whatever best compliments your soup, pasta, or dip. you can add another 1/8th tsp of salt for salt lovers.
*If you are making them sweet, try adding dried coconut, raisins, or cinnamon. Also add 1/4c more sugar (you can use white or brown)
Make a well in the center and add the milk mixed with the egg. Use wooden spoon to begin mixing, then finish with your hands, till smooth and soft.
Roll out on lightly floured surface till 1/3 of an inch thick, cut into 8 triangles.
Deep Fry in fresh oil till golden, turning to fry both sides. (about 2-3 min per side)
Allow to drain on paper towel and serve warm! Enjoy!

Description: 
a tasty fried quick bread, with my own twist. 
you can make it in about 15 minutes! Great for serving with dips, soups, pastas or just eat alone for breakfast with some butter and or jam!
