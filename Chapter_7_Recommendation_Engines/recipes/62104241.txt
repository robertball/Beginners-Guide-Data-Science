Title:
Tennessee Mud Cake Recipe
Image:
https://images.media-allrecipes.com/images/79590.png
Ingredients:
4 eggs
2 cups granulated sugar
1 1/2 cups self rising flour
1/3 cup powdered cocoa
1 stick butter, melted
1 tsp salt
1 cup chopped black walnuts
1 cup flaked, sweetened coconut
1 tsp vanilla extract
7 oz marshmallow creme
Frosting
1 stick butter
6 tbsp milk
1/3 cup powdered cocoa
1 cup chopped black walnuts
8 oz cream cheese, softened
1 tsp vanilla extract
1 lb confectioner's sugar

Directions:
Preheat oven to 350 degrees.  Grease and flour or coat with cooking spray one 9x13 inch baking pan.
In larbe bowl, beat eggs.  Add sugar, beating on high until thick.
Add melted butter to egg and sugar mixture.
Sift together the flour, salt and cocoa powder.  Add slowly to egg and sugar mixture, beating on medium speed.
Stir in chopped walnuts, coconut and vanilla.
Pour into prepared pan.  Bake at 350 for 35-45 minutes, until cake resembles brownie and feels firm to the touch.
Spread hot cake with marshmallow creme.
Let set for 30 minutes.
Prepare icing as follows:
Mix together softened butter, 6 tbsp milk, 1/3 cup powdered cocoa, nuts, softened cream cheese, vanilla.  When thoroughly mixed, slowly add 1 lb powdered sugar.  Mix on medium speed until well blended.  Spread over marshmallow creme on cake.

Submitter: 
Amy
Description: 
This cake has the heavy, moist texture of a thick brownie.  It is incredibly dense and sweet -- kids love it, and adults do, too.  This is my aunt Susan's recipe, and it is a crowd pleaser at her church pot-lucks.
