Title:
Healthier Slow Cooker Beef Stew I Recipe
Image:
https://images.media-allrecipes.com/userphotos/250x250/280672.jpg
Ingredients:
2 pounds beef stew meat, cut into 1 inch cubes
1/4 cup all-purpose flour
1/2 teaspoon salt
1/2 teaspoon ground black pepper
4 cloves garlic, minced
1 bay leaf
1 teaspoon paprika
1 teaspoon Worcestershire sauce
1 onion, chopped
1 1/2 cups reduced-sodium beef broth
3 potatoes, diced
4 carrots, sliced
1 stalk celery, chopped
3/4 pound butternut squash, peeled and cut into small chunks
1/2 head escarole, washed and torn into bite-sized pieces

Directions:
Place meat in slow cooker. In a small bowl mix together the flour, salt, and pepper; pour over meat, and stir to coat meat with flour mixture. Stir in the garlic, bay leaf, paprika, Worcestershire sauce, onion, beef broth, potatoes, carrots, and celery, and butternut squash.
Cover, and cook on Low setting for 10 to 12 hours, or on High setting for 4 to 6 hours.
Stir in escarole. Cover and cook for 15 to 20 minutes longer or until tender.

Submitter: 
MakeItHealthy
Description: 
This hearty and delicious stew is perfect for the rainy days of fall. By adding more vegetables and using reduced-sodium beef broth I make this recipe extra healthy for my family. Increasing the amount of garlic also helps ward off any colds!Modified from: Slow Cooker Beef Stew I
