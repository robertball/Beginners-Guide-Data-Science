Title:
Spinach and Onion "Enchiladas" Recipe
Image:
https://images.media-allrecipes.com/images/79590.png
Ingredients:
2 TBS olive oil
1 med. onion, chopped
2 cloves garlic, chopped
1 10-oz package frozed chopped spinach, thawed, drained and squeezed dry.  (May use fresh spinach)
1/3 cup cilantro, chopped
2 cups shredded jack cheese
1 tsp cumin
1 14-oz can medium heat fire roasted enchilada sauce
7 6-inch corn tortillas
1 cup frozen corn kernels, thawed
Sour cream and chopped green onions for garnish

Directions:
Preheat oven to 425 degrees.  In a medium skillet, heat oil and saute onion and garlic for 2-3 minutes, until just tender.  Stir in spinach, cilantro, 1 cup cheese and cumin; stir until cheese melts.
Coat an 8x8-inch baking dish with cooking spray.  Pour 1/2 cup enchilada sauce in dish.  Place 4 tortillas in dish, overlapping each other and edging up dish sides.  Spread spinach mixture evenly over tortillas; top with corn and salt and pepper.  Top with remaining 3 tortillas.  Pour remaining enchilada sauce over all and sprinkle with remaining 1 cup cheese.  Bake 20 minutes, until bubbly and lightly browned.  Cool slightly before cutting; garnish with sour cream and green onions.

Description: 
Everyone seems to want this recipe whenever I serve it or bring it to a pot luck!  Assembling ingredients in layers reduces time and effort.
