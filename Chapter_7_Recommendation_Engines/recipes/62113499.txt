Title:
20th Century Pie from the 20th Century Restuarant in Youngstown, OH Recipe
Image:
https://images.media-allrecipes.com/images/79590.png
Ingredients:
2 sticks margarine
1 C. sugar
2 squares unsweetened baker's chocolate
2 eggs
1/2 tsp. vanilla
3/4 C. chopped pecans or walnuts
1 9inch baked pie shell

Directions:
Toast the nuts lightly in the oven at 300 degrees for 10 minutes, checking periodically to lightly brown and cool.
In a large mixing bowl mix the margarine and sugar with an electric mixer.
Beat 5 minutes on high spead, occasionally scrape the sides of the mixing  bowl.
Melt the chocolate, cool then add to the sugar mixture and beat again for 5 minutes.
Add the first egg and beat for 5 more minutes on high spead.
Add the second egg and beat for 5 minutes.
Last of all, add the vanilla and beat for 5 minutes.
Fold in the nuts and pour into a baked pie shell.
Refrigerate for at least 2 hours

Submitter: 
PJ's kitchen
Description: 
There used to be a restaurant in our town that was famous for this pie. It's very rich and creamy. Just like eating chocolate custard with toasted pecans. Top with a dollop of whipped cream. Deliciously deadly!
