Title:
Sweet and Sour Chicken Recipe
Image:
https://images.media-allrecipes.com/images/79590.png
Ingredients:
2 medium chicken breasts cut into ~3/4 inch cubes or strips or appropriate bite size pieces
2 heaping tablespoons cornstarch
4 tablespoons flour
1 tsp garlic powder
1/2-1 tsp salt
Fresh ground pepper to taste
3 teaspoons sweet paprika
2-3 tablespoons  EVO
1/2 large White or red onion Vertically sliced in 1/4 inch slivers
1 large green pepper similarly sliced
2-3 carrots julienned
1 -1.5 cups pineapple chunks preferably fresh or at least 8 ounces and save the juice
salt/pepper to taste
Sauce:
1/2 cup sugar
1/3rd cup apple cider vinegar
1 cup water
reserved pineapple juice
2 tablespoons ketchup
dash or 3 of soy sauce
1/4 cup water
2 tablespoons  corn starch

Directions:
whisk cornstarch, flour, garlic powder salt, pepper and paprika together in a shallow bowl
dredge chicken pieces in cornstarch /flour mixture
heat 3 table spoons EVO in heavy large fry pan
Cook chicken pieces in 2 batches ~3 minutes a side till nicely browned
remove from pan and keep warm in oven
Make Sauce:
Mix sugar thru soy sauce in a medium saucepan till boiling and whisked smooth
mix water and cornstarch into a slurry and add to the sauce and allow to thicken.
Keep warm
In the same fry pan you cooked the chicken add 1 more tablespoon EVO and bring to a shimmer over medium high heat.
add the vegetables and season with salt and pepper.
Stir fry till veg is crisp tender and onions are wilted.
add chicken and appropriate amount of sauce and stir together.
Serve over white rice

Submitter: 
Baron
Description: 
Chinese restaurants have ceased putting any effort into sweet and sour dishes.  They are just not the same as they were in the 60's and 70's.  (Yes I know it's not a classic Chinese dish)  Here's my take on a classic Sweet and sour Chicken
