Title:
Clam Chowder; Red (Manhattan Style) Recipe
Image:
https://images.media-allrecipes.com/images/79590.png
Ingredients:
3 doz; Clams (in shell or canned okay) ... cut into .5 inch pieces
2.5 cups; Clam Broth or Clam Juice
2 TBL; Vegetable Oil
6 slices; Bacon ... cut into 1/2" pieces
1 cup; Onion ... chopped
1/2 cup; Green Pepper ... chopped
1/2 cup; Celery ... chopped
2 cups; Potatoes ... white, with our without skin, cut into 1/2" pieces
2.5 cups; Tomatoes ... chopped with juice retained
2 TBL; Basil ... fresh, chopped fine
2 TBL; Parsley ... flat leaf, fresh, chopped fine
Kosher Salt ... to taste
Black Pepper ... fresh ground, to taste
2 tsp; Tobasco Sauce ... or to taste / optional

Directions:
In a heavy-bottom pot, heat the Oil over moderate-low heat.  Add Bacon and cook over medium heat until cooked through.
Add, together, Onions, Pepper, & Celery.  Stir ingredients together.  Reduce heat and continue stirring until vegetables soften.
Add Potatoes.  Stir ingredients together.  Continue to simmer, uncovered, 3-5 minutes.
Add Clam Broth.  If using fresh Clams, retain broth from cooking, return shells to broth after removing Clams, simmer until slightly thickened.  If using canned Clams, combine broth from can with bottled Clam Juice.
Add Tomatoes, with juice (juice).  Reduce heat to simmer, cover, and cook 12 minutes.
Fold in Basil & continue to simmer, uncovered, 5 minutes.
Transfer complete mixture to a slow cooker (if available).
After Clams have cooled, cut into 1/2" pieces.  Add to mixture along with empty shells.  Add Parsley, Salt, Petter, & Tobasco.  Cook on lowest heat setting for 1 hour (up to 3 hours).
Serve with Oyster Crackers.

This is a great winter-time dish.  I prefer to serve it as a main dish ... thus allowing guests to graze of dinger-food appetizers, and still have room for dessert.
