Title:
Pork pozole Recipe
Image:
https://images.media-allrecipes.com/images/79590.png
Ingredients:
1 1/2 pounds cubed pork shoulder (for stew)
1 tbsp olive oil
4 cups fat free, low sodium chicken broth
4 dried pasilla chilies or ancho chilies
1 garlic clove
1/4 tsp. black pepper
1/4 tsp. oregano
1/4 tsp. salt (or to taste)
Pinch of ground cumin
15 oz. can hominy
1/2 onion, quartered
1 lime, quartered
2 small radishes, chopped
1/2 onion, chopped
1 jalapeno, seeded and chopped
1/2 cup cilantro, minced
2 cups iceberg lettuce (or cabbage), shredded

Directions:
Remove stems and seeds from dried chilies.
Boil dried chilies and garlic clove in about 2 cups of water until softened.
Blend the chilies, garlic clove, remaining water, black pepper, oregano and cumin.
Meanwhile, brown cubed pork in olive oil over medium heat.
Add chicken broth and about 2 qts. more water.
Add chili sauce and salt. Bring to a boil.
Add quartered onion. Lower heat, cover and simmer for 2-3 hours.
Add hominy during last hour of cooking.
Serve and add toppings to your liking (lime juice, radishes, jalapeno, onion, cilantro and lettuce or cabbage).

Submitter: 
Jenna
Description: 
I missed my grandma's pozole and finally recreated her recipe with great results. Not too spicy, but definitely not bland! The soup is usually accompanied by corn tostadas.
