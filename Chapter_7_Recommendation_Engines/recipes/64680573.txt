Title:
Hoisin Pork And Broccoli Stir-Fry Recipe
Image:
https://images.media-allrecipes.com/userphotos/250x250/4881261.jpg
Ingredients:
1 pound pork tenderloin cut into chunks
2 tablespoon hoisin sauce
1 tablespoon seamane oil
1 onion halfed and split apart
2 cloves garlic minced
2 tablespoons ginger root minced
1 cup chicken stock
1 tablespoon cornstarch
1 cup broccoli
1 garden corn on cop chopped and blanched
1 garden tomato chopped
1/2 (4 ounce can) drained sliced water chestnuts
1/4 (15 ounce can) drained straw mushrooms
1 cup white rice cooked
oyster sauce and white pepper to taste

Directions:
Marinate pork in hoisin sauce for at least 2 hours or overnight in refridgerator.
Heat sesame oil in a large wok or saute pan. Stir onion, garlic, and ginger root for a few minutes and then add pork and cook another 8 minutes or until pork is brown.
Add 1/2 chicken broth to stir fry. Stir in broccoli and cook/toss until broccoli begins to wilt.
Mix cornstarch and chicken broth in a small bowl and add to stir fry. Stir corn, tomatoe, water chestnuts, and mushrooms. Cook and stir until thickened and bubbly.
Scoop some white rice on a plate, sprinkle with white pepper and drizzle oyster sauce, then topped with stir-fry.

Submitter: 
James
Description: 
A summertime pork stir-fry with garden corn and tomatoes in a fragrant marinated sauce. As long as you have a pan or wok and the right kind of oil, you can experiment with any combination of vegetables.
