Title:
Friendswood Fire Chili Recipe
Image:
https://images.media-allrecipes.com/userphotos/250x250/2805767.jpg
Ingredients:
2 lbs. coarse ground beef chili meat
1 lb. breakfast sausage
1 19oz. can of Horner Chili (no beans)
1/2 lb. bacon(cut up into small pieces)
1 large yellow onion(diced)
4 habaneros
3 jalapeños
2 cloves garlic(minced)
14 oz. canned whole peeled tomatoes (drained)
16 oz. canned tomato sauce
28 oz.  canned diced chili tomatoes
3 cans black chili beans (15 oz. each)
1 (12 fl. oz.) beer
4-1/2 tablespoons chili powder
3 tablespoons flour
3 tablespoons beef bouillon
1-1/2 teaspoon ground cumin
1 tablespoon cayenne pepper
1 tablespoon black pepper
1 tablespoon season salt
1 tablespoon carne guisada seasoning

Directions:
Cut off the stems and of the top part of the habaneros and jalapeños and throw into a pot of boiling water. Boil for about 10 minutes. Carefully remove peppers from water and dice. Save water pepperes were boiled in. Set aside.
Cook bacon over medium high heat until browned, right before it becomes crunchy.  Drain and set aside.
Cook beef and pork seasoned with black pepper and season salt with diced onion until browned. Drain.
Add chili powder, flour, cayenne pepper, cumin,  carne guisada seasoning, and beef bouillon to the browned beef and pork. Pour the water used to boil the peppers with into the pot until it covers about half of the meat. Stir well.
Add can of chili, diced peppers, all tomatoes,  tomato sauce, beans, bacon, garlic, and beer.
Simmer for approximately 30 minutes.
