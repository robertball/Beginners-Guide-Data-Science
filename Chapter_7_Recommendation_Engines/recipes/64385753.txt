Title:
Hamusta Soup Recipe
Image:
https://images.media-allrecipes.com/images/79590.png
Ingredients:
1 large potato
2 stalks celery
2 carrots
2 zucchini (small)
3 lemons juice (about 9 TBS)
4-5 leaves Swiss chard (Mangold in Hebrew)
1 TBS sugar (optional)
1 TBS salt
1 head garlic broken in 2
1 1/2 liters water

Directions:
Take the head of garlic separated in 2 and peel it, and press lightly on each clove with the edge of a knife.
Peel the potato and cut in cubes.
Peel the carrots and cut in slices.
Cut the zucchini in quarters in thick pieces.
Wash the Swiss chard well, take out the heart in the middle and cut the Swiss chard in slices of a cm.
Separate the celery stalks from the leaves. Cut the stalks in 1/2 cm pieces.  Cut the leaves.
Put the garlic, potato, celery, chard, carrots, zucchini, lemon juice, salt and sugar in the pot. Add the water. Bring it to boil over a medium flame, Turn down the flame and cook, almost boiling, for 15 minutes, until the vegetables are soft and you can poke them with a fork.
Add kubbeh and continue cooking in an uncovered pot for 30 minutes.  It is important that it boils down and is not too watery.

Submitter: 
Linda
Description: 
This is an Iraqi Kurdish soup that is very popular in Israel.
