Title:
Suegra Hilda's Mexican Rice Recipe
Image:
https://images.media-allrecipes.com/images/79590.png
Ingredients:
4 Cups Rice (cook 10 min. extra if using brown rice.)
6 cloves garlic, separated
~2 Tbsp olive oil
2 slices of large onion
4 strips bacon (optional)
10 roma tomatoes, grilled
1/2-1/3 Cup vegetable oil (to cover rice)
8 Cups hot water
4 Tbsp chicken boulion
salt to taste
6-8 raw baby carrots (optional)

Directions:
Rinse rice twice in lukewarm water.
Drain thoroughly and place in sun to dry while preparing next step.
Sauté 1 clove garlic on med-high heat in olive oil. Add onion and bacon (if using). Sauté until the onion is somewhat translucent. Take onion out of skillet and put into blender. Set aside.
Lower tempurature to med. Add vegetable oil and rice.
Brown rice. Drain oil from pan.
Remove skin and seeds from tomatoes. Place in bender with sautéd onions. Add remaining 5 cloves of garlic. Blend well.
Add 8 cups water and tomatoe mixture to drained rice. (If using carrots, add once liquid is boiling.)
Allow rice to cook until most of the liquid is absorbed.
Add boulion and stir around edges of pan. Cover.
Cook for 10 more minutes.

Submitter: 
Marianne Jones Islas
Description: 
This is my mother-in-law's recipe. She is from Culiacán, Sinaloa, Mexico. I wrote it down while she made it since she never works from a recipe.
