Title:
Sauerbraten Recipe
Image:
https://images.media-allrecipes.com/userphotos/250x250/702694.jpg
Ingredients:
2 1/2 lbs boneless chuck roast
For marinade
1/2 teaspoon ground black pepper
2 onions, sliced or chopped
1 carrot, sliced
1 stalk celery, chipped
4 whole cloves Or 1/2 teaspoons of cinnamon
4 whole black peppercorns
1 cup of red wine vinegar
2 bay leaves
4 cups of water
When Cooking
6 tablespoons of butter
1 1/2 teaspoons of salt
4 tablespoons of all-purpose flour
1 tablespoon of sugar
1/8 teaspoon ground ginger
1/8 teaspoon ground clove Or cinnamon

Directions:
Sprinkle meat with pepper. place in a Non-aluminum bowl. add onions, carrot, celery, cloves or cinnamon, peppercorns, vinegar, and bay leaves, add water to cover meat. cover and refrigerate for 4 days.
on fifth day, remove from the marinade. heat 2 tablespoons of butter in a dutch oven Or 5 Qt Saute Pan. add meat and brown on all sides. sprinkle with salt. add marinade.
heat to simmering. cover and simmer for 2 to 2 1/2 hours. until meat is fork tender.
melt remaining butter in pan, stir in flour. add sugar, ginger, and cloves or cinnamon. ladle in 2 cups liquid from simmered meat. cook, stirring, until thickened. remove meat to heated platter. serve with the sauce.
serve with Spaetzle, potato dumplings, cooked carrots, cabbage.

Submitter: 
Mark Farmer
Description: 
A Spicy marinade flavors this classic German method of preparing less tender cuts of meat. Be sure to use a NON-aluminum container for the meat and marinade
