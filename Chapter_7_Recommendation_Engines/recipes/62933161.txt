Title:
Sorullitos de Maiz (Cornmeal/Cheese Sticks) Recipe
Image:
https://images.media-allrecipes.com/userphotos/250x250/718320.jpg
Ingredients:
2 cups of pre-cooked finely ground yellow corn meal (also called masarepa)
1/4 cup of all purpose flour
1 teaspoon of baking powder
1 teaspoon of salt
1 teaspoon of black pepper
1/2 teaspoon of cinnamon
1/2 teaspoon of paprika
1 tablespoon of  sugar
1/4 cup of butter or shortening
1 cup of water
1/2 cup of milk
1/2 cup shredded cheese (in Puerto Rico they use mild cheddar cheese known as Queso de Papa or Bola) -

Directions:
Measure out the dry ingredients in a bowl.
Cut in shortening until the mixture resembles peas.
In a medium saucepan heat the water and milk to a simmer. Remove from heat.
Add to cornmeal mixture and stir rapidly forms a soft ball that separates from the sides of the bowl. Add the cheese and stir.
Cover with clean dishcloth and let rest for 5 minutes.
Form the sorullitos by scooping about a heaping teaspoon, forming a ball first, then rolling on your hand into a stick.
Fry in plenty of oil until golden.
Serve with Salsa Rosada or Ketchup.

Submitter: 
MKCortes (Latin Goddess)
Description: 
Cornmeal sticks or Sorullitos are a snack or appetizer that is popular amongst Hispanic Caribbean folk. In Puerto Rico they are commonly served as part of a cheese platter with Salsa Rosada (Puerto Rican Style homemade Thousand Island dipping sauce made with garlic, mayo, ketchup, worchestire sauce and cilantro) or Ajilimojili (garlic and sweet chili pepper dipping sauce).
