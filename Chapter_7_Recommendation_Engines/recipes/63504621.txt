Title:
Raj Style Kedgeree Recipe
Image:
https://images.media-allrecipes.com/images/79590.png
Ingredients:
2 large Eggs - hard boiled
450g/1lb Smoked Haddock fillets - all bones removed
1 Bay Leaf
180g/6oz Basmati Rice
Sea Salt
60g/2oz Ghee or Butter
Whole Milk - for poaching fish
1½ in/4cm fresh Ginger - grated
2oz/4tbsp Spring Onions - finely chopped
1 clove Garlic - finely chopped
1tbsp Red Pepper - finely chopped
2tbsp medium Curry Powder
2 Tomatoes - peeled, deseeded and chopped
1tsp Dijon Mustard
Handful fresh Coriander - torn and shredded
150ml dry white Wine
100ml hot Chicken Stock
½ Lemon - juice only
1 small fresh Red Chilli - seeds removed - finely chopped

Directions:
Cook the rice, drain, cool and set aside;
Boil the eggs for some 10 minutes or so, drain, cool and set aside;
Place the fish and bay leaves in a shallow pan and cover with half whole milk and half water;
Bring to the boil, cover and, reducing the heat, simmer for some 5 to 6 minutes or so or until fish just cooked through;
Remove from pan and leave to cool;
Remove the skin from fish, flake into biggish chunks and set aside;
Melt the butter in a pan over a low heat, add the ginger, onion, red pepper and garlic and cook for some 5 minutes or so to soften;
Add the curry powder and mustard, stir in well and cook for a further few minutes;
Add the chopped tomatoes, chicken stock, wine and lemon juice, stir in well and, increasing heat, bring to boil;
Reduce heat to gentle, add the fish and rice to the pan and, stirring gently, heat through;
Shell and Quarter the eggs;
Add the eggs, most of the coriander and the chilli and stir gently;
Serve on plates adding the rest of the coriander as garnish.

Submitter: 
Hasta Luego
Description: 
The British Raj breakfast special. Now makes a great lunch and/or dinner meal. With delicious piquant and smokey flavours combining with great effect, this is a rice dish to savour.
