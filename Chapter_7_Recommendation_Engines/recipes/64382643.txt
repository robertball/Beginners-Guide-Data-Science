Title:
Gluten Free Pork Belly Buns Recipe
Image:
https://images.media-allrecipes.com/images/79590.png
Ingredients:
FOR THE PORK BELLY
1 slab pork belly (about 2 pounds)
1 tablespoon cooking oil
2-3 cloves garlic, finely minced
1 tablespoon finely chopped fresh ginger
1 fresh chili pepper, minced (optional)
1 green onion, chopped
1/4 cup packed brown sugar
2 tablespoons rice vinegar
3 tablespoons Asian fish sauce
1 tablespoon soy sauce
1/2 cup water
For the Dough:
1 packet dry yeast (1 packet contains 7 grams of dry yeast)
2 tablespoons lukewarm water
1 teaspoon sugar plus 1/4 cup sugar, separated
1 1/4 cup cornstarch
1/2 cup tapioca starch
1/2 cup plus 2 tablespoons brown rice flour
1 teaspoon baking powder
1/2 cup warm water
1/2 cup warm milk
2 tablespoons butter or margarine (room temperature)

Directions:
Pork Belly:
Season pork belly with salt & pepper
Wrap tightly in 2 layers of tin foil
Roast for 2 hours at 275 degrees the let rest until cool enough to refrigerate.  Do not open, refrigerate for few hours or overnight.
Slice into 1/2" pieces
Heat up oil in pan and brown both sides.
Remove
Saute garlic, ginger, green onion in fat
whisk together fish sauce, soy sauce (gf), rice vinegar, green onion, garlic, ginger & fresh chili
Pour sauce into same pan and place pork belly back in pan, simmering for about 20 min, covered.
For Buns:
Place the yeast, 2 tbsp of warm water & tsp of sugar in bowl. Let sit until the yeast doubles in size & gets a foamy appearance. In separate bowl, combine the warm milk & warm water
Whisk together the remaining sugar, cornstarch, tapioca starch, brown rice flour & baking powder. Using wooden spoon, mix in butter & proofed yeast mixture, then slowly add the warm milk until soft, pliable dough
Place the dough inside large zip lock bag, place bag in a warm place for an hour, or until the dough has nearly doubled in size
Cut parchment or wax paper into 16 3-inch squares. Take dough out of the plastic bag and divide into 16 even pieces. Roll each piece into a ball, using cornstarch on your hands to prevent the dough from sticking. Take 1 ball of dough and flatten it in your palm to form a 4x3 oval and fold it over to make a bun.  Keeps the buns loosely covered until ready to cook.
To cook, put inch of water in the wok.  Place a steamer rack inside, plate on top of the rack & place the buns on parchment paper on plate and cover the wok with lid.  Steam for about 15 min.
