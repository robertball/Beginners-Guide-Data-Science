Title:
Fresh Cherry Shortcake Recipe
Image:
https://images.media-allrecipes.com/userphotos/250x250/665357.jpg
Ingredients:
SHORTCAKES:
2 cups all-purpose flour
1/2 teaspoon salt
2 teaspoons cream of tartar
1 teaspoon baking soda
4 tablespoons sugar + sugar for topping
1 tsp. ground cardamom
1 tsp. cinnamon
1/2 cup butter, frozen
1 egg, beaten
1 egg white (optional)
1/2 cup cold cream
CHERRIES:
6 cups bing cherries, pitted and split
6 tablespoon sugar
Zest of 2 lemons
TOPPING:
Whipping Cream
1 cup almonds, chopped
6 perfect cherries, with stems

Directions:
Preheat oven to 425 degrees.
Mix flour, salt, baking powder, spices and sugar in a medium bowl. Cut butter into flour mixture. Combine egg and half-and-half; pour into flour mixture. Toss with a fork to form large clumps. Add a teaspoon more cream to the bowl if dough won't come together.
Using an ice cream scoop, scoop dough into hand and gently form into a ball. Place on parchment covered baking sheet. Press lightly to form 1/2‚Äù discs. Brush tops with optional egg white and sprinkle with sugar. Bake until golden brown, about 12 to 14 minutes. Cool.
Combine pitted cherries, sugar and lemon zest. Let macerate for several hours.
When ready to serve, slice cakes in half horizontally, layer with a generous portion of cherries, almonds and cream. Top with dollop of cream, sprinkle with ground almonds and top with a single, perfect cherry.

I had a big bag of fresh bing cherries and wanted a fresh and different dessert for the July 4 weekend. Integrating unique spices, this twist on the traditional shortcake dessert is delish.
