Title:
Sourdough Biscuit Recipe Recipe
Image:
https://images.media-allrecipes.com/userphotos/250x250/797862.jpg
Ingredients:
1/2 cup sourdough starter
1 cup milk or *buttermilk (see directions below)
2 1/2 cups all-purpose flour, divided
1 tablespoon sugar
1 teaspoon baking powder
1 teaspoon salt
1/2 teaspoon baking soda
1/4 cup melted butter

Directions:
Combine starter, milk (*buttermilk) and 1 cup flour in a large, non-reactive bowl, cover and let sit out overnight in a warm place. (Be sure to replenish your starter by adding 1/2 cup all-purpose flour and 1/2 cup water; let stand in a warm place for several hours or until bubbly; then refrigerate.)
Combine the sugar, baking powder, salt, baking soda, and 1 cup flour and add to the dough, kneading lightly until well mixed.
Turn dough out onto a surface that has been sprinkled with 1/2 cup flour.
Roll or pat dough out to approx. 1/2 inch thickness. Dip biscuit cutter in melted butter and cut out biscuits. Place biscuits close together ina greased 13 x 9 x 2-inch baking pan, cover and let rest for 1/2 hour.
Brush with melted butter. Bake in preheated 375° F (190 °C) oven about 30 minutes or until golden brown. Serve warm.
* Buttermilk can be created by adding a teaspoon to tablespoon vinegar to 1 cup and milk and allowing to sit for 5-10 minutes.

Submitter: 
James Noble
Description: 
This is the way we've been making sourdough biscuits for years. It results in a lighter less dense sourdough biscuit than with traditional methods.
