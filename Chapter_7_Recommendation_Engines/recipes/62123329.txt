Title:
Grandma's chickpeas caserole Recipe
Image:
https://images.media-allrecipes.com/images/79590.png
Ingredients:
2 tablespoons of extra virgin olive oil
1/2 pound of any firm sausage (bratwurst, polish sausage or spanish chorizo)
1/2 pound of ham in small cubes (can use left over meats)
1 thick slice of bacon in cubes
1/2 cup of finelly chopped onions
1/2 cup of finelly chopped red peppers
4 cans of cooked chickpeas
1 jar of tradicional or chunky style pasta sauce
2 cups of watter

Directions:
In a large pot or casserole, if ceramic better, heat the olive oil and cook in it the sausage, ham or leftover meats and bacon until throughly cooked and starting to brown.
Add the onions and peppers and cook until soft and transparent.
Incorporate the drained chikpeas and the pasta sauce and watter.
Simmer for an hour or so until thick.

Submitter: 
Sergio Romo
Description: 
This is my updated and simplified version of my Grandma's chickpeas that were always comforting. A rustic dish that is specially good in winter and very easy to make. Serve along with a good rustic bread, olives, cheese and a good spanish wine.
