Title:
Zeytoon Parvardeh (Persian olive pomegranate dip) Recipe
Image:
https://images.media-allrecipes.com/userphotos/250x250/3343739.jpg
Ingredients:
Unseasoned green olive, 12 Oz
Pomegranate paste (melasses), 1/4 cup
Chopped garlic, 2 cloves
Olive oil, 1/3 cup
Ground walnut, 1/3 cup
Ground almond, 1/3 cup
Chopped fresh or dry mint, 1/4 cup
Chopped fresh or dry tarragon, 1/4 cup
Lemon juice, 2 Tbsp
Balsamic vinegar, 1 tsp
Thyme powder, 1/2 tsp
Salt and pepper, to taste

Directions:
Halve or coarsely chop olives. In a bowl mix well all ingredients. The mixture may not need salt if the olives are salty. Otherwise fix salt and lemon juice to a desire. Store the mixture tightly closed in a fridge at least 3 hours before serving. Can be stored in the fridge up to a week.

Submitter: 
Soheila
Description: 
This is an amazing Persian appetizer can be served as a side dish of a main course or as a dip with pita bread. The origin of this recipe is from North of Iran where there are plenty of olive gardens. You can modify the taste as you desire by adding more lemon juice or pomegranate seeds.
