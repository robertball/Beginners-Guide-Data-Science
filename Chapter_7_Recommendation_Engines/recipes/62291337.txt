Title:
Ken's Kickin' Posole Recipe
Image:
https://images.media-allrecipes.com/userphotos/250x250/357958.jpg
Ingredients:
8 oz bacon roughly diced
2 pounds pork loin cut into 1 inch cubes
1 onion diced
10 garlic cloves minced
2 fresh pasilla chiles roasted, seeded, skinned and diced
2 jalapenos cored seeded and diced
1 teaspoon Mexican oregano
2 teaspoons ground cumin
1 teaspoon New Mexico chile powder(or any mild)
1/4 teaspoon ground cloves
1/2 teaspoon kosher salt
1 small bunch cilantro chopped
8 cups chicken stock
1 14 oz can mild red enchilada sauce
2 28 oz cans hominy drained and rinsed
lime wedges, sour cream and grated cheese for garnish

Directions:
In a large heavy bottom pot over med. heat cook the bacon till starting to crisp.  Remove to paper towels and pour off all but 1 tablespoon grease from the pot.  Add the pork and cook over med/high till browned then remove to a plate.  Add the onion and garlic and sautee till soft and starting to brown about 5 mins.  Add the pasillas, jalapenos, oregano, cumin, chile powder, cloves, salt and cilantro.  Cook for another min then add the pork and bacon back to the pot along w/ the stock and enchilada sauce.  Bring to a boil then lower the heat and simmer partially covered for an hour.  Add the hominy to the pot and simmer another hour.  Serve and enjoy.

Submitter: 
Ken from CA
Description: 
This is my version of the classic dish.  Part soup, part stew but always comforting.  Garnish with lime and sour cream on top.
