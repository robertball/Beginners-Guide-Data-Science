Title:
Chicken Cacciatore Recipe
Image:
https://images.media-allrecipes.com/images/79590.png
Ingredients:
12 oz dried pasta (vermicelli or linguine works well)
1 (2 1/2 to 3 lb.) broiler/fryer chicken, cut up
-- or 2 game hens quartered.
1 stalk celery sliced
2 med onions chopped
4 tb. olive oil
1/4 c. dry white wine
1 (16 oz.) can whole tomatoes, crushed
1 (8 oz.) can tomato paste plus 1 can water
1 green pepper, cut into 1 inch pieces
1 can sliced mushrooms, drained
1 can whole ripe olives, sliced in half (about 1 cup)
3 cloves of garlic, minced
1 bay leaf
1 t. dried oregano
1 t. dried basil
1/2 t. paprika
1/2 t. dried rosemary
Salt & black pepper to season bird pieces
Grated Parmesan cheese to taste

Directions:
1. In large (electric) skillet, saute onions & celery in olive oil on
medium-high; about 10 minutes.
Remove onions/celery, leaving as much oil as possible in pan.
2. Salt & pepper poultry. Brown pieces on all sides on medium heat, about
10 minutes per side. Remove.
3. De-glaze w/ wine, scraping up the stuck bits on bottom of skillet.
Add onions/celery, tomatoes, pepper, paste, & water.
Stir in remaining ingredients.
4. Add meat. Bring to boil, stir --then reduce heat to low. Spoon sauce over
top of meat and simmer (covered) for 60 minutes. Stir & turn meat
occasionally.
Taste sauce about 10 minutes before serving; if it's too sharp, add a
pinch of sugar.
5. Cook pasta. Put drained pasta in large shallow serving dish.
Ladle about half of the sauce on pasta. Arrange poultry on top & pour
remaining sauce over meat.
Sprinkle grated Parmesan on individual servings.
