Title:
Cockaleekie (Chicken and Leeks) Soup Recipe
Image:
https://images.media-allrecipes.com/images/79590.png
Ingredients:
For Stock:
4 chicken legs or thighs or breasts or a mix.
2 large onions, quartered, skins left on
2 celery stalks, roughly chopped
2 carrots, roughly chopped
4 cloves garlic, peeled
bouquet garni, rosemary, bay leaves, thyme
8 cups water
salt and pepper
For soup:
2 onions, diced
2 large leeks, sliced lengthwise then chopped
2 carrots, diced
3 cloves garlic, minced
6 cups chicken stock
meat from chicken used for stock
1 cup pot or pearl barely
1 tsp dried rosemary
1 tsp dried thyme
1 tsp coriander
2 bay leaves
salt and pepper

Directions:
In a large stock pot, add chicken, onions, carrots, celery, garlic, and herbs.  Cover with water, bring to a boil, and skim any foam from top.  Reduce heat and simmer, covered, for 2 hours.  Remove chicken to plate and reserve.  Remove veg with a slotted spoon and discard.
Slice the leeks, then rinse in a colander to remove any grit.  Chop onions, carrots, celerey, and mince the garlic, add to the pot. Add seasonings and the reserved chicken, removed from bones and chopped.
Bring to a simmer, and add the barley.  Simmer, covered over low heat, stirring often, for about 40 minutes or until veg and barley are tender.  Check seasonings, remove bay leaf, and serve.  Enjoy!

Submitter: 
Sarah
Description: 
Adapted from the old Scottish classic, this soup is mild enough for children to love, and still full of flavor.  A healthy, feel-good soup, it makes a big batch, and freezes nicely.
