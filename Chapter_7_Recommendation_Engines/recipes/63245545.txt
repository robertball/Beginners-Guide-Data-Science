Title:
Carnitas Corn Chip Casserole Recipe
Image:
https://images.media-allrecipes.com/images/79590.png
Ingredients:
2 1/2lb bone in pork shoulder or boston butt
12oz can jalapenos in escabeche
1/2 tablespoon ground cumin
1 tablespoon Mexican oregano
1 pasilla pepper, diced
1 red pepper, diced
1 large red onion, diced
2 cloves garlic, minced
10 1/2oz can cheddar cheese soup
10oz can mild red chili sauce
1/2 cup chicken broth
10 1/2oz bag corn chips
1 cup shredded queso cheese

Directions:
To make carnitas, rub pork shoulder with cumin & oregano. Place in slow cooker, and pour jalapenos in excabeche over the top. Cover & cook on low for 8 hours.
Remove pork from slow cooker, and shred into small pieces, removing any fat. Place in large mixing bowl, and pour 1/4 cup of pork broth from the slow cooker over the top.
Preheat oven to 325 degrees.
In saute pan, cook peppers, onions and garlic until soft, about 5-7 minutes. Place vegetable mixture into bowl with carnitas.
In separate bowl mix cheese soup, red chili sauce and chicken broth. Pour liquid mixture into bowl with carnitas.
Add bag of corn chips to bowl, and mix thoroughly making sure all pieces are covered with liquid. Transfer mixture into casserole dish and cover.
Place in pre-heated oven and cook for 45 minutes. During the last 15 minutes add the queso cheese to the top and cook uncovered.

Description: 
I love Mexican casserole recipes. I developed my version with left over carnitas and corn chips we had after a barbeque. This casserole is very spicy, just they way my famlily likes it!
