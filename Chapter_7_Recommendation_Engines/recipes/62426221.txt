Title:
Frogmore Stew (a/k/a Lowcountry Boil) Recipe
Image:
https://images.media-allrecipes.com/images/79590.png
Ingredients:
4 quarts water
1 large onion, halved and sliced
2 cloves garlic, crushed
1 pinch coarse salt
1/4 cup Old Bay Seasoning (or 1 bag of crab boil)
1 1/2 pounds red potatoes, scrubbed and cubed
3 ears corn, husk and silk removed (or 6 small ears)
3 lbs. unpeeled, large fresh shrimp*
2 pckg. Hillshire Farms smoked beef sausage, sliced into chunks
Optional Dipping Sauces:
Cocktail sauce:   1/2 c. ketchup and 2 T. prepared horseradish, or to taste
Tabasco dip: 1/2 cup butter, melted 1 dash hot pepper sauce (e.g. Tabascoâ„¢), or to taste

Directions:
1. Bring the water to a boil in a large pot.
2. Add onion, garlic, salt and Old Bay seasoning. Reduce heat to medium-low, cover, and simmer for 10 minutes.
3.  Add the potatoes, and return to a boil.
4.  Cut corn in half (if using large ears), add corn to pot and return to boil.
5.  When potatoes are tender with a fork, add
smoked sausage, return to boil and cook for 20 minutes.
6.  Stir in shrimp and cook until shrimp turn pink.
Use slotted spoon to serve, or drain off liquid before serving. It may be reserved for other uses such as soup stock.
Serve optional sauces with the seafood and vegetables for dipping.

Submitter: 
Babs Thomas Crow
Description: 
This seafood stew is a staple of our diet here in Beaufort. When we welcome out of town guests, I always cook this stew. And feeds ALOT of people!
