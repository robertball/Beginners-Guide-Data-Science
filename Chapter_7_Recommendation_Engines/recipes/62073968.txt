Title:
Pork Normandy Recipe
Image:
https://images.media-allrecipes.com/images/79590.png
Ingredients:
1 1/2 lbs pork tenderloin
1 medium onion sliced thin
1 dessert apple, peeled, cored, and sliced
1 tbsp butter
1 tbsp flour
5 oz chicken stock
4 oz hard apple cider
2 tbsp heavy whipping cream

Directions:
Ore-heat over to 350 degrees.
Brown pork tenderloins in butter, remove from pan.  Brown the onion for 2-3 minutes, add apple and cook til golden brown.  Stir in flour and cook for 30 seconds. Mix stock and apple cider together and add to pan.  Bring to a boil.
Put tenderloins back in the pan and season to taste.  Cover and bake 45 minutes until tender.  Remove the meat and cut into slanting 1 1/2 inch slices.
Place the sauce, along with the cooked onions and apples into a blender or food processor and process until smooth.  Return the sauce to the pan, reheat, then stir in 2 tbls heavy whipping cream, heat until warm, adjust seasoning, then serve over the meat.

Submitter: 
Tiffany Curtis
Description: 
I got this recipe from my mother who learned to make it when we lived in England.  It's a family favorite and great for company.
