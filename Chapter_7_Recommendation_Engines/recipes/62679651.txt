Title:
Country-Ham Potato Soup Recipe
Image:
https://images.media-allrecipes.com/images/79590.png
Ingredients:
1/4 cup butter;
1 1/2 cups chopped onion;
1 tablespoon minced garlic;
1 cup chopped country ham;
2 tablespoons all-purpose flour;
3 cups chicken broth;
4 cups unpeeled, chopped new potatoes;
1 1/2 cups heavy whipping cream;
2 teaspoons chopped fresh thyme;
1/2 teaspoon crushed red pepper;
1/2 teaspoon ground black pepper;
1 cup shredded sharp Cheddar cheese;
1/2 cup sour cream;
Garnish: chopped country ham

Directions:
1. In a large Dutch oven, melt butter over medium-high heat.
2. Add onion, garlic, and ham; cook until onion is soft and ham begins to brown, approximately 4 minutes.
3. Add flour, and stir until well blended, approximately 1 minute.
4. Stir in broth; cook until thickened and bubbly.
5. Add potatoes; bring to a simmer. Reduce heat to medium-low, cover, and cook until potatoes are tender, approximately 15 minutes.
6. With a potato masher, mash potatoes until only a few chunks remain.
7. Stir in cream, thyme, red pepper, and black pepper; cook for 2 minutes.
8. Stir in cheese and sour cream until well blended.
9. Garnish with chopped country ham, if desired.

Submitter: 
Christy Jayne
Description: 
This is from a 'Taste of the South' Special Collectors 'South's Best Comfort Food' issue that my mom picked up. She requested I make this recipe for her birthday and she loved it so much, it's become a regular.
