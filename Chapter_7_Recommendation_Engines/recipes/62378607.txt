Title:
Ida Belle's Mountain Pudding Recipe
Image:
https://images.media-allrecipes.com/userphotos/250x250/416631.jpg
Ingredients:
12 oz fresh berries or 1 large can berry pie filling
1/8 cup sugar(if using fresh berries)
1 cup water(if using fresh berries)
2-3 tablespoons corn starch(if using fresh berries)
1 cup sugar
1 cup milk
1 cup self-rising flour
2 tablespoons butter or margerine

Directions:
Preheat oven to 400 degrees.
Melt butter into square 9"x9" pan or round baking dish.
If using fresh berries, combine water, 1/8 cup sugar, and berries into a sauce pan.  Heat on medium until saucy.  Turn off heat. Add 2 tablespoons corn starch to make into pie filling consistency.  If not thick enough, add one more tablespoon corn starch.
Pour into pan.
If using pie filling, open can and pour filling over butter in pan.
Combine flour, sugar, and milk in a bowl into a thin batter.  Wisk until smooth.  Pour over berries in pan.
Bake for 30 minutes or until batter is solid and golden brown, depending on oven. Remove and let cool 15-20 minutes before serving.

Submitter: 
Sara Black
Description: 
This is a cobbler that my grandmother who was raised in East Kentucky makes. It can be made with fresh berries or pie filling of cherries, strawberries, blueberries, or blackberries and still be delicious!
