Title:
Chicken and Shrimp Lo Mein by whodunitrdr Recipe
Image:
https://images.media-allrecipes.com/userphotos/250x250/286146.jpg
Ingredients:
2 skinless boneless chicken breasts; thinly sliced
1/2 teaspoon cornstarch
3 tablespoons soy sauce; low sodium - divided
1 teaspoon sesame oil - divided
1/2 teaspoon white pepper - divided
1/2 pound cooked large shrimp; sliced lengthwise
1 tablespoon oil
3/4 cup onion; thinly sliced
1/2 cup celery; thinly sliced
1/2 cup carrot; coarsely grated
1/2 teaspoon garlic; minced
1/2 teaspoon fresh ginger; minced
1 cup fresh mushrooms; sliced
1/2 cup red bell pepper; julienned
1 cup snow peas; sliced lengthwise
1 cup fresh bean sprouts
1/2 can bamboo shoots; julienned
1 cup chinese cabbage; sliced
1/2 pound spaghetti; cooked
3/4 cup chicken broth
3 tablespoons oyster sauce
1 teaspoon sweet chili sauce, or more to taste
1 1/2 tablespoons dry sherry
1 1/2 tablespoons cornstarch
1 1/2 tablespoons cold water
1/2 cup green onions; sliced for garnish

Directions:
Have all vegetables sliced and ready to cook before starting to cook. Combine chicken and 1/2 teas. each cornstarch and soy sauce, 1/4 teas. each sesame oil and white pepper, mix and let marinate for 20 minutes. Combine shrimp and 1/2 teas. soy sauce and 1/4 teas. each sesame oil and white pepper, mix and let marinate for 20 minutes. Combine chicken broth, oyster sauce, chili sauce and remaining soy sauce and sesame oil, set aside. Combine remaining cornstarch and water, set aside. Heat wok over high heat, add a little oil and stir fry chicken for a few minutes until cooked through, remove from wok and place in serving bowl. Add a little more oil and stirfry onion, celery, carrot, garlic and ginger for 2 minutes or until crisp tender. Add to chicken in bowl. Add a bit more oil and stir fry mushrooms for 1 minute, then add bell pepper and snow peas, cook for one minute. Add bean sprouts, bamboo shoots, mix in. Add shrimp, noodles and cabbage. Add chicken broth mixture and cover wok. Let steam for 2-3 minutes or until shrimp and noodles are hot and cabbage starts to wilt. Return mixture from serving bowl to the wok, combine and scoop towards sides of wok, leaving liquid in the middle bottom of the wok. Add sherry to liquid in the wok, bring to a boil. Add cornstarch slurry, cook and stir liquid for a minute or until thickened and clear. Toss to coat everything in the wok with the sauce. Remove back to serving bowl and garnish with green onions.
