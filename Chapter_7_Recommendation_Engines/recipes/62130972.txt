Title:
Fiddlehead Chicken Stirfry Recipe
Image:
https://images.media-allrecipes.com/images/79590.png
Ingredients:
1/4 to 1/2 of a 16oz. package of angel hair pasta
2 chicken breasts, cut into strips
1 cup fiddleheads, fresh
1/4 cup each of yellow, orange, green, and red bell peppers
1 small head of broccoli, chopped
1 celery stalk, thinly sliced
2 8oz. cans of whole water chestnuts
1/4 cup sweetened sake
1/2 cup soy sauce
splash lime juice
pinch each of crushed red pepper, Asian 5-spice mix, garam masala, ginger, and white pepper
1 Tbsp minced garlic
1 Tbsp hot chile sesame oil

Directions:
In a small mixing bowl, combine sake, soy sauce, and spices.
Boil about 1 1/2 cups of water in a small pan. When boiling, add fiddleheads and allow to boil for about 5 minutes. Strain, rinse, and set aside.
In a wok or frying pan, heat oil and lightly brown the garlic.
Add chicken and all of the "marinade". When chicken is nearly cooked, add vegetables.
In a medium-sized pot, heat about 2 cups of water to a boil. Add a pinch of sea salt as well as soy sauce and the spices listed above if desired.
When boiling, add angel hair pasta and cook until al dente.
Strain pasta and add to ingredients in the wok/fry pan.
Stir and serve with a sprinkling of sesame seeds for garnish.

Submitter:

Description: 
For a brief 1-2 week period in new england, fiddlehead ferns are ready for harvest. If you're lucky enough to find them, they're a great addition to any meal calling for broccoli or asparagus.
