Title:
Western Style Flat Iron Steaks Recipe
Image:
https://images.media-allrecipes.com/images/79590.png
Ingredients:
2 flat iron steaks (1 pound each)
1 teaspoon powdered beef bouillon
2 tablespoons chili powder
1 tablespoon smoked paprika
1 tablespoon onion powder
1 teaspoon ground cumin
1/2 teaspoon dried cilantro
1/4 teaspoon black pepper
Pinch of dried rubbed sage
1/4 cup finely chopped onion
5 tablespoons good quality olive oil
3 tablespoons minced fresh garlic (jar type)
1 tablespoon balsamic vinegar
1 tablespoon lime juice
1 tablespoon molasses

Directions:
Rub both sides of room temperature steaks with beef bouillon.
In shallow baking dish, add remaining ingredients and combine.
Add steaks in marinade being sure to coat both sides well.
Cover dish and let stand at room temperature for about 15 minutes.
Meanwhile, preheat grill to medium high heat.
Remove steak from marinade reserving marinade for basting.
Place steaks on grill and cook to desired doneness (about 6 to 7 minutes per side) basting often with marinade.
Remove from heat and let meat stand for 3 minutes before slicing.

Submitter: 
Alta Nesbitt Rees Sieker
Description: 
Flat Iron steaks are a tender cut of meat from the top blade or shoulder of the chuck portion. Usually in a triangle shape resembling an iron, hence the name.
I use a variety of common spices to create my zesty rub/marinade.
