Title:
Traditional German Apple Cake Recipe
Image:
https://images.media-allrecipes.com/images/79590.png
Ingredients:
4 c. flour
4 tsp. baking powder
3/4 c. sugar
2 pkg. vanilla sugar (available at Cost Plus, or other fine food stores)
dash salt
2 eggs
1 1/2 Tbl. milk
2 sticks of butter
Filling:
3 lbs. tart apples
3/8 c. sugar
1 1/2 tsp. cinnamon
3 tbl. raisins, soaked in 2 tbl. rum

Directions:
In large bowl mix flour and baking powder.  Add sugar, vanilla sugar, and salt.  Add butter by cutting small "flecks" into bowl.  In small bowl combine eggs and milk.  Add to flour mixture.  Remove from bowl and knead by hand to a smooth dough.  Dough should not stick.  Chill 30 min.  Meanwhile, combine filling ingredients and set aside.  Remove dough from refrigerator and roll out 3/4 of it to fit the bottoms and sides of an 11 1/2" springform pan.  Work up the side 2".  Prick w/fork several times.  Bake at 375 degrees 10-15 min.  Add apple filling.  Roll out remaining dough and place on top, sealing with the dough underneath.  Combine 1 egg and 2 tbl. milk; brush over crust.  Prick the top of the crust and sprinkle with sugar.  Bake at 375 degrees for 30 min.; cover w/foil and bake an additional 20-30 min.  Cool completely on wire rack.  Serve with whipped cream.

Submitter: 
MISS ALIX
Description: 
This is an authentic German kuchen, not like American cakes.  It's made in a springform pan, with a top and bottom crust, and a filling of apples and raisins.  And of course, topped with fresh whipped cream!
