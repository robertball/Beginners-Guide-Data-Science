Title:
Wanton Wonton Soup Recipe
Image:
https://images.media-allrecipes.com/userphotos/250x250/567339.jpg
Ingredients:
2 Tbsp. oil ( I used canola)
1 Tbsp. of minced garlic
1 1/2 Tbsp. minced ginger
1/4 C finely chopped green onions (both white and green stems)
2 qts. chicken broth
4-5 leaves of thinly sliced bok choy
7-8 medium sized mushrooms, thinly sliced
1 8oz can sliced bamboo shoots
For the wontons:
1 pkg. wontons (find in your produce dept.)
3/4 pound ground pork
1 egg
1 Tbsp shoyu soy sauce
1 Tbsp rice wine vinegar
2 tsps. sesame oil
generous dash of red pepper flakes (or to taste)
1 Tbsp. minced garlic
1 Tbsp. minced ginger
2 Tbsp. minced green onion

Directions:
Heat oil in a stock pot. Add garlic, onion, and ginger cook stirring frequently til fragrant. (1-2 minutes). Add chicken broth and bring to a hard simmer. Meanwhile, assemble the wontons. Combine the pork with the egg, soy sauce, vinegar, sesame oil, red pepper, onions, garlic and ginger. Mix well. Place approx 1 tsp of filling into the center of the wonton wrapper. With wet fingers, wet 2 sides of the wonton and fold into a triangle. Squeeze out any air bubbles. Cross the pointed end of the long side to the middle. Wet fingers to make it adhere. Set aside. Add the bok choy, mushrooms, and sliced bamboo shoots to the broth. Cook for about 5-7 minutes. Add prepared wontons to the simmering broth. Once they float, they are done. Approx 5 minutes. Top with a garnish of chopped green onion and sprinkle of sesame seeds.
