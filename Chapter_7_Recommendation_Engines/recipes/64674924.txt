Title:
On Luck's Kung Pao Chicken Recipe
Image:
https://images.media-allrecipes.com/userphotos/250x250/4621687.jpg
Ingredients:
3 Chicken Thighs, cut into 1/2-inch chucks
1 cup chicken broath
5 chinese chiles (Optional)
1 tablespoon soy sauce
1 tablespoon rice vingar
1 teaspoon sugar
½ teaspoon white pepper
½ teaspoon crushed red pepper
1 teaspoon szechuan bean paste (Optional)
1 green or red pepper, cut into 1/2-inch dice
½ cup carrot, diced
½ cup celery, diced
½ (4 ounce can) drained sliced bamboo shoots
½ (4 ounce can) drained sliced water chestnuts
2 cloves garlic, minced
1 tablespoon ginger root, minced
2 tablespoon sesame oil
1 scallion, diced (Optional)
¼ cup peanuts (Optional)

Directions:
Heat sesame oil in a large wok or saute pan. Stir garlic, ginger root, and crushed red pepper for a couple minutes, then add chicken and cook chicken until it is tender.
Add ½ chicken broth to stir fry. Stir in celery and carrot. Cook and stir until vegetables begin to wilt.
Mix in all other ingredients except peanuts and scallions. Cook another 5 minutes and thicken with cornstarch rue.
Serve with scallion and peanuts spread on top and white rice on the side.

Submitter: 
James
Description: 
While in college I worked at a restaurant that typically made their spicy chicken dish this way. I have been know to substitute Thai Red Curry for the Szechuan Bean Paste.
